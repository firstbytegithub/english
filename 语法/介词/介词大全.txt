一、介词的定义
　　介词（preposition)又称前置词，是十分活跃的一个词类，可以与名词或名词性词语结合成介词短语，说明地点、方向、时间、原因、方法等，在句中主要起状语和定语的作用，也可以充当表语和补语，还可以充当主语和介词宾语。例如：
He stayed in his seat.
An old farmer in the back row looked up towards the ceiling.
The students are interested in the subject.
上述第一句里的介词in 和is seat所构成的介词短语说明动词stayed的位置，第二句里的介词短语in the back row 限定名词an old farmer，第三句里的介词短语in the subject 表示are interested的范畴。
二、介词的类型
　　介词可分为简单介词和复合介词两类：
（一）简单介词（Simple Prepositions）
　　凡是由一个单词组成的介词都称为简单介词，例如：in, on, across, underneath等等。简单介词约有80个，其中包括一些由动词或形容词转化而成的介词，例如：except, excepting, concerning, considering, following, including, granted, pending, less, like, near, save, unlike, worth等。
（二）复合介词（Complex Prepositions）
　　凡是由两个或两个以上单词组成的介词都称为复合介词。根据它们的结构，复合介词可以细分为下列三种：
A)副词+介词:along with, apart from, as for, as to, out of, together with, up to等。 
B)动词（形容词、连词等）+介词：except for, owing to, due to, but for, because of等。 
C)介词1+名词+介词2：by means of, in addition to, in comparison with, on account of, with regard to等等。
　
三、介词的宾语
　　介词短语是由介词与介词宾语组成的。介词宾语通常是名词或代词（宾格）；此外，还可以是副词、形容词、短语（包括介词短语、动名词短语、不定式短语）、复合结构或名词性从句。
（一）名词或代词做介词宾语
（二）副词做介词宾语
（三）形容词做介词宾语
（四）短语做介词宾语
（一）名词或代词做介词宾语
例如：
We saw your friend at the meeting last night.
I supposed that the old lady in the flat above ours was moving the furniture about.
While we are waiting for her, let's have some coffee.
Note:
A）介词之后的代词一般都要用宾格，但有时也用主格。例如，介词but（“除……以外”）之后的代词如果在意思上与句子的主语平行，则倾向于用主格，这时的but也可以看成是连词。
例如：
All but he had fled (= All had fled, but he had not fled.)
I saw nothing but him.
Ｂ）在口语中，如果动词或介词之后接两个代词作宾语，那么第二个代词常用主格。
例如：
He won't let my wife and I into the house.
All debts are cleared between you and I.
Ｃ）as, than, but, save 等连词有“准介词”之称，他们之后可接主格代词，也可接宾格代词。接主格代词时算是连词，接宾格代词时算是介词。例如：
One day you will be as old as I /me.
You are a better player than he/him.
（二）副词做介词宾语
例如：
I met him a year ago, since then I haven't seen anything of him.
Where does he come from?
You should be out instead of in on such a fine day.
Note:
　　当副词作介词宾语时，其作用相当于一个名词或代词。这类介词短语通常用来表示时间或地点。
例如：
until now（到现在），since then（从那时以来），in there（在那里面），from here to there（从这里到那里），from abroad（从国外）等。
（三）形容词做介词宾语
　形容词作介词宾语主要见于某些固定词组，例如at worst（在最坏的情况下），of late（近来），of old（从前），for good（永远），for long（长久），in general（一般说来），in vain（徒然），on high（在高空），at first（最初），at least（最少)等。这些形容词本来可能接一个名词，如for long在意思上相当于for a long time,，但for long已经形成了固定的词组，其中的形容词实际上已经名词化了。
例如：
He is a fool at (the) best, but at (the) worst he's a criminal.
We thought she'd come for a visit, but it seems she's staying for good.
The drowning man shouted in vain, nobody heard him.
（四）短语做介词宾语
Ａ）介词短语做介词宾语
　　介词短语象副词一样，也可以用作介词宾语，但也有人把from over his glasses 里的from over看成是一个复合介词，其中的over表示方位，from表示运动的出发点。例如：
He was looking at me from over his glasses.
They stayed here till after midnight.
You can find that sort of thing nowhere but in England.
Ｂ）动名词短语做介词宾语
例如：
He was trying to make a little money by selling newspaper.
I am not in a position of complying with your request.
Ｃ）不定式短语做介词宾语
　不定式短语一般不用作介词宾语，仅出现于少数几个介词之后，介词之后的不定式符号to有时可以省略。
例如：
I had no choice but to accept the offer.
She does nothing but complain.
Their housemaid does everything except (to) wash the car.
Ｄ）复合结构做介词宾语
例如：
He was annoyed at you saying that.
You may depend on me to be there early.
He lay on his bed with the bedroom door shut.
Note:
　　在口语中，介词之后的动名词的逻辑主语通常用名词或代词的通格，在书面语中则用名词或代词的所有格。例如上述的第一个句子在书面语中应改成：
He was annoyed at your saying that.
Ｅ） 名词性从句做介词宾语
例如：
Ｉam not interested in whether you like the plan or not.
You have no idea as to how badly he writes.
He is thankful for what I have done for him.
四、介词短语的句法功能
　　介词短语在句中主要用作状语和定语，此外，也可以用作表语和补语，偶尔还用作主语或介词宾语。
（一）介词短语作状语
（二）介词短语作定语
（三）介词短语作表语
（四）介词短语作补语
（五）介词短语作主语、介词宾语
（一）介词短语作状语
Ａ）介词短语作状语，用来修饰动词。
例如：
We live quite near my office（地点）
Who knows what will happen in the future? （时间）
The sewing machine is worked by foot（方式）
We're waiting for them to arrive for lunch（目的）
He worked himself to death（结果）
He is so useful to me that I can't do without him（条件）
With all your faults, I still like you（让步）
He lay on his bed with the sunlight falling on his face（伴随状语）
I don't believe in dream（范畴）
What country do you come from?（来源）
Everything depends on whether he is punctual（根据）
The men who govern the country are chosen by the people（行为者）
B)介词短语作状语，用来修饰形容词
例如：
He was not conscious of my presence in the room.
I am not interested in doing business with that firm.
They are proud of their clever children.
Ｃ）介词短语作状语，修饰副词（比较少用）
例如：
John is now somewhere on the high seas, and we wish him a pleasant holiday.
Do you live here in Middle Town, Mr John?
Ｄ）介词短语作状语，修饰整个句子
例如：
On the whole, Jack has been a good friend.
（二）介词短语作定语
例如：
The weather during the last few days has been perfect.
The hands of the clock pointed to half past six.
He has had a quarrel with his father and left home.
（三）介词短语作表语
例如：
Two soldiers are always on guard outside the president's house.
His wife is a member of many committees and is always on the go.
Short dresses have gone out of fashion.
（四）介词短语作补语
例如：
The soldiers suddenly found themselves under fire on all sides.
These things should be kept in repair.
We were left with all the refugees on our hands.
（五）介词短语作主语、介词宾语
例如：
Between six and seven will suit me. （主语）
Before breakfast is when I do my best work. （主语）
He went nowhere except to school. （介词宾语）
五、介词宾语的转位
　　介词宾语通常紧接在介词之后，但有时因结构、修辞等的需要，介词宾语反而位于介词之前。在口语中，特殊疑问句中的疑问词，限定性定语从句中的关系词以及不定式所带的关系代词或连接代词，通常与介词分离，使介词位于句末。
Ａ特殊疑问句中介词宾语的转位
例如：
Which class are you in?
What am I sitting on?
Ｂ限定性定语从句中介词宾语的转位（介词与关系词分离时，这个关系词在口语中通常省略）。
例如：
Isn't that the man (whom) you came with?
The little school (which) we used to go to has disappeared and now there's a vast comprehensive school in its place.
Ｃ不定式中介词宾语的转位
例如：
Give me a knife to cut the bread with (=Give me a knife with which to cut the bread).
I am not sure which restaurant to eat at (= I am not sure at which restaurant to eat).
Note:
1在口语中通常用who代替whom，用whom反而不自然。但是与介词连用时，只能用whom，不能用who。
Who are you speaking to?
To whom are you speaking?
你在跟谁讲话？
2带介词的不定式短语相当于一个带介词的定语从句。例如“Give me a knife with which to cut the bread” 相当于“Give me a knife with which I can cut the bread”但在口语中通常将介词移至句末。这时关系代词必须略去（即：Give me a knife to cut the bread with）
3如果不定式短语前面有一个连接代词，介词通常移到句末。这种不定式短语相当于一个名词从句，连接代词不能省略。虽然可以说“I don't know to whom to give it”，但口语中通常说“I don't know who to give it to”
在下列情况下，不论是否用于口语，介词都必须移至句末：
Ａ）关系代词that引导定语从句时：
There are several questions that I must talk to you about.
Ｂ）连接代词what引导名词从句时：
This is what I am looking for.
Ｃ）在被动语态的结构中：
When we first started such success was never dreamed of.
Note:
1在被动语态结构中，句子的主语常常是介词的逻辑宾语；在某种非被动语态结构中，有时句子的主语也是介词的逻辑宾语。例如：
He is impossible to deal with. (=It is impossible to deal with him)
2在下句中，介词与介词宾语形成相对固定的组合，因此介词与介词宾语不能分离。
I don't know by what train he will go.
3有时为了强调介词宾语而将介词宾语提前到句首。
Two things I can not live without --truth and love.
　
六、时间介词的省略
（一）表示时间的短语，如果冠有last, next, this, that以及every, some等词，它们的时间介词几乎总是省去。
例如：
It rained last night.
The meeting will be held next January.
Have anybody thought of a good way to spend our vacation this summer?
I was very busy that day.
The doctor came every other day.
Some day I should go there.
Note:
1下列各句中的时间短语均带介词：
He ought to have been here by this time.
I send you my best wishes on this happy occasion.
We have a meeting on every other day.
2在下列词序倒装的时间短语里，英国英语通常要求带有介词：
My vacation starts (on) Friday next (=starts next Friday).
We met (in) June last (=met last June).
（二）Today, tomorrow, yesterday等词分别含有this, next, last的意思，它们不带介词，直接用作时间副词。
She wrote her grandmother a nice long letter yesterday.
May we meet you at the hotel tomorrow morning at ten?
（三）当我们间接地提到过去或将来某个时间时，这个时间短语前面的介词在口语中通常省略。例如：(on) the day after tomorrow（前天），(on) the day after his death（他死后的第二天）。
We arrived in New York (in) the December before last.
Would you like to drive around the city with me (on) the day after tomorrow?
They are going to get married tomorrow week.
She died (on) the following day.
（四）在美国口语中，周日之前的介词on通常省略，在英国口语中偶尔也有省略现象。
I usually leave here Friday night and come back Saturday or Sunday.
（五）介词for用来表示行为或状态延续的时间长短时，经常可以省略，但在正式文体里最好不省略，而当时间长度短语前有all时，for必须省去。
The performance lasted (for) three hours.
We shall stay (for) two weeks.
He has been working all (the)day.
Note:
　　表示时间延续长度的for用于句首或用于否定词之后时，一般不能省略。
例如：
For a year we waited to hear what had happened to him.
The train will not arrive for two hours.
　
七、介词短语的强调词
　　某些副词可以用来修饰介词短语，起强调作用。
例如：
I just at that moment thought of it.
He must be well past forty.
He has travelled all over the world.
Go straight along the road and you'll find number 86 right at the end.
Tell him to come as soon after nine as possible.
　
八、带介词的动词
　　有些动词能跟介词组成比较固定的短语，它们的作用相当于一个及物动词，在它们之后需要接一个宾语。在意义上这个宾语可以看成是这个“及物动词”的宾语，但在语法上，这个宾语应是介词宾语。
例如：
His friends refused to deal with (associate with) him after his release from prison.
Mr Robinson spends most of his time running after his secretary, many people think he will marry her.
She came at (attacked) me like a tiger.
Note:
1动词之后带介词时，大多数介词并不影响动词的意思。
2英语中有大量的动词能与副词组成固定的短语动词，这其中的副词大部分与介词同形。试比较下列句子：
Peter turned on the boastful youth and stuck him.
John turned on the got-water tap.
上述句子都包含这样的结构：turn+on+宾语，但第一句中的on是介词，说明the boastful youth与动词turn之间的敌对关系，因此on之后接的是介词宾语；而第二句中的on却是副词，它与动词构成一个短语动词，相当于汉语的“开（水龙头、灯等）”，因此on之后接的是动词宾语。同样，下列第一句中的upon是介词，第二句中的off是副词：
The president called upon the nation to economise as much as possible.
The young couple decided to call off the wedding.
3动词与副词结合可以形成不及物的短语动词，也可以形成及物的短语动词，及物短语动词中的副词可以与动词分离，移到宾语之后（而动词之后接介词时，介词则不能移到介词宾语之后。）
Mrs. Brown cut up the cake (=cut the cake up) and gave each of us a piece.
4如果短语动词的宾语是人称代词时，副词通常移到人称代词之后。例如：
He knew he was being followed but could not shake them (his pursuers) off.
I forgot to wind it (the clock) up.
　
九、介词性副词
　　某些词既可以用作介词，又可以用作副词。
例如：
They climbed up the steep cliff.
They climbed up and soon reached the steep cliff.
The factory was by the river.
The accident occurred when nobody was by.
从以上例句可以看出，up,by等可以用作介词，后接介词宾语。这些介词说明了介词宾语和句中另一个词之间的某种关系。在从上下文可以理解的情况下，介词宾语常可以不表示出来，因此，up,by等又起副词作用。这类副词可以称为介词性副词。
Note:
1常见的介词性副词有：about, above, after, along, around, before, below, between, by, down, in, of, on, over, past, since, through, under, up, without等。
2有些介词不能转为介词性副词，如at,beside,for,from,into,of ,till,to,until,with等；out of也永远是介词，转为副词时只能用out.
The man jumped out of the window.
The man ran to the window and jumped out.
　
十、介词的释义和用法
aboard
　　aboard原来是指“上船”、“在船上”，后又延伸表示“上车”、“在车上”、“上飞机”、“在飞机上”等，例如：
We are aboard the ship.
He got aboard the train.
She came aboard a plane.
He went aboard a horse.
Note:
aboard也可用作副词，如下例所示：
He came aboard of the ship.
about
　　about表示“在……各处”、“向……各处”、“在……周围”、“在……附近”、“在……身边”等空间位置；又引申表示“大约”、“即将”、“关于”、“从事于”等意思。
A“向……各处”、“在……各处”
例如：
We wandered about the city on our first evening there.
The children had left their toys lying about the room.
The cowslips dotted about the field.
Note:
　　在英国，口语中常用about表示“向……各处”、“在……各处”。在美国口语中常用，around代替about表示这个意思，about只用于正式文体。
B“在……周围”
例如：
She has a gold chain about her neck.
We could see the high wall about the prison.
Note:
　　about的这个意思只用于正式文体，一般口语中应说round或around。
C“在……附近”
例如：
The streets about the castle are full of places of historic interest.
The bomb fell about here.
Note:
1.around常用来代替about表示这个意思，尤其是在美国英语里。
2.about有时不指“在……附近”，而含有“在……里头”的意思（somewhere within a given place or area)。
Mrs Jones does not appear to be about the house, so I presume she has gone out.
D“在……身边”、“在……身上”
例如：
Do you happen to have his letter about you?
There is a strange smell about him.
Note:
　　介词on,with等也可以表示“在……身上”、“在……身边”的意思。
E“大约”、“近于”、“左右”
例如：
I shall be home somewhere about lunch-time.
My watch has been gaining about ten minutes a day for some time.
His house is about ten miles from London.
He is about my statue.
Note:
1介词about可以用于表示“时刻、数量、大小”等的“大约”数字或程度；表示“时刻”时，也可以说at about,但这里的about已转化为副词。
Ｉ woke (at) about seven o'clock.
2在其他情况下（不是表示“时刻”时），about之前的at是不能省略的（这里的about是副词）。
They sold at about ＄3 a share.
3.about之前可以根据表达的需要加用其他介词。
He has been in the office for about an hour.
I'll go for a fitting in about a week's time.
F“正要”、“即将”
例如：
They are about to be married (=They are going to be married).
I feel that something terrible is about to happen.
Note:
　　“be aboutto do sth” 表示即将发生的行为，因此不需要再用表示将来时间的状语。其主语可以是有生命的，也可以是无生命的，但用“on / upon the point of doing sth”时，主语通常是有生命的。
We were on the point of telephoning you when your telegram arrived.
G“关于”、“对于”
例如：
I hadn't heard about that.
Roger is rather fussy about food.
Be careful about the pronunciation of "quiet" and "quite"
The story is about giants.
Note:
1在某些动词之后，有时可以用of代替 about表示“关于”的意思，例如speak of, tell of, dream of等。这些短语相当于speak about, tell about, dream about。但of通常用于比较正式的文体，因此平常比较少用。
The soldier often dreams about / of home.
2有些及物动词与about /of连用时转化为不及物动词，行为的直接对象变成了间接对象，语义也随之发生变化。
I don't know Mr Smith but I know about / of him.
我不认识史密斯先生，但我听说过这个人。
I read the book.
我曾读过这本书。
I read about / of the book.
我曾读过关于这本书的介绍。
3动词think后面接about或接of，意思不一样。试比较：
I've been thinking about (=considering) the question for a long time.
这个问题我已经考虑很久了。
Such a thing is not even to be thought of.
(=to be brought to mind)
这种事情不可思议。
4当about所构成的短语修饰名词时，about通常不能改用of或concerning。
Have you any books about stamp-collecting?
5在口语中，“What/How about …?”这类句子用于表示“提议”或“提问”。
例如：
What about going there now?
How about (having) a drink?
What/How about it? (=What shall we do? 或Tell me more precisely what you mean.)
H“就……的特征而言”
例如：
It's a funny thing about holidays; no matter how long a holiday we have, I always feel I want a few days more.
Note:
　　在口语中常说“He has something about him”，意思是”他很能干（很有个性）“（having energy, force of character, etc），反之就“He has nothing about him”　
I“从事于……”
例如：
What are you about (=at)?
I do not like to be interrupted when I am about an important piece of work.
Note:
1.set about与to about都含有“着手做……”的意思。
例如：
I must set about my packing.
He went about the job half-heartedly.
2. see about 含有“处理”、“照料”的意思。
例如：
He promised to see about the matter.
　
above
　　above的原意是“在……上面”，表示地理位置，后又引申表示（地位）“在……之上”，（水平、数量、质量、价值等）“超过……”，此外，还含有（因为太好，以致）“不可能……”、（行为、意思）“不屑于……”、“不至于……”等意思。
A“在……上面”（地理空间位置）
例如：
The shelf should be six feet above the level of the floor.
The castle towers above all the land.
Note:
1.above只表示一物处于比另一物更高的位置（meaning simply that one object is on a higher level than the other),不一定在正上方，它与below相对应。
over则通常表示两个物体之间垂直的相对位置（tending to indicate a direct vertical relationship),与under相对应。over可以用来代替above，above却不能用来代替over。
例如：
The falcon sailed above the clouds.
A bird hovered over /above us.
2.over与表示运动的记号连用时，有时含有“越过”的意思，相当于across，而above却没有这个意思，它只表示运动的位置是“在……上面”。试比较：
We flewover /across the Sahara.
We flew above the clouds.
3.on(upon)也表示“在……上面”，但仅用于两个物体相互接触的情况下。如果两个物体没有接触，那就应该用over或above。试比较：
In his room there is a clock over the fireplace.
There is a cat on the roof.
4.above有时还引申表示“在……上游”的意思。
例如：
The waterfall is above the bridge.
B“在……之上”（位置）
例如：
A navy-captain ranks above an army-captain.
He is above me in rank.
Note:
　above与over都可以表示地位、职位“高于……”；below与under都可以表示地位、职位“低于……”；但over与under还表示“统辖”、“支配”关系，above与below则只一般地说明地位、职位的高低。因此，表示“统辖”、“支配”时，只能用over或under。
C“越过……”、“超出……”（能力、水平）
例如：
As a scholar, he is far above me.
I must confess it is above my comprehension.
D“在……之上”（数量）
例如：
Above two hundred people attended the meeting.
The temperature was only a few degrees above the freezing-point.
Note:
　　表示数量“在 …以上”时，above与over可以互相替用。
Over / Above five hundred people were present at the ceremony.
E“胜于……”、“重于……”（质量、价值）
例如：
Health is above wealth.
We should value honour above life.
F“无可……”、“不胜……”（因为太好、太伟大而）
例如：
The missing boy's uncle was above suspicion.
His behaviour is above praise.
Note:
　　这里的above经常可以与beyond互相替用。
G“不屑于……”、“不至于……”（行为、思想）
例如：
William is above meanness (=He wouldn't ever be mean).
All these men are honest and above bribery.
Note:
　　“not above”含有“不耻于……”等意思。
例如：
If we want to learn, we must not be above asking questions.
He is not above telling a lie, if it will serve his purpose.
H注意下列词组的意思：
１）above all（首先，首要的是）
Think about what I have told you; above all, don't breathe a word of it to Henry.
２）keep one's head above water （脱离困境，摆脱债务）
Erown's business is not doing well; he is finding it difficult to keep his head above water.
３）get above oneself（自高自大）
He got above himself.
４）to be above oneself（兴高采烈）
He is above himself.
５）above sb's head（过于深奥使人不能理解）
The lecture was above the heads of the audience.
６）over and above（除……之外）
He gets a number of perquisites, over and above his salary.
７）above board（光明正大）
There's nothing secret about what we are doing. It's all perfectly above board.
　
according to
　　according to是由副词according 与介词to 组成的复合介词，表示“根据”、“按照”等意思。
A“根据”、“按照”
例如：
Do this problem according to the way you have been showed.
Take one to three tablets, according to the severity of the
pain.
B“据……所说”、“如……所示”
例如：
According to the Bible, Adam was the first man.
According to my watch it is half past two.
Note:
　　according to 用作介词；而according as 则用作连词，后接从句，意思是“根据……而……”、“按照……而……”。例如：
You may go or stay, according as you decide.
People see things differently according as they are materialists or idealists.
across
　　across含有“横过”、“在……另一边”、“交叉”等意思，并上“横过”引申为“经过”（一段时间）、“遍及”（整个地区）。
A“横过”、“穿过”
例如：
He went across the street to the baker's.
He walked across the square.
Note:
1.along 与across 相对应，across表示“横过一条直线或一个平面的运动”；along则只表示“沿着一条直线的运动”。
They walked not only along the paths but also across the grass.
2.across表示“横过”时，可以与 over替用。
They drove across /over the frontier.
B“在……的另一边“
例如：
They bought those things across the river.
Lincoln was carried unconscious to a house across the road from the theatre.
Note:
1我们可以把across与from短语搭配使用，具体表明“出发点”或“观察点”。
He lived across the street from my house.
2.across表示“静止空间位置”时，意思是“在……的另一边”；along表示“静止空间位置”时，含有“沿线”、“在……的另一端”等意思。
There is a hotel across the road.
There are trees all along the river banks.
C“经过”（一整段时间），“遍及”（整个地区）
例如：
I can remember, across the years.
They travelled across the country.
D注意下列短语的意思：
１）come (run) across（遭遇，巧遇）
They came across a tiger in the jungle.
An idea came across my mind.
２）get across sb（引起某人的不满或反对）
Dr. James has got across several of his patients because of his brusque outspoken manner.
３）across the board（全体成员）
It is an across-the-board pay rise.
　
after
　　after的基本意思是“在……之后”，可以表示时间、顺序、位置等的先后，与before相对应。此外还表示“由于”、“尽管”、“仿照”、“依照”、“追踪”、“追赶”、“追求”、“关于”等意思。
A表示时间,“在……之后”
例如：
After a sound sleep, he went for a swim.
After a rain the air is much cleaner than at ordinary time.
Note:
1表示时间“已过几点”或“几点过后”，在英国多用past，在美国则用after.
It's half after / past two.
It's after / past two o'clock.
2.after与具体的时间数字连用时，含有“在若干时间之后”的意思（at the end of a specified period），动词只能用过去时态。如：我们可以说“He came back after three years”（他三年之后回来了。）而当表示“从说话时间起经过若干时间之后”时，则不用after，而用in。如：He would (will) come back in （不用after）three years（他将于三年之后回来）。试比较：
After a little time Mrs Priestley joined us again.
He said he would come again in a few days.
3.after也可以用作副词，与具体的时间数字连用。当表示“在若干时间之后”时，动词也只能用过去时态；如果副词after单独使用，动词的时态则不受限制，这时的after相当于later或later on。
He fell ill on(upon) Monday and died three days after .
(=after three days = three days later)
We can do that after (= later = later on).
4副词later与具体的时间数字连用表示“经过若干时间以后”时，时间起点可以是过去或将来，但不能是现在。如我们只能说I shall see you in a week而不能说I shall see you a week later.
I called on(upon) him on 25 July, and called again a week later.
I shall call on him on 20 October, and shall call again a week later.
B表示顺序,“在……之后”（behind in place of position); （名望、地位）“次于”、“亚于”（below in rank or excellence); “跟着”、“接着”（following, behind)
例如：
They came to England after us (later than we did)
Your name comes after mine in the list.
Milton is usually placed after Shakespeare among English poets.
we drove mile after mile through open moorland.
Note:
1表示位置“在……之后”时，用behind比用after更为普通。
The sun was behind (hidden by) the clouds.
The boy was hiding behind a tree.
2.behind可以由表示位置的“在……后面”引申为“落后”、“不如”等意思，而after则不能。同样地，当after表示时间、顺序“在……之后”时，也不能代替behind。
The train was behind time.
The country is far behind its neighbours.
C“后果”、“结果”
after还可以由上述的“在……之后”、“经过……以后”引申出某种“后果”、“结果”的意思。
A）“鉴于”、“由于”、“因为”（as a consequence of, as a result of, because of)
例如：
You must be feeling tired after that long walk.
I dislike him after his treating us thus.
B）“尽管……但……”（ in spite of)
例如：
After all my advice, he still went his own way.
After all my care in packing it, the clock arrived broken.
Note:
after all单独用作状语时，有两种解释：
1“不管怎么样”、“到底”、“毕竟”等（in spite of)
He failed after all (in spite of all that had been done).
After all it is not so hard as it looks.
2“切记”、牢记“（It must be remembered that)
I know he hasn't finished the work, but after all he is a very busy man.
Why shouldn't she eat the cake? After all she baked it.
D“仿照”、“模仿”
例如：
These ships are built after the same model.
The painting is after the great master.
E“依照”、“与……相一致”
例如：
It is a job after his own heart.
(=of the kind he very much likes)
The boy was named Samuel after his uncle.
H“追赶”、“追求”、“追踪”
例如：
The boy raced after the butterfly.
What are you after?
They started after the enemy in hot haste.
Note:
1在call, shout等动词之后接after时，含有“唤起某人注意”的意思（attempting to attract attention)。例如：
I shouted after him, but apparently he did not hear, for he still went on.
2在某些动词（如seek,search, yearn, long等）之后。可以接after，也可以接for，同样表示“寻求”的对象、目的（to show the aim or object of certain actions)，但for 比after普通。例如：
We searched after / for the lost kitten.
He yearned after / for his son.
3.look after与look for有语义上的差别，试比较：
Will you look after my house while I'm away?
我不在的时候，请你照看一下我的家好吗？
He went to Australia to look for gold.
他到澳大利亚去寻找金子。
I“关于”
例如：
Mrs Jackson asked after you and the children.
He inquired after your health.
　
　
against
　　against可以表示“朝着”、“逆”、“反抗、反对”、“违反”、“倚靠在……上”、“撞在……上”、“在……对面”、“以……为背景”、“与……对比（对照）”、“预备”、“预防”等意思。中心意思是“对立”。
A“朝着”、“对着”
例如：
Stop knocking the stick against the fence.
The rain beats against the window-panes.
B“逆”
例如：
It is hard to swim against the current.
They sailed against the wind.
Note:
　　against表示方向的“对立”时，与with相对应。例如：
Sound waves go faster with the wind than against the wind.
It is much easier to row with the current than against it.
C“反抗”、“反对”
例如：
Is there a law against spitting in the streets in this country?
Note:
1在对某人某事表示反对或赞成时，against与for相对应。这里for的意思是in favour of。
Are you for or against the motion?
2在对处于冲突或竞争状态的双方中的一方表示反对或支持时，against与with相对应。这里的with的意思是on the same side of。
Are you with us or against us?
3.against在与fight连用、表示“跟”、“同”（……竞争、对抗）时，可与with互相替用。试比较：
Great Britain fought with / against Germany in two wars.
Great Britain fought with (on the side of) France against Germany.
4.against有时引申为“对……不利”。
Things have come out against us.
D“违反”
例如：
Smoking is against the school rules.
He did it against his will.
E“倚靠在……”
例如：
He rested his bicycle against the wall.
He stood with his back against the door.
Note:
　　动词lean之后有时接against，有时接on(upon)。如果依靠在某一侧面上，通常用against，但也可以用on；如果是倚靠在某一平面上，则只能用on。例如：
He leaned the ladder against / on the wall.
He leaned his elbows on the table.
F“与……相碰或相撞”
例如：
He hit his forehead against the kerb when he fell.
As I groped my way through the darkness of the room, I knocked against a chair.
G“在……对面”
例如：
Against the laboratory stands a water tower.
实验室对面有一座水塔。
He lives over against the school.
他住在学校对面。
H“以……为背景”，“在……的衬托下”
例如：
The house looks very pretty against the white snow.
The beautiful villa stood out against the slums surrounding it.
I“与……对比”、“与……对照”
例如：
Four students have passed this year's examination against three of last year.
3 against 0.
Note:
　as 可以与against连用，表示“对比”、“对照”。例如：
The business done this year amounts to ￡2,000 as against ￡1,500 last year.
J“防备”，“预防”
例如：
She always had a room ready against the arrival of unexpected visitors.
We must take precaution against fire.
　
along
　　along可以表示“沿着一条直线在水平方向上的运动”，也可以表示“在沿着一条直线边上的地理位置”，有时还含有“在……的另一端”的意思。
A“沿着”、“顺着”
----处于作为along的宾语的“道路”、“河流”等之中（in the direction of the length of）例如：
A good walk by the sea or along the cliff every day would do you a world of good.
Our boat made her way along the river.
Note:
1.along与“运动的动词连用，通常表示沿着一条直线在水平方向上的运动，与across相对应。
2.up与down通常表示沿着垂直方向向上或向下的运动，但up与down也可以转用于水平方向的运动，这时，up与down实际上等于along。
3.along可以跟here或there连用，表示朝着这个方向或那个方向(in this or that direction)。
He is walking along here.
It flew along there.
----处于作为along的宾语的“河流”或“道路”的近旁。（in a line next to the length of）例如：
He lives along the street.
Farmhouses were to be found here and there along the river.
Note:
　　along可以表示“在沿着一条线的某些地方”，也可以表示“沿着整条线”（from one end to another of the length or of any part of the length）。但为了明确表示“沿着整条线”，along通常与副词all连用。例如：
There are trees all along the river banks.
There were muddy foot marks all along the corridor.
B“在……的另一端”
例如：
There is a bank along the street.
Note:
　　表示在一条线的中部时，可以用half way along…这样的结构。例如：
Half way along the street they stopped.
　
alongside
　　alongside的意思是along和beside两者的结合，含有“在……旁边”，“与……一起（并肩）”，“横靠”等意思，此外还引申为“与……比较”的意思。
A“在……旁边”、“与……一起（并肩）”、“横靠”（side by side with）。例如：
The dog ran alongside me all the way.
It's a pleasure to work alongside such men.
Note:
　　alongside经常与of连用，但这时它成了副词。例如：
The dog trotted alongside of us.
B“与……比较”（in comparison with）
例如：
His works are second-rate productions, alongside those of some of his contemporaries.
　
along with
　　这里的along实际上是副词，with才是介词，但在许多情况下，along with结合得很紧密，起着一个复合介词的作用，意思是“与……一道（一起）”、“连同……”（in the company of, together with）。
例如：
He went on the dangerous journey along with his two friends.
Put this letter in the file, along with the others.
Note:
在口语中常说“Get / go along with you”意思是“滚开”或“我不相信你的话”。这里的along是一个独立的副词，与动词get / go组成一个意群，不同于前面论述过的复合介词along with。
　
amid(st)
　　amid也可以写成 amidst，意思是“在……当中”、“在……中” (in the middle of, among)。例如：
He persevered amidst all these difficulties.
The curtain fell amidst (= in the accompaniment of) the cheers of the audience.
Note:
1.amid(st)多见于文学用语。在英国口语中，amidst比amid常见，在美国口语中情况正好相反，即amid比amidst普通。
2.amidst跟amongst一样，可以指“在某一数量不定的群体之中”(apply to an indefinite number of objects)。但amidst可以与物质名词或抽象名词连用，而amongst则只能与复数的名词或代词连用，试比较：
Amidst / Amongst the flowers the rose is queen.
They walked amid(st) the snow.
　
among(st)
　　among也可以写成amongst，为了声音的和谐或句子的韵律，有时用among，有时则用，在美国通常用。含有“在……的包围中”、“在……之中（之间）”等意思，并引申为“其中之一”、“在……中间分配”、“通过……的联合行动”等意思。
A“在……的包围之中”、“在……之中”、“在……之间”
例如：
My home is among the mountains of Switzerland.
The mother is sitting among her children.
Note:
1.among(st)跟动词连用时，表示“从……中间（穿过）”（through the midst of）。
He passed among /through the crowd.
2当among(st )接具体物体时，有时可在among(st)之前加用in。例如：
I found the letter in amongst some papers that were on(upon) the table.
3.from among(st)…用来表示选择的“范围”。例如：
Choose a book from among these.
B“其中之一”、“包括在……当中”
例如：
Sir Winston Churchill can certainly be numbered among the great British Prime Ministers.
This mountain is among the highest of the world.
Note:
1.among之后接最高级形容词时，意思是“……的其中之一”；among(st)之前接最高级形容词时，意思是“其中之最”。试比较：
Shelley is among (one of) the world's greatest poets.
What is the greatest among (of all) European powers?
2.among others （或 among other things）的意思是“除……以外还（也）……” (in addition to others; along with others)。例如：
He among other things, talked about the excellent situation at home and abroad.
C“在……中间（分配）”
例如：
The sweets are to be shared among the five children.
The prizes were distributed among ten winners.
D“通过……的共同行动”（through the common action of），表示“合作”、“集体”的意思（cooperation or aggregation）例如：
Among them they collected over a hundred pounds.
They have settled the matter among themselves.
Note:
1.among(st)之后必须接复数名词或代词，但在口语中，有时用among(st) each other代替among(st) themselves / ourselves。例如：
They agreed among each other (themselves).
They are quarrelling among themselves.
2.between通常表示“在两者之间”， among(st)则表示“在三者或三者以上之间”。试比较：
The money was divided between the two persons.
The money was divided among the three persons.
3.between有时也可以表示“在三者或三者以上之间”，但它强调了每两者之间的关系或区别。例如：
The five diplomats settled the question between them. (Between is here used to emphasize the fact that each member of the group agreed individually with each of the others.)
4在表示地理位置时，通常也用between表示“在三者或三者以上之间”。例如：
Switzerland lies between France, Germany, Italy and Austria.
　
apart from
　　这里的apart是副词，from是介词。在许多情况下，apart from的作用相当于一个复合介词，它可以表示“扣除……以外”（except for），也可以表示“除……以外（还，也）”（=besides）。
A“除开……（就，都）”、“只有……”
例如：
The children hardly see any one, apart from their parents.
We had a pleasant time, apart from the weather.
B“除开……（还，也）”、“不但”
例如：
What do you study apart from English?
Apart from cost, the hat doesn't suit me.
Note:
1试比较下列两句：
We had no trouble on(upon) the journey apart from (except for) a flat tyre.
Apart from (In addition to) a flat tyre, we had faulty brakes.
2有时，apart在意思上并没有与from组成复合介词，而是单独用作副词，如：
One girl sat apart from the rest.
3.aside from是美语的用法，意思等于apart from。
　
　
apropos (of)_
　　apropos 原是副词，来自法语，与介词of连用，组成复合介词，含有“关于”、“讲到”等意思。
A“关于”、“至于”、“就……来说”，常和of一起组成复合介词。例如：
Apropos of that matter I don't believe one half of it.
B“谈到”、“提起”
例如：
Apropos of earthquakes, we felt one last night.
Apropos of John's new job, did he tell you how much he is
earning?
Note:
apropos of 偶尔也写成apropos to。
　
around
　　around与round的意思基本相同，英国常用round，美国常用around。around表示“在……附近”、“到……各处”、“以……为中心（基础）”、“绕过……”等意思。
A“在……四周”（on all sides of, all around, surrounding）例如：
A thick grey mist was all around us.
Many people stood around the injured man.
Note:
1.around和round都可以表示“围绕某个轴心的相对位置”。但当表示“围绕某个轴心转动”时，在正式文体里通常用round。
2在口语中，不论表示什么意思，around通常都可以与round互相替用。
B“在……附近”（in some place near to, in the area of）
例如：
Most of the houses around here were built between the two world wars.
He lives somewhere around London.
Note:
在口语中，around和about都可以表示“在……附近”的意思；但about比around正规。
C“到……各处”
例如：
The news soon travelled around the world.
Would you like to walk around my garden?
D“以……为中心（基础）”
例如：
The society was built around a belief in God.
E“避开……”、“绕过……”
例如：
John did not weigh enough to join the navy, but he got around that; he drank a lot of water before his physical examination.
　
as
　　as可以表示“象……”、“以……资格（身份）”、“扮演……角色”、“以……形式”、“为了……”、“因为……”、“当某人是……的时候”、“似乎是……”、“（看成）是……”、“例如……”等意思。
A“象”、“如同”
例如：
They all rose together as one man.
He behaved as a drunk.
B“以……资格（身份）”
例如：
He came here as a specialist.
She spent some weeks as a nurse in a hospital in Paris.
C“扮演……角色”
例如：
The illustration shows Sir Henry Irving as Shylock in the "Merchant of Venice".
He acted several times as Hamlet.
D“以……形式”
例如：
We all start life as babies.
E“为了……目的”、“作为……用途”
例如：
This watch was given as a birthday present.
The building was originally intended as a house for aged workers.
F“因为是 …”
　　在这种用法中，as是as being的省略形式，所以含有“因为是……”的意思。如：
We chose this one as the most suitable for our purpose.
Three men were detained as suspects.
G“当某人是……的时候”
例如：
As a schoolboy he showed every sign of genius.
She showed me a portrait of her mother as a young woman.
H“似乎是……”
例如：
Though their daughter was in her early twenties, they still treated her as a child (=as if she were a child).
Disguised as a clergyman, Charles went aboard a small ship, the Doutelle, to sail for Scotland.
I“把……看成……”
例如：
He viewed his son's lawless behaviours as an attack on himself.
I acknowledged him as the best player (=to be the best player) in our team.
Note:
1在某些动词之后（例如count, elect, appoint等），引导补语的as可以省略；但在动词regard之后，引导补语的as 则不能省略。例如：
I count him (as) my friend.
They elected Nixon (as) president.
They appointed him (as) chairman.
I regarded him as my brother.
2当consider的意思是“认为”、“以为”（regard as）时，它的补语之前不能带as； 但如果consider意思是“考虑”、“思考”（think about, give one's mind to）时，它的补语之前必须带as。例如：
I consider it a good thing (not "as" a good thing).
We will consider Hamlet as an example of a Shakespearian tragedy.
3在动词take之后有时用as，有时用for来引导补语，意思不同。“to take … as…”意为“把……当作……来用”，而“to take … for …”则意为“把……（误）当作……来看待”。例如：
He took these words as evidence.
He took me for an actor.
4在动词mistake之后，只能用for来引导补语。例如：
I mistook the stick for a snake.
J“例如……”
例如：
She has certain definite beauties, as her hair.
In some words in English the initial "h" is not sounded, as "hour", "honour", "honesty", etc.
Note:
1在列举事例时，通常应该用such as而不要单独用as一个词。
2英语常用“as…as…”这种结构进行同程度的两者间的比较。这里的第一个as是副词；第二个as是连接词，用于引导比较的对象。由于连接词as之后通常省略与主句重复的成分，使得as逐渐趋近于介词。
He thought he would walk as quickly as his friend, but he couldn't.
3在口语中，“He is as tall as me”比“He is as tall as I”更普通，因为这里的谓语动词是不及物的，所以as之后的代词不论用主格还是用宾格的形式，都不会引起句子意思的改变。但如果谓语动词是及物的，as之后就得用宾格代词，这时，这个代词可以理解为动词的宾语，也可以理解为动词的主语。为了避免这种意思上的含糊，在正式文体中主格和宾格代词是区别使用的。试比较：
You hate him as much as I.
你象我一样恨他。
You hate him as much as me.
你象恨我一样恨他。
　
as from
　　as from是复合介词，意思是“从……时候起”（from the time stated），相当于复合介词as of，主要用于正式函件。例如：
As from / of today you are in charge.
The guarantee will cease to be operative as from 24th April, 1999.
　
as for
　　as for是复合介词，意思是“至于……”、“就……方面说”。例如：
As for the terms, they are quite agreeable to me.
I could stay for one day or two days, but as for staying a week, it would be out of the question.
Note:
1.as to有时用来代替as for的这种用法。
I don't blame George. As for (as to) John, he has behaved very badly.
2. with regard to, with respect to, with reference to等词组也表示as for的意思，但它们用于较正式的文体，而且还可以用来引述新的话题，尤其是在商业函件中。
With regard to your letter of June 22nd, I enclose our most-recent catagories.
3.as for还经常用来对某人或某事表示轻蔑。
As for you, I never want to see you here again.
As for others, they didn't count.
　
as of
　　as of的复合介词，含有“从……时起”和“到……时止”等意思。
A“从……时起”
例如：
The rule takes effect as of July 1.
B“到……时止”
例如：
As of this writing, no reply has been received.
Note:
短语as of right意思是“按法律”（according to law, by right）。
All that money are now yours as of right.
　
as to
　　as to是复合介词，含有“至于”、“关于”、“按照”等意思。
A“至于”
例如：
As to your final grade, that depends on your final examination.
As to your brother, I will deal with him later.
B“关于”
例如：
I don't know anything as to the others.
No agreement was reached as to how much we should pay.
Note:
1.as to表示“关于”时，常与表示疑问、争论、怀疑等意思的词连用。
He inquired me as to the state of my health.
I have no doubts as to your son's ability.
2在动词之后用as to表示“关于”的这种用法不算正规，但很常见。
We want to know from you as to whether you have ever experienced any cruelty.
在名词或形容词之后用as to表示“关于”的用法完全正确。
I am terribly uncertain as to whether he is the right person for me.
C“按照”
例如：
They sorted the eggs as to size and colour.
　
astride
　　astride的意思是“跨在……上”，又引申为“横跨在……的两旁”。严格地说，astride是副词，它和介词of连用，形成astride of这样的结构，表示“跨在……上”的意思。但现在astride已被单独用作介词。
A“跨在……上”
例如：
He is sitting astride a horse.
The boy sits astride a chair.
B“横跨在……的两旁”
例如：
There is a bridge astride the river.
　
at
　　介词at的释义及用法很多，现分述如下：
A“在……（地方）”
用来表示空间位置上的某一点（used with something seen as a point）。例如：
The two rivers join at this point.
Can you meet me at the station, as I shall have a lot of luggage?
Note:
1不管大地方或小地方，都可以从远处着眼，视为“一点”，而将其形态、大小忽略。因此，at所表示的是一种比较笼统的空间位置，不涉及与其周围的人或物的相对关系，意思相当于汉语的“在 …（地方）”，若要表示具体的相对位置，则用in（在……里面）、on(upon)（在……上面）、by（在……旁边）等介词。试比较：
There was a ring at the door.
There was woodworm (=damage caused by wood-worms) in the door.
There was a new coat of paint on the door.
There was a dog by the door.
He is at the station.
He is in the station.
2.at可以表示端点、极点的位置。例如：
He was standing at the top of the hill.
You know you can't burn the candle at both ends and you need a good rest.
3.at 还可以表示进出点。例如：
I've noticed that everything you tell her goes in at one ear and out at the other.
"When poverty comes in at the door, love flies out at the window." Don't believe it!
4一般地说，在地址、建筑物、村庄或小城镇之前用at，在较重要的城镇、城市、省份、国家等之前用in，试比较：
She lives at the white cottage.
She lives in London.
Germany is a country in Europe.
5事物的大小是相对而言的，当我们把象纽约这样的大城市看成是地球上的一点时，我们可以用at New York；而当我们把它看成是一个区域，想到里头的街道、建筑物等时，则可以用in New York。试比较：
We stopped to refuel at New York on(upon) our way to Tokyo.
We have a house in New York.
6在动词arrive后面，用at表示到达的地点，用in表示到达的区域，用on表示到达的现场。试比较：
The train arrived at Beijing at 11:30.
火车11点30分到达北京（车站）。
The train arrived in Beijing at 11:30.
火车11点30分到达北京（城）。
Two policemen arrived on(upon) the scene after a few minutes.
7在英国，街道（street），路（road）之前一般用in（在美国则一般用on），意思是“在街上”、“在马路上”；如果在街名、公路名之前用at时，则指该街的公共汽车站或该路的火车站。例如：
For the British Museum, alight at Tottenham Court Road.
Does this bus stop at Great Portland Street?
8.in the corner of指“在……的角落里”，at the corner of则指“在……拐角处”。例如：
He is sitting in the corner of the room.
The house stands at / on(upon) the corner.
9.at后接场所时，不一定指具体的地点名称，而可以引申为事件发生的场合。例如：
How many people were there at the lecture？
Ｉfirst met him at a dinner party given by a friend of mine.
10注意下列由表示空间位置的引申出来的比喻用法：
The British public was always solidly at her back.
I think I ought to tell you how we arrived at the decision.
B“在……（时刻）”
表示时间上的某一点（used with a point of time)
At seven o'clock tomorrow I shall be going to a party.
I was a child at that time and soon forgot my sorrow.
试比较下列两句：
An examination is held at the end of the term.
In the end (=at last) they reached a place of safety.
Note:
1一般地说，表示“在某一时刻”用at，如：at 5:15 am（上午5点15分）, at dawn/daybreak/noon/sunset/nightfall/dinner-time（在黎明、拂晓、中午、傍晚、黄昏、吃饭时间）；表示在“某一天”用on，例如：on(upon) Sunday（在星期天），(on) the following day （第二天）；表示“比一天长或比一天短的时间”用in /during，例如：in the week / month /season/year/century（在本周、本月、本季度、本年、本世纪），in the morning /afternoon/evening（上午、下午、晚上）。
2泛指“在晚上”时用at night，指“在某一个晚上”时，用in the night或during the night。by night有时可以代替during the night。
We always lock the door at night.
I woke up three times in /during the night.
3虽然“比一天短的时间”可以用in来表示，但如果要具体表明这段时间属于哪一天时，却要用on。例如：
I do most of my work in the morning.
They landed on(upon) the morning of May lst.
We'll have a party on Sunday night.
4圣诞、新年等节日通常被视为一个时节，因此用介词at，如：at Christmas（圣诞节期间），at Easter（复活节时节）等。如果指的是节日当天，则用介词on，如：on New Year's day（元旦那天），on Christmas Day (on Christmas-day（圣诞节那天），on Christmas Eve(on Christmas 圣诞节前夕）。
How do people greet their friends at Christmas?The usual greeting is "A Merry Christmas".And at New Year, we say "A Happy New Year".
We have a holiday at Easter.
We will begin our journey on(upon) Easter Sunday.
5表示“在周末”时，英国人用at the week-end，美国人则用on(upon) the weekend.
6可以用来表示年龄。
Shakespeare died at (the age of)fifty-two.
He ought to be earning his own living at his age.
He has to work hard in his old age.
7.at还可以用来表示顺序。
At first it seemed very easy, but it soon got more difficult.
He was ill and at last he died.
C“向……”、“针对……”
　 表示行为的目标、对象（used with an attended aim or object towards which a thing or an action is directed）, 例如：
It sounded as if someone was tapping at (on) the window.
They are practising shooting at the target.
Note:
1.at可以表示瞄准者心目中的目标，但未必打中，试比较：
He shot at a bird, but missed it.
He shot a bird and killed it.
2.at行为的目标与某些动词连用时，常含有敌意，而to则纯粹表示行为的方向，试比较：
He threw the ball at me (tried to hit me).
他掷球打我。
He threw the ball to me (for me to catch).
他把球传给我。
3在look at、 gaze at 等词组里，at只表示视线的目标。如果用on代替at，on则表示视线的落处。这种视线还带有某种特殊的情感，因此它们常与with所构成的短语连用，表明这种情感的具体内容。试比较：
The girl is pretty to look at.
He seemed to look on(upon) me with distrust.
The young American gazed at me in surprise.
He gazed on her with rapture and admiration.
4.smile at中的at表示以笑传情的对象、目标；这对象、目标有的滑稽可笑、有的愚昧无知（to have a feeling which a smile expresses; consider something slightly funny and silly）。而smile on则表示“对……的赞许”或“对……加以青睐”（to act or look favourably）。例如：
What are you smiling at?
She smiled at his threats.
The weather smiled on(upon) us.
His mother smiled on him.
D“在……中”
表示状态（a state or condition in which things, a person, group of persons are）例如：
Tom is never at rest except when he is asleep.
The countries are at war / at peace.
E“从事于”,试比较：
Mr Green is now at dinner.
I hope to see you at dinner this evening.
Were you at the meeting?
I have been at the funeral.
Note:
1在口语中，at常用来表示doing的意思。
What are you at ? (= What are you doing?)
你在干什么？
2在口语中，at it 用来表示doing something的意思，即泛指“在忙着什么事情”。就可分别与介词at，for, about连用，表示不同的情感。试比较：
He was anxious at her non-arrival.
她未到来使他感到忧虑不安。（刺激）
The boy was anxious for a new bicycle.
这男孩渴望得到一部新的自行车。（目的）
Mrs Owens is anxious about her son's health.
欧文斯太太担心着她儿子的健康。（内容）
3在英国英语中，当引起感情刺激的是人或物、而不是抽象的言行时，则不用介词at，而用介词with。
The children were pleased with their presents.
They felt very pleased with themselves because they could speak English.
但在美国英语中，即使引起刺激的是人或物，也常用介词at。
I am angry at / with my wife.
I was pleased at / with John's present.
F“在……方面”
　　用在某些表示能力或造诣的形容词或名词后面，说明某种能力或造诣所属的领域。例如：
He is no good at describing things or expressing ideas.
He is an expert at repairing clocks.
试比较下列两句：
He is skillful at using chopsticks.
他筷子用得很熟练。（比较简单的动作）
He is skillful in managing school.
他善于管理学校。（比较复杂的工作）
“以……”
　　表示速度、比率、价格、代价等（the rate either of movement or of the charge made for goods or services）
Ｈe is driving at (the rate of) sixty miles an hour.
With the ground, the house is valued at 10,000 dollars.
The ship was built at an immense cost.
G“至”、“最”
表示极端的状况：
You might at least send me words that you were coming.
You ought to hear at the latest by Friday.
Note:
at once, at all等短语也可以算是这一类用法。
The doctor promised to come at once.
You haven't done well at all.
　
athwart
　　athwart含有“横过……”、“与……相反”等意思，多用于航海方面，而在一般口语或书面语中都少用。
A“横过……”、“横跨……”
A bridge was thrown athwart the river.
B“与……相反”、“逆……”
It went athwart his purpose.
The ship sailed athwart our path.
　
atop
　　atop是文学用语，意思是“在……顶上”，相当于on the top of, at the top of。例如：
He is standing atop the tall building.
The old man lived in a cottage atop the hill.
　
bar
　　bar的意思是“除……以外”，相当于except 或excluding,但现在很少用了。例如：
The group was at the party, bar John.
He gave us all bar three of the sketches.
　
barring
　　barring跟bar一样，可以表示“除……以外”；此外，它不含有“若无”、“除非”等意思。
A“除……以外”
The whole group was at the party, barring John.
B“若无”、“除外”
Barring rain, we leave tonight.
We knew that barring a miracle, they could never be able to do it.
　
because of
　　because of是由副词 because和介词of组成的复合介词，意思是“因为”、“由于”（owing to, on account of, as a result of）。例如：
Because of his poor health, he could not work in a tropical country.
I said nothing about it because of his wife's being there.
Note:
由because of所构成的介词短语通常用作状语，有时也用作表语，但不能用作定语。
　
before
　　before的基本意思是“在……之前”。可以表示“位置”、“时间”、“次序”、“地位”等，并可引申表示“与其……（勿宁……）”的意思。
A“在……的前面”
表示位置（in front of）
例如：
He sat just before me.
The ship sailed before the wind.
Note:
1.before表示位置时与behind相对应。
2表示“在某建筑物的前面”这个意思时，不用before in引导的介词短语，而用复合介词in front of。
例如：
There are some trees in front of the house.
A big oak tree stood in front of the building.
3.before可以由“在……前面”引申为“在……面前”（on the presence of），尤指在权威者面前。它还可以引申为“在……前头”，常指“前途”、“前程”，等。
例如：
Your suggestion will be placed before the committee.
The headmaster congratulated the boy before the whole school.
We have a long journey before us.
You are young men with the world before you.
B“在……以前”
表示时间（earlier in time than）
例如：
My sister arrived that night before ten o'clock.
I met Smith yesterday, but I knew him long before that.
Note:
1.before表示时间时，与after相对应。
2.before有时指“在……的前一个”(immediately proceeding)。
例如：
I don't quite know what I was doing the day before yesterday.
He sold his shares in the company the year before last.
3.before long的意思是“不久” (in a short time) ；而long before的意思是“很久以前” (a long time early)（前者中的before是介词，后者中的before是副词）。试比较：
I'm afraid Susan will go, too, before long.
I had been there long before.
4.before有时与动名词短语连用，表示“在做……之前”。
例如：
Before crossing the road, you should look both ways.
I should like to think the matter again before giving my reply.
5在否定句中，before常与till(until)替用。
例如：
We did not leave before / until 9 o'clock.
我们到9点才离开。
6.before one's time含有“在某人所处的时代之前”，或“超越某人所处的时代所能接受的”等意思。
The motorcar was invented before my time.
Robert Owen, with his enlightened idea on(upon) working conditions in factories, was before (ahead of) his time.
C“在……之前”，“先于……”
表示次序或地位（occupying a superior place in order of precedence or importance）
例如：
Work should come before pleasure.
He always puts the interests of others before his own.
D“与其……（勿宁……）”（in preference to, rather than）
例如：
They would die before yielding.
Death before dishonour.
　
behind
　　behind可以表示“在（向）……后面”、“在……的那边”等空间位置，并引申表示“紧随在……之后”、“躲藏在……后面”、“作……的后盾”、“在……过后”、“遗留在……后面”、“落后于……”、“迟于……”等意思。
A“在……后面”、“向……后面”
　表示位置（at or to the back of），例如：
One of the things I liked best was the garden behind the house.
Look behind the tree.
Note:
1.behind表示位置时，不能与after替用。
2.behind有时含有“紧随在……之后”的意思，相当于following。
例如：
There was rain behind the wind.
The dog ran behind its master.
3.behind还常用来表示“躲藏在……后面”，“隐藏在……之后”。
例如：
There is someone behind the door.
Malice lay behind his smile.
4.behind sb有时含有“置之脑后”、“不予考虑”等意思。
例如：
He put the idea behind him.
He put unhappy memories behind him.
5.behind one's back或behind the scenes都含有“在背后”、“秘密地”的意思。
例如：
Never say anything behind a person's back that you couldn't say to his face.
The decisions were made behind the scenes.
6.behind有时指“在……的那一端”。
例如：
The chemist's shop is a few yards behind the church.
B“作……的后盾”、“支持……” (in support of)
例如：
In these proposals we have the whole party behind us.
He has powerful men behind him.
C“在……随后”、“随手”(after one has passed through)
例如：
She bolted the door behind her.
Shut the door behind you.
Note:
　　behind表示“随手”时，可以与after替用，但behind更常用。
D“在……身后”、“在……死后”（remaining after one has departed）
例如：
He left a wife and two children behind him.
Though Milton is dead, he still lives in the works he has left behind him.
E“落后于……”、“不如……”（inferior to, lower than）
例如：
John is rather behind the rest of the class in mathematics.
She is in some things behind her years.
F“迟于……”（later than, after）
例如：
He arrived at the office ten minutes behind me.
G“对……来说成为过去”、“不复存在”（in the past and not likely to occur again）例如：
Your hard days are behind you now.
All our difficulties are now behind us.
H注意下列短语的意思：
1）behind time（迟、误）
We are five minutes behind time already.
The train is ten minutes behind its time.
You are behind the appointed time.
2）behind the times（落后于时代、过时的、老式的）
These attitudes are behind the times.
His ideas are well behind the times.
　
below
　　below可以表示“在……下面”、“在……下游”、“在……这一边”、“在……以下地段”、“在……南部”等空间位置，并引申表示等级、地位、数量、年龄、价值、标准、水平、程度等的“在……之下”以及“与……不相称”、“有损……的尊严”等意思。
A“在……下面”
表示位置（in a lower place than, on a lower level than）
例如：
Sign your name below mine on this document.
The sun sank below the horizon.
Note:
1.below泛指“在……下方”，而不一定是“在……的正下方”。below与above相对应。如：
Shall I write my name on, above or below the line?
2. under通常表示垂直向下而且靠得比较近的位置，below有时可用来取代under，表示上述意思，如：
He had a bruise just below/under his left eye.
但是，under却不能随意与below互相替用。试比较：
They are the most distressed families under roof.
他们是人间最贫苦的家庭。
The Johnsons live in the flat below this one.
约翰逊一家住在这下面一层套间。（不一定在垂直的下方）。
3. below,beneath和underneath常可以互相替用，表示“在……下面”，但beneath比较不通用，多用于正式文体，“underneath”常对有互相接触的二者而言。例如：
He is swimming below/beneath/underneath the surface.
4. below有时还引申为“隐藏在……背后”的意思。如：
Do you know the real reason below the mass of pretexts?
B“在……的下游”、“在……的这一边”、“在……以下的地段”、“在……的南部”如：
There was a boat a few yards below the bridge.
The bus stop is a few yards below the post office.
Below the village the valley opens into the broad flat meadow.
Richmond is below (further south than) Washington.
C“在……之下”
　　表示程度、地位、数量、年龄、价值、标准、水平、等级等（lower I rank, amount, value, etc. than）如：
He is a grade below me.
I occupy only a humble position in the firm, but there are several people below me.
The number is below ten.
He cannot be much below sixty.
There is nothing below 2 shillings.
He is below the standard height for a soldier.
The rainfall of this month is below the average.
Note:
1.below仅表示“等级低下”，under则可以表示支配、统辖关系。试比较：
He is below me in rank.
He has good men under his orders.
2.under可以表示数量“在……之下”。注意：在下面句子中，below用于一般场合，under用于具体场合。例如：
A gold dollar weighing under 28.5 grains is below the standard.
D“与……不相称”、“有损……的尊严”（unworthy of ）
例如：
He thought manual labour below him.
It is below him to say such a thing.
Note:
　　below表示“与……不相称”、“有损……的尊严”时，可以与beneath互相替用。
E注意下列短语的意思：
1）hit below the belt 暗中伤人
The boxer was disqualified for hitting below the belt.
Most of the criticisms hit below he belt.
2）below one's breath 低声地
He protested below his breath because he was afraid to speak up.
　
beneath
　　beneath可以表示“在……（正）下方”、“在……脚下”等空间位置，并引申表示“比……不如”、“不值得……”、“有损……的尊严”等意思。
A“在……（正）下方”
如：
Beneath his rough exterior was a heart of gold.
Note:
1.beneath可以与under或below互相替用，表示“在……正下方”、“在……下面”；但在诸如下列那些固定词组中，它们是不能互相替用的：
beneath notice
below average
under the influence of drink
beneath主要用于正式文体。
2.under虽然可以与beneath或below互相替用，但under常起着突出“覆盖物”的作用。如下列两句中，第一句只一般表明猫睡觉的位置，人们的注意力集中在“猫”身上；第二句引起读者注意的则首先是“table”，其次才是“cat”，即突出了“猫”上面的“桌子”。
A cat was sleeping beneath the table.
A cat was sleeping under the table.
B“在……脚下”（directly under or at the foot of）
如：
There was a flower border beneath the wall.
C“比……不如”（inferior to, not suitable to the rank of）
如：
As writer of tragedy, most of the other Elizabethan dramatists are far beneath Shakespeare
She is beneath him in birth and education.
D“不值得……”、“有损……的尊严”、“有失……的身份”（unworth of）
如：
Such a fellow as that is beneath my notice.
It is beneath his dignity to plead with her father.
It is beneath you to say so.
　
beside
　　beside可以表示“在……旁边”、“在……附近”、“沿着……的旁边”等空间位置，并引申表示“与……相比”、“与……无关”等意思。
A“在……旁边”、“在……附近”
如：
The bride stands beside the bridegroom.
There is an orchard beside the house, about half a mile off.
Note:
1.beside和by都可以表示“在……旁边”，也都可能泛指“在……附近”。
2.beside有时表示“沿着……的旁边”。如：
The road leads beside a branch of the White River.
There was a ditch beside the road.
3短语stand by sb，含有“支持某人”的意思；因此，单纯位置上的“站在……的旁边”应译成stand beside…。试比较：
He stood beside me.
He stood by me.
B“与……相比”
如：
He isn't very good at arithmetic, but beside Harry, he is a genius at it.
You are quite tall beside your sister.
C“在……之外”、“与……无关”
Your statements are beside the mark.
D注意下列短语的意思：
1）beside oneself（发狂，因为强烈的情绪而推动自制）
He was beside himself with rage when the train left without him.
She flung her arms around her son, beside herself with happiness.
2）beside one's patience with（对……耐不住）
At last he was beside his patience with them.
　
besides
　　besides可以表示“除……外（还有）”的意思；在否定句中，还可以表示“除……外（不再）”的意思。
A“除……外（还有）”
　　用在肯定句里（as well as, in addition to），例如：
There were three others present at the meeting besides Mr Day.
Besides a mother, he has a sister to support.
B“除……以外（就没有）”
　　用在否定句里，例如：
We spoke of no one besides you.
Have you nothing to tell besides what I have already heard?
Note:
1用在肯定句里的except和besides含义不同。except的意思是“把……除外（不计）”，即所扣除的东西“不包括在内”（not including），是一种带否定的说法。besides则表示“除……以外（还、也……）”、“既……又……”，是一种带肯定的说法。试比较：
All of us passed besides John (John passed, too).
All of us passed except John (John didn't pass.).
2除了besides外，我们还可以用in addition to, as well as等复合介词来表示“附加”。例如：
They stole three valuable paintings, in addition to the money.
You can go as well as Geore.
3.besides之后除了接名词外，还可以接动名词短语；当besides之前的动词是do时，besides之后还可以用原形的动词。如：
What has he done besides reading / read the paper?
　
between
　　between的意思是“在……之间”、“在……之中”，可以表示时间、位置、数量、状态、相互关系、共同作用的结果（效果）等。
A“在……之间”、“在……之中”
　　表示位置或时间，例如：
She lost her purse somewhere between the bank and the post office.
The burglary took place between 9.30 and 10.30 p.m.
B“在……之间”、“来往于……之间”、“从……到……”、“介乎……之间”
　　表示数量和状态，例如：
We must have walked between eleven and twelve miles.
It occurred between his incoming and outgoing.
The flavour is between sore and sweet.
The liner sails between Southampton and New York.
It is about thirty miles between Nottingham and Sheffield.
C“把……隔开”
例如：
There is a wide river between the two villages.
Don't let such a trivial matter as this come between us.
D“在……之间”
　　表示两者或两者以上的相互关系，例如：
The relation between teacher and pupil is not what it used to be.
Divide this between you four.
E“由于……共同作用的结果（效果）”
例如：
Between us, we ought to be able to find a solution to the problem.
The members of the class collected fifty dollars between them.
Note:
1.between通常指“在（两者）之间”，among(st)则指“在（三者或三者以上）之间”。不过，有时也可以用来表示“在（三者或三者以上）之间”，强调其中每两者之间的关系或区别。例如：
There were flowers between the trees.
We know the difference between gases, solids and liquids.
2在表示地理上明显、准确的位置时，只能用between来表示“在……之间”，而不能用among，即使是指“处于三者或三者以上之间”也一样。例如：
Ecuador lies between Columbia, Peru and the Pacific Ocean.
3.between each house或between every house之类的短语，从语法上说是不规范的，因为在between之后通常不能接单数的名词或代词。但这种用法很常见，而且在许多名家的作品中也不乏其例。这种短语的意思是between each one and the next。
4当between用来表示“由于……共同作用的结果 / 效果”时，也可以指三者或三者以上的“之间”。例如：
Between cooking, cleaning, washing, sewing and knitting, she was very busy.
Between the four factories, this project was soon completed.
5短语between you and me 也可以写成between you, me and the gatepost或between ourselves，意思是“只限于咱们俩之间（不得外传）”。例如：
Between you and me, I am considering resigning from the post of club secretary.
Between you and me, he's rather stupid.
　
betwixt
　　betwixt的意思相当于between,但现在很少用，仅出现于诗歌中，也偶尔见于带幽默的口语中。在诗句中常缩写成'twixt。例如：
There's many a slip 'twixt cup and lip.
Note:
　　短语betwixt and between既可用作介词，也可用作副词，意思是“两者之间”。例如：
Grey is neither white nor black, but betwixt and between (the two).
　
　
beyond
　　beyond表示位置“在（向）……的那边”，并引申表示范围、能力“超出……”、数量“多于……”、时间“迟于……”；以及“除……之外”等意思。
A“在（向）……的那边”
　　表示位置，例如：
The woods go for about two miles beyond the river.
Do not allow yourself to go beyond the limit.
Note:
　　表示“在（向）……的那边”时，past常用来代替beyond。它们的区别在于：beyond不但指明两点位置，而且还暗示了讲话人的位置，即“观察点”; past则没有这个意思，试比较：
I could see the town beyond the lake.
There is a village past / beyond the bus stop.
B“超出……”
　　表示范围、能力，例如：
The scenery is beautiful beyond my description.
That prisoner's guilt was proved beyond doubt.
Note:
　　beyond之后接某人时，常指“超出某人的能力”。例如：
He soon realized the task he had undertaken was beyond him.
If it is beyond George, it is certainly beyond me.
C“迟于……”
　　表示时间（later than），例如：
Don't stay out beyond midnight.
It is an hour beyond his usual time.
Note:
　　beyond表示时间“迟于……”时，相当于after。实际上，在这个意义上，after比beyond更常见。
D“除……以外”
例如：
I know nothing about the newcomer beyond what I have been told.
Note:
1表示“除……以外”的beyond通常见于否定句或疑问句。例如：
He has nothing beyond his pension.
Is there anything else I can do for you, beyond what I've done?
2短语“I wouldn't put it beyond him”的意思是“我不敢说他就不至于如此”。例如：
I have no proof that he forged the signature, but I would not put it beyond him.
　
but
　　but用作介词时的意思是“除……以外”，相当于except，但近来常与no, nobody, nothing, all, everybody, any where一类词或与who, what, where等疑问词连用。此外，它还与first, next, last等顺序词连用，表示与顺序有关的位置。
A与no, nothing, nobody一类词连用。例如：
No one but Shelock Holmes can solve this problem.
You have no choice but marry her now.
I heard nothing but the wind.
B与all, anything, everything一类词连用。例如：
They are all wrong but me.
Everybody went but himself.
They looked in every place but the right one.
C与who, what, where等疑问词连用。例如：
Who but a fool would do such a thing?
What is he but a student?
Note:
1.but,except,save等是同义词，都表示“除……以外”，但save主要用于正式文体，but只能与no, nothing, all, anybody, who, what, where等词连用。在下列句子中，第一句中的but,except,save可以互相替用，但第二句不能用but。试比较：
We have lessons every day but/except/save Sunday.
The window is never opened except/save in summer.
2.except, excepting通常用作后置修饰语，如all except three；而but则毫不例外地只用作后置修饰语，如any time but now因此，but用作介词时，不能出现于句首，如不能说“But me, everyone was tired”而应该说：“Everyone but me was tired”或“Everyone was tired but me”。在“But me no buts”这样的句子中，句首的but是动词，另一个but是名词，这句话的意思是“不要老是跟我说‘但是’、‘但是’了。”3.but之后接代词时，有时既可以是宾格，也可以是主格。但在口语中通常用宾格。接主格代词时，but算是连词。例如：
No one can do it but him (but he).
4在下面第一个句子里，因为but所组成的短语紧接在动词之前，貌似主语，所以but后的代词习惯上用主格he，若用宾格him反而觉得不自然。（实际上，all 才是真正的主语，而but he只是all的修饰语 。）试比较：
The boy stood on(upon) the burning deck, whence all but he had fled.
I haven't told this secret to anyone but her.
5在口语中，nothing but常用来强调but后所接的名词，表示“不以为然”、“厌烦”等语气。例如：
He is nothing but a student.
That car has given me nothing but troubles ever since I have had it.
6.anything but之后可以跟形容词或名词，常用于避免正面的陈述，即所谓的“不充分的陈述”。例如：
She looks anything but well (She looks ill).
He is anything but mad (far from being mad).
He is anything but a clever man (he is a fool)
7.but之后可以接动词不定式，也可以接动名词。到底该接哪一种，这取决于but之后的成分与句中的哪个成分相当。如下列第一句中的making money与nothing平行，第二句中的to make money与for nothing 平行。nothing but, anything but之后的不定式可以不带to。例如：
He thinks of nothing but making money.
He lives for nothing but to make money.
D与first, next, last等顺序词连用。
　　but与顺序词连用时，表示与顺序词隔若干距离。例如，first but one的意思是“顺数第二”（one from the first）， last but two的意思是“倒数第三”（two from the last）。例如：
Mr Brown lives in the next house but one to us.
Take the next turning but one on your left.
　
but for
　　but for 是复合介词，意思是“若不是”、“要不是”（if not, without），作用相当于否定条件句。例如：
But for the sun, we could not live.
But for the doctor's skill, he would have died.
Note:
except for有时可以用来代替but for以表示这个意思。
　
by
　　by的含意十分广泛，可以表示“被……”、“通过……”、“在……旁边（附近）”、“偏……方向”“经过……旁边”、“途经……”、“抓（握）某人或某物的……部位”、“（数量、程度）达到……”、“以…为单位”、“尺寸是……乘……”、“每次……地”、“由于……”、“按照（根据）……”、“就……而言”、“到……时（为止）”、“不迟于……”、“在（白天、夜晚等）的情况下”、“对……”、“由……（雄性动物）所生”等等意思。
A“被”、“由”
　　表示行为者，例如：
The speaker was surrounded by a group of listeners.
America was discovered by Columbus.
Note:
1.by在被动语态中用来表示动作的执行者，这个动作执行者通常指人，有时也可以指物。
2在被动语态句子中，表示行为者用by，表示使用的工具用with。试比较：
The lock was opened by the thieves with skeleton key.
He was killed by a falling tile.
(=A falling tile killed him).
He was killed with a tile.
(=some one killed him with a tile).
3用with表示使用的工具时，暗示了有使用工具的动作执行者，因此我们说“we were driven indoors by the rain”（这阵雨迫使我们躲到屋里），却不能说“We were driven indoors with the rain”，除非认为天神在使用这个自然力。
4.by还可以表示著作者。
“War and Peace”was written by Leo Tolstoy.
The book I read was "David Copperfield", by Charles Dickens.
B“靠”、“用”、“通过”
　　表示方法、手段，例如：
I often ring them up and order what I want by phone.
The various idiomatic uses can only be learned by experience.
Note:
1.by常可用来泛指所采用的交通手段，这时by后面的名词都不带定冠词。例如：
Did you come by train or by car?
He went by air, and sent his heavy boxes by sea.
2一般用by表示交通手段，如by bus, by boat等，但有些交通手段却用on表示。例如：on foot, on horseback, on bicycle。例如：
We shall go there on foot.
3表示交通手段时既可以说by bus/boat/ plane，也可以说on the bus /the boat/ a plane， 这时介词宾语须带冠词。例如：
Do you travel to school by bus (on the bus)?
4.on the bus /the boat /a plane不但可以表示交通手段，也可以表示位置。在表示位置的情况时，on不能与by替用。例如下面句中的on the ship不能改成by the ship。例如：
There were a lot of passengers on the ship.
5在交通工具之前用in时，也表示位置，强调“在……里”（inside），on则是一般地指“在……上”。例如：
Do you ever ride in a bus? What happens to people standing inside when the bus starts up?
I shall have lunch on the train.
6在下面的句子里，on之后虽然有定冠词，我们却可以用by来替代它。就是说，要说明交通工具的具体出发或到达时间时，on, by都可以用。例如：
I shall go on / by the 10:30 train.
7表示书写的方式用in，如in pencil（用铅笔），in ink（用墨水），with表示收发室的工具，如with a pencil, with a pen。工具是具体的东西，因此应加冠词。但讲“用钢笔与墨水”时，又能说“with pen and ink”，不能说“with a pen and ink”。例如：
Write your composition in every other line with pen and ink, but not with a pencil.
C“在……旁边”、“靠近……”
例如：
When I came to myself, I found my friends sitting by my bedside.
On a cold evening it was pleasant to sit by the fire.
Note:
1短语have sth. by one中的by one含有“在手头”、“在身边”的意思。例如：
It's useful to have a good dictionary by you when you're reading.
stand by one含有“支持”、“拥护”的意思。例如：
They all stood by the president.
2.by之后接反身代词时，含有“独自地”（alone）的意思，by之前可以带all以加强语气。例如：
The box is too heavy for me to lift by myself.
He wasn't married; he lived in a little house all by himself.
C“偏……方向”
例如：
The island lies south by east from here.
D“经过……旁边”
例如：
I go by his office every day.
He walked by the shop without realizing it.
Note:
1短语by the way的意思是“顺便问一下”、“顺便提一下”。
By the way, what are you studying?
By the way, don't confuse "already" and "all ready".
2短语by way of的意思是“经由……”。
We came to Oxford by way of Warwick and Banbury.
E“途经……”、“由……”、”“沿……”
例如：
They travelled to India by South Africa.
Did you come by the nearest road?
F“（抓<握>某人或某物）的……（部位）”
例如：
He seized me by the hair/ arm.
The prisoner was chained to a post by the ankle.
Note:
by之后接人或物的某一具体部位时，应带定冠词，例如by the hand。但在短语by hand里的hand被用作抽象名词，这里的by表示行为的方式或手段。例如：
The nurse held the child by the hand.
These things are made by machinery, not by hand.
G“以 …”、“达……”
　　表示数量、程度。例如：
The price has been increased by five pounds.
I know him by sight, but not by name.
Note:
短语by far与比较级、最高级词连用时，起加强语气的作用，含有“……得多”、“最……”等意思。 例如：
It's better by far.
He is by far the cleverest boy in the school.
H“按……”、“以……（为单位）”
　　表示度量、单位，例如：
Milk is sold by the pint, butter by the pound, and eggs by the dozen.
Most workmen are paid by the week.
I“乘……”
　　表示尺度，例如：
The carpet is three yards by three and a half (3 yds×3.5 yds).
They lived in a room 15feet by 20 feet.
Note:
　　在数学上，用by表示“乘数”或“除数”。例如：
5 multiplied by 3 is 15 (5×3=15).
20 divides by 5.
J“每次……地”、“逐一”
　　表示每次量，例如：
Little by little his savings accumulated.
His archers could kill the attackers one by one.
K“由于……”
　　表示原因，例如：
He succeeded by hard work..
I am sorry, I took it by mistake.
L“（名）叫……”
　　表示人名或地名，例如：
A person by the name of Smith wants to see you.
He lived in a small village by the name of Butly.
Note:
　　在短语by the name of里，by的意思相当于having。
M“以……名义发誓”、“对着……起誓”
例如：
He swore by (the name of) God.
I swear by everything I believe in.
Note:
1用by表示“发誓”的短语，有时可以转用来表示“感叹”。例如：
By heaven I'll have.
By heaven this is too much.
2短语to swear by something的意思是“认为……是可靠的（有效的，有名望的）”， 表示对by之后的东西深信无疑。 例如：
Our doctor swears by this mixture as a cure for colds.
N“按照……”、“根据……”
　　表示根据，例如：
By my watch, the time is half past eleven.
By your theory we should have a good summer, since we have had a hard winter.
O“就……而言”
例如：
He is British by birth although he was born in France.
She is very generous by nature.
P“到……时（为止）”、“不迟于……”
　　表示时间，例如：
Please send me the tickets by next week (I want to have the tickets not later than next week).
I hoped that by that time I should be able to run a business.
Note:
　　by表示在某一点的时间（at a point of time）,till(until) 则表示时间延续的终点（the end of a period）。例如：
I shall be here by eight.
I shall be here till eight.
Q“在（白天，夜晚等）的情况下”
例如：
Cats sleep by day and hunt by night.
What is done by night, appears by day.
Note:
　　表示方式、手段的by，可以引申来表示“在……环境（情况）下”，这时 by的意思相当于during，但by着重强调特殊的环境。这种用法，常见的只有by day, by night 这两个短语。例如：
The enemy attacked by night.
Everything was quiet during the night.
R“对”
例如：
I want to do my duty by him.
He did well by her.
S“由……（雄性动物）所生”
例如：
Golden trumpet, by Golden Rain out of Silver Trumpet.
Note:
用by表示动物出生是技术用语，通用于马的繁殖。by之后接雄性动物，out of之后接雌性动物。
　
concerning
　　concerning实际上是动词concern的现在分词，但它在句中所起的作用常相当于介词，意思是“关于”、“至于”、“就……而言"。介词concerning仅用于书面语，不用于口语。例如：
We still have no information concerning his where abouts.
Concerning your letter, I am pleased to inform you that your plans are quite acceptable to us.
　
considering
　　considering原是动词consider的现在分词，但它在某些结构中常起介词的作用，意思是“考虑到”、“就……而言”、“鉴于……"。有时，由于所考虑的是令人意外的事实，构成鲜明的对照，因此还含有“虽然”、“尽管”等“让步”的意思。例如：
It is excusable considering that he has no experience.
She looks young considering her age.
He did poorly in his examinations, considering how hard he had studied for them.
Note:
　　在口语中，considering有时可以不带宾语而单独用在句末，意为“全面地考虑”（all things considered）。这时候的considering 可以理解为副词。例如：
We've not done very badly, considering.
His speech was really quite good, considering.
　
cum
　　cum是拉丁语介词，相当于“with”，含有“附有”、“联合”、“兼”等意思，主要用于构成复合词。例如：
dinner-cum-tea （附有茶点的）小餐
cum dividend附有红利
house-cum-farm（兼有住房的）农场
Note:
1用cum连接两个地名时，表示两个地方联合成一个行政区或教区。
Tudeley-cum-Capel图德莱和卡佩尔联区
Chorton-cum-Hardy乔尔顿和哈帝联合教区
2用cum连接两个建筑物用途的名称时，表示同一个建筑物兼有两用。
a dwelling-cum-workshop住宅兼工厂
a bed-cum-sitting room办公室兼书房
　
despite
　　despite用于表示“让步”的意思，译为“尽管”、“不顾”、“任凭”，相当于in spite of， 但用于比较正式的场合，口语中一般不用。例如：
He came to the meeting despite his serious illness.
Despite all this, he managed to keep his temper.
Note:
1短语in despite of等于despite，但仅用于正式文体。有时还可以将in省略，写成despite of（但有人认为这种用法不算正确）。例如：
They are generous (in) despite of their own economic troubles.
2表示让步的同义介词还有：regardless of等。
　
down
　　down可以表示“（由上）向……下”、“在……下”、“沿着……向下”、“沿着……”、“在……那一端”、“往（在）（市区、市内商业区）”、“向（在）……南部”等意思；表示时间时，含有“自……以来”、“自……以后”等意思。此外，down有时还含有“向……”的意思。
A“（由上）向……下”、“在……下”
例如：
The child fell down the stairs.
His office is down the stairs.
Note:
1.down与up相对应。
2在阅读文件资料时，down表示从本页的开头读到结尾。
例如：
The secretary glanced down the list of names.
I always look down the "Deaths" column in the Times before I start reading the news.
B“沿着……往下”、“沿着……”、“在……那一端”
例如：
The tears ran down her face.
I went down the country lane for a few miles.
He is now in the shop down the road.
Note:
1沿着一条平路走，可以说down the road，也可以说up the road。如果这条道路通过市中心，那么走离市中心一般用down the road，向着市中心用up the road，在普通的路上则以说话者为中心，走离说话者一般用down the road，向着说话者走则用up the road。
2沿着水平方向离开说话者的运动，可以说go down the road，也可以说come down the road。用go或come，取决于讲话者“心目中”的中心，离开“心目中”的中心，用go，向着“心目中”的中心用come。
3从首都、城市、北方、上游、内地往边远地区、农村、南方、下游、海边等用down，反之用up。
C“往（在）……的下游”
例如：
The ship sailed down the river.
They hired a boat and went for a row down the river.
D“（由郊区、城市的住宅区）往（市区、市内商业区）”，“在（市区、市内商业区）”。
例如：
He comes down town every morning.
E“自……以来”、“自……以后”
　　表示时间，例如：
We can't look ahead down the years and know what the future will bring.
F“向……去”
例如：
I'm just going down the shops.
I'm going down the town.
G.down与up连用时（up and down），表示“上下往返”的运动。
例如：
The passenger was walking up and down the station platform.
due to
　　due to是由形容词due和介词to构成的复合介词，意为“因为”、“由于”、“应归于”等，用于表示原因，相当于owing to。例如：
The delay is due to heavy traffic.
The first place was due to Milton.
Note:
1.due原是形容词，因此由due to所组成的介词短语是形容词性的短语，照理只能用作表语或定语，不能用作状语。但现在这个界限已经打破，越来越多的人把due to看成跟owing to,because of一样，可以用作状语。例如：
The event was canceled due to inclement weather.
2在有些句中，有due to代替owing to 作状语时，可能会引起意思上的含糊。例如“Many children were separated from their parents during the disturbance due to the huge crowd that quickly gathered",在这样的句中，人们无法理解到底是“人群引起分离”，还是“人群引起骚动”，如果将due to改成owing to，意思就明确了。
Many children were separated from their parents during the disturbance owing to the huge crowd that quickly gathered.
3下列句中的due to属于形容词性短语，相当于定语从句的省略。
The number and influence of investors are increasing, (which is) due to several causes.
　
during
　　during可以表示“在某一整个时期”，也可以表示“在某一时期中的某个时刻”。
A“在（整个）……期间”
例如：
Sentries guarded the camp during the night.
The doors will remain shut during the concert.
B“在……期间”、“在……的时候”
例如：
I woke up three times during the night.
The fire started during the workmen's dinner.
Note:
1.during与in常可以互相替用，但如果动词表示在某个时期的“状态”或“习惯”时，倾向于用during。例如：
He was in the army during the war.
He was injured in / during the war.
2在stay, visit, meal等名词之前，只能用during表示“在……过程中”。例如：
He took me to the "guest room" at his college where I was to stay during my visit.
During the meal we talked about our plans.
3在季节之前用in,如in summer（不带定冠词），系泛指每年夏季，而during the summer则特指某一年的夏季。试比较：
In summer people go to the seaside.
Where shall you be during the summer?
4我们可以说“To whom did you give the letter?”，也可以说“Who(m) did you give the letter to?”，但during与wh-疑问词连用时，就不能分开。例如我们可以说“During which years was he Prime Minister?”却不能说“which years was he Prime Minister during?”。
　
ere
　　ere与before同义，都是“在……以前”，但before是普通用语，而ere属于古用语，现在主要用于诗歌里。例如：
Ere morning the storm had passed.
He must leave ere long.
Note:
有时ere long（不久），ere now（以前）等可以分别写成erelong, erenow.
　
ex
　　ex是拉丁语介词，为英语所借用，仅限于金融，商业界使用。含有“不、无”、“自从”、“在……交货”等意思。
A“未”、“不”、“无”、“不包括”、“没有”、“无权获得”、“免收……费”。
　　表示否定的意思，例如：
ex divided红利未付
ex coupon不付息票
ex interest无利息
ex rights无权认购新股
ex all无一切利益
ex new无权要求新股
ex ship不收船费
ex store不收栈租
ex elevalor不收起卸费
He was willing to buy the shares ex rights.
他愿意买进这些无权认购新股的股票。
B“从”、“自”、“依据”，例如：
ex animo出自内心的，恳挚的
ex libris藏书者签条
ex officio依据职权的
C“在……交货”，例如：
ex rail铁路旁交货
ex bond关栈交货
The goods will be supplied ex stock or ex factory at the option of the buyer.
从仓库或直接到工厂提货，由买者选择。
Note:
1表示价格时，ex还可由from引申出特殊的含义，例如，一部汽车定价为￡750 ex factory，表示出厂价为750英镑，交货时可能还得附加其他费用。
2美国，ex还可以表示“from the class of”，“of the specified class”，用于表示某一年度入学的学生。如ex'75（七五级的)。比较：(in) the class of 1975(1975届毕业班的，这里指毕业年度)。
　
except
　　except的意思是“除……之外”，即所扣除的“不包括在内”（not including, leaving out）。except之后可接名词、代词、副词、介词短语、动词不定式、名词从句、状语从句等。
A.except之后接名词、代词
例如：
He won't eat anything except the largest, most splendid pieces of meat.
All the pupils attended the meeting except him.
B.except接副词、介词短语、状语从句
例如：
I looked everywhere except there.
I take no orders except from the commander.
He usually goes to work by bike except when it rains.
C.except接动词不定式
例如：
I shan't have to do anything at all except sit back and enjoy the journey.
Nothing remains for us to do except to enjoy the fruits of our labors.
Our housemaid does everything except (to) wash the car.
Note:
　　except之后的不定式可以带to，也可以不带to。如果except之前的那个动词带有助动词，那么except之后的这个不定式的to可以省略，因为这个时候，我们可以模糊地感觉到这个不定式前似乎也有个助动词。例如：
The children found there was nothing they could do with their money, except spend it on(upon) sweets.
D.except接名词从句
例如：
I received nothing except what was due to me.
I know nothing about him except that he lives next door.
Note:
1.except之后接名词从句时，有时转义为“只是”，即在说明基本情况后，对某个具体的细节问题加以修正。例如：
He is a very good student except that he is occasionally careless.
He is very clever except that he is a poor speaker.
2复合介词except for也用来“在说明基本情况后，对某些细节加以修正”，相当于“except for the fact that……“。
3表示“除……之外”的介词还有“excepting, but,bar,save,beyond,apart from等。
　
except for
　　except for是复合介词，含有“除……之外”、“只是”、“若非”等意思。
A“除……之外”（except）
例如：
He answered all the questions except for (=except) the last one.
You can all go except for (=except ) George.
Note:
1表示“除……之外”时，except for 与except常可以换用。
2在句首只能用except for，不能用except，例如在“Except for George, you can all go”这种句子中，我们不能用except代替except for。
B“只是”
例如：
This essay is good except for the careless mistakes.
We had no trouble on the journey except for a flat tyre.
Note:
　　except for主要用于“在说明基本情况后，对某个具体的细节问题加以修正”。这时候不能用except来代替except for。这里的except for含有“on condition that you ignore the following fact”的意思。试比较：
There is not one in the streets, except the policeman on(upon) duty.
The streets were deserted, except for the policeman on duty.
C“若非”，“要不是”
例如：
She could leave her husband except for the children.
Except for you, I should be dead by now.
Note:
复合介词except for可以代替复合介词but for，表示“要不是”这样相当于虚拟条件句的否定意思。但but for却不能表示“除……之外”，“只是”等意思。
　
excepting
　　excepting用作介词时，意思相当于except（“除……之外”）。但excepting通常只用于句首，或用于not, without, always等词之后。
A.excepting用于句首，例如：
Excepting this one, they are all right.
B用于not, without等否定词之后，例如：
Everyone, not excepting the pilot, survived the crash.
Note:
　　当excepting与not 或without连用时，不能被except代替。例如：
Everybody must observe the rule except the principal.
Everybody must observe the rule not excepting the principal.
C用于always 之后（这里的always 只起加强语气的作用），例如：
Everyone was tired, always excepting John (=John was not tired).
　
excluding
　　excluding实际上是动词exclude的现在分词，但它往往起着介词的作用，因此，在现代英语里，已经被确认为介词之一，意思是“不计算……在内”。例如：
There were thirty people in the hotel, excluding the hotel workers.
Note:
1.excluding的意思相当于exclusive of, 与including或inclusive of相对应。试比较：
The ship has a crew of 57, excluding (exclusive of ) officers.
We were 57 persons, including (inclusive of) children.
2.besides的意思是“除……之外（还有）”，表示“附加”，而excluding则表示“扣除”，即“不计算……在内（有）”。试比较：
There were thirty others present besides me.
There were thirty present excluding myself.
3.except和excluding虽然都表示“扣除”，都含有“not including”的意思，但except说明的是“例外”的情况，excluding却表示一种“计算”方式。试比较：
They were all present except the captain.
There were fifty-two people present, excluding the officials.
　
following
　　following是动词follow的现在分词，但在句中常起着介词的作用，因此在现代英语里也算是介词，意思是“紧接……之后”，用于表示时间上的先后。表示时间上“在……以后”时，通常用after，但强调两者之间在时间上和内容上的紧密关系时，宜用following。following所联系的两者都是某种安排的组成部分。例如：
Following the speech, there will be a few minutes for questions.
Following the lecture the meeting was open to discussion.
　
for
　　for 可以表示如下意思：“往……（目的地）”，“向……”、“为了……”、“（希望）……”、“给……（接受者）”、“替……（服务）”、“（有益、有损）于……”，“（适合，不适合）于……”，“对于……”、“赞成（拥护）……”、“因为（由于）……”、“结果……”、“虽然（尽管）……”、“代表（代替）……”，“换……”，“作为……的代价”，“以……为价钱”、“作为（当作）……”、“每……”、“就……而言”，“至于……”、“（时间、距离）计……”，“第……（次）”、“在……时候”、“（时间约定）在……”。此外，for还和不定式连用，主要引导逻辑主语。
A“往……”、“到……”
　　表示目的地，例如：
Tomorrow Jan and Fried leave for Switzerland.
The ship sails for the Far East.
B“向……”
　　表示方向，例如：
It's getting on for 2 o'clock.
His illness took a turn for the better.
C“为了……”
　　表示目的，例如：
He plays the piano for pleasure.
He had an operation for a heart disease.
Note:
1由for组成的表示目的的短语介词有for the purpose of, for the sake of, for the benefit of, for the good of等。例如：
For what purpose do you want it?
For my sake, please be careful on the expedition.
The money is to be used for the benefit of the poor.
2表示目的的for常可以改写成in order to的形式。例如：
I am going into this shop for (in order to buy) some cigarettes.
He applied for (in order to gain) a post in Camford University.
D“（希望）……”
　　表示希望，愿望的内容，例如：
His hopes for success in the election were soon shattered.
How I long for a sight for my home.
Note:
在口语中，for用在感叹词oh之后，表示某种的愿望。例如：
Oh for the days of our youth.
E“给……”
　　表示接受者，例如：
Whom is that letter for? Is it for me?
I gave her the flowers that I had brought for her.
Note:
1有些动词之后带有直接宾语和间接宾语，例如“They gave him the first prize”这里的him是间接宾语（通常是指人），the first prize是直接宾语。这样的间接宾语相当于由介词to或者for所引导的介词短语，to表示实际的接受者，for表示预定的接受者。这里的for常有“替”的意思。例如：
They gave the first prize to him.
I bought a new hat for her. (=I bought her a new hat.)
2在“He bought a new fan for the office”这样的句子中，for之后的“预定接受者”不是人或动物，不能把此句改成“He bought the office a new fan”。
F“为……”、“替……”
　　表示“服务”的对象，例如：
They gave their lives for their country.
What can I do for you?
G“（有益、有损）于……”、“（适合、不适合）于……”
　　表示有利或不利于某一对象，例如：
I am telling you this for your good.
This holiday has been good for you.
The place is fit for a walk.
H“对于……”
　　表示行为或感情的对象，例如：
He has a taste for art.
For some time now I have realized that the friendship and affection I have always had for her had turned to love.
We felt deeply sorry for him in his great trouble.
Note:
　　在表示“关怀”、“悲怨”、“懊悔”等词之后皆可接for，说明流露感情的对象，如concern for, anxious for, solicitude for, fear for, grieve for, mourn for, regard for等。表示“为某事而悲愁（懊悔、关切）”时，则可以用at或over, at说明刺激感情的“因素”，over表示感情的“关联”。试比较：
She grieved for her wayward son.
She grieved at her son's waywardness.
She is mourning for her son.
She is mourning over her son's death.
I“赞成……”、“拥护……”
例如：
I am all for anything that will make our work easier.
I'll vote for Labour.
Note:
　　表示赞成的for与against相对应。 例如：
How many are for the motion? How many are against it?
J“因为……”、“由于……”
例如：
He went away for fear of disturbing you.
Note:
1当for表示“烦恼”、“困苦”的原因时，常相当于for want of。例如：
I am rather pressed for time today.
I found him to be distressed for a little money.
2.for 有时还含有“为了纪念……”的意思，例如：
This rare plant was named for a former governor of India.
3.for在虚拟条件句if it were not for中表示“若不是因为……”，例如：
If it were not for the generosity of his father he would be pretty badly off.
4.for表示原因时，主要见于否定句或半否定句。在肯定句中，通常表示“奖赏”或“处罚”的原因。 例如：
I shall reward him for his service.
The motorist was fined for careless driving.
5.for可以表示心理上的原因或动机，主要见于某些短语。如：for fear/love/want (of) /joy/sorrow等。例如：
He trembled for fear.
He danced for joy.
K“结果……”
　　表示结果，例如：
We have had all our trouble for nothing.
He is always looking for something for nothing.
L“虽然……”、“尽管……”
　　表示让步，例如：
For all his efforts, he didn't succeed.
Note:
　　表示“让步”时，通常见于“for all…”的结构，但在下面的句子里，for也含有让步的意思。例如：
I love him none the less for his faults.
M“代……”、“替……”、“代替……”
　　表示代表、代替，例如：
I can only give my opinion, I cannot speak for others.
我只能给出我的看法，我不能代表别人的意见。
"Do" is a substitute for practically any verb of action.
“Do”这个词实际上可以代替任何行为动词。
N“换……”
　　表示交换，例如：
I don't like the book that I borrowed; will you please change it for another one?
I'll give my watch for your camera.
Note:
　　an eye for an eye（以眼还眼），a tooth for a tooth（以牙还牙）， word for word（逐字）等短语里的for也可以算是“交换”的引申。例如：
When you are translating from one language into another, you can not translate word for word.
O“以……价钱”
例如：
I paid ￡ 50 for the picture.
He will buy it for ￡ 20,000。
They are asking ￡ 3,800, but we might get it for ￡ 3,600 or￡ 3,650.
Note:
1用for表示“以……价钱”、“作为……的代价”时，都是表示“交换”的引申。表示“以……价钱”时，for 之后接的是具体价钱，表示“作为……的代价”时，for之后接的是具体的事物。试比较：
I gave a hundred dollars for my bicycle.
Will you sell it to me for one hundred dollar?
2在下列句中的for表示票面的价值。
She wrote a cheque for ￡ 20.
3注意用at和for表示“价格”时的区别：
I bought it for ￡ 5.(that was what I paid)
I bought it at ￡5.(that was what it cost then, but it may be different now.)
P“当作……”，“作为……”
例如：
How do you know him for a spy?
Note:
1.for之后可以接形容词说明某种状态，这时的for 等于as being。
I shall be there for certain (as being certain).
All his friends gave him up for dead.
2.for example, for instance, for one等短语里的for也含有“作为……”的意思。
Many great men have risen from poverty, Lincoln and Edison, for example.
I, for one, shall vote against the proposal.
Q“每……”、“与……相比”
　　表示比率，比例。例如：
For every mistake you make you'll lose half a mark.
They have built one house for every two they have demolished.
Note:
　　for表示比率、比例时，其后通常接each,every或某些数词，有时接“every+数词”。
R“就……而言”、“至于……”
例如：
William is big for his age.
It is very warm for October.
Note:
1..As for常被用作复合介词表示“至于”、“就……而言”。
As for myself, I am satisfied　　就我而言，我已经满意了。
2在enough, too, rather之后用for时，分别含有“肯定”、“否定”或“怀疑”的意义。例如：
The book is difficult enough for students.
I thought you were too old for dolls, Margaret.
The footprints are rather large for a boy of 12.
S“计……”
　　表示时间、距离的长度
1）表示时间的长度。
例如：
We will leave our car here for the time being, and then find somewhere to park it for the night.
I shall be here for at least another year.
Note:
1表示时间长度的for常常可以省略，特别是紧接在动词之后时，例如：
We stayed (for) three days.
I have been there (for) a week.
2.for之后接有all时，for必须省略。例如：
I was there all the time.
3当for用于句首或用在否定词之后时，一般不能省略。例如：
For a year we waited to hear what had happened to him.
I haven't seen him for several years.
2）表示距离的长度。
例如：
For miles and miles you see nothing but trees.
We walked (for) a short distance and parted.
T“第……（次）”
表示次第, for+序数词+time，例如：
He failed for the third time.
For the last time, will you please take that boy out of the shop?
Note:
　　短语for once含有“就这么一次”、“第一次”等意思，主要用于口语，常带有讽刺的意味。例如：
For once he was telling the truth.
U“在……时候”
例如：
Many people no longer wear black for funerals.
The churches all hold special services for Christmas and Easter.
V“在……”
　　表示约定的时间，例如：
In this case it is necessary to cancel the party planned for February 12th.
The meeting is arranged for seven o'clock.
　　表示不定式的逻辑主语，例如：
For the son of a working man to go to university (that the son of a working man should go to university) was something undreamed of in those days（主语）
In country districts it was no uncommon thing for children to walk two or three miles to school each day (=if children should walk two or three miles to school each day)（同位语）
It must be nearly the time for the match to start（定语）
In that company you had only to mention football for a heated argument to start（状语）
Note:
在下列句中，for不但引导了不定式的逻辑主语，还含有“义务”、“权力”的意思。例如：
It is for you to make a move.
Whether he shall be allowed to return to school is for the headmaster to decide.
　
from
　　from可以表示地点、时间、数字、次序、地位等的“起始点”，还可以表示“观察点”、“来源”、“原料”、“情况的转变”、“根据”、“原因、动机”、“脱离、离开”、“免除、阻止”、“区别、对照”等。
A“从……”
　　表示出发地点，例如：
We set sail from Liverpool.
The musicians travelled from London to .
Note:
1下列句中的from引申表示某种动作发生的起点。
She took fever but continued her work from her bed.
The sun came out from behind the cloud.
2.from还可以引申表示“依附”点。例如：
A lamp hung from the ceiling.
3在下列“from…to…”词组中的from表示某种范围的起点，to表示终点。注意在起点、终点的名词之前都不带冠词。
He read that book from beginning to end in an hour.
The town is being searched from top to bottom.
4“From house to house”,“from place to place”这类短语含有“from one to the next”、“through a whole series”的意思。例如：
They went from house to house making inquiries, but could find no trace of the lost dog.
B“从……”、“从……开始”
　　表示时间的起点（showing a starting point in time），例如：
From Sunday next railway fares will be increased by five percent.
The bank will be open from eight o'clock .
Note:
1表示时间时，“from…to…”常可以写成“from……till”。 例如：
From early morning to /till(until) late at night she never rested.
We stayed there from June to / till September.
2.from May to November的意思是“from May up to end including November”为了清楚表明这一点，可以采用美国的表达方法：from May through November。用up to时，to之后的时间单位就不包括在内。试比较：
We worked up to Christmas (i.e. but not over Christmas).
We worked there from June through December.
3注意from time to time, from day to day等短语的意思。
You have given us from time to time (now and then) a "rule" of grammar.
He grew thinner from day to day.
C“从……”、“从……起”
　　表示数字、次序、地位等的起点，例如：
Count from 1 to 20.
D“从……”
　　表示观察点，例如：
He was looking at me from over the top of a newspaper.
From where we stood we got an excellent view of the procession.
Note:
　　表示“观察点”时，from之后不一定接“实际的地点”，有时可以接抽象的“观点”、“地位”等。例如：
From the workmen's point of view the proposals are unacceptable.
From his position on(upon) the board, he is able to glean a good deal of information.
E“从……来”、“从……”
　　表示来源，例如：
Many English words are derived from Latin.
He gets his good looks from his mother.
Note:
1.from表示来源。to与from相对，表示目标。试比较：
I borrowed the book from Bill.
Bill lent the book to me.
2.of也可以表示来源，但主要用于表示“出身”及一些固定的词组。试比较：
Where do you come from?
He comes of a good family.
I hear from her every week.
I hadn't heard of her husband's death when I met her.
3在一些句中，from与of可以互相替用。例如：
He borrowed some money from / of a friend.
You must not expect too much of / from me.
F“用……（制造的）”
　　表示原料，例如：
Beer is brewed from barley.
Coke is formed from coal in exactly the same way as the charcoal is made from wood.
Note:
1当制成品失去了原材料的性质，即发生化学变化时，用from表示“原料”。如果制成品仍保留着原材料的性质，从材料到成品之间只发生物理变化，则用of表示“材料”。试比较：
Steel is made from iron.
The bridge is made of steel.
2在不能断定材料与成品之间的变化到底是物理或化学变化时，用from或of均可。例如：
Bread is made of /from flour, water and yeast.
What is butter made of /from?
3当我们把“材料”作为句子的主语时，则用into表示“制成品”，这里的into表示变化的“结果”。试比较：
Wine is made from grapes.
Grapes is made into wine.
4在be made with中，with表示制成品的一种成分，在be made (out) of中, out of 或of表示整个成品的材料。试比较：
A fruit cake is made with fruit.
A glass jug is made (out) of glass.
G“由……（变成）……”
　　表示情况的转变，例如：
The wind has changed from north to east.
Her behaviour is from bad to worse.
H“从……”、“根据……（判断）”
　　表示根据，例如：
I thought from his accent that he must be a foreigner.
He repeated that speech, word by word from memory.
Note:
　　from表示某种推论的根据、凭据，即进行推理、判断的基础，而by则表示行为（或推论）的方式、手段。试比较：
When I advise you to do this I am speaking from experience（from表示结论的根据）
We can learn wisdom by experience（by表示行为的方式）
They extract experience from practice（from表示来源）
I“由于……”、“出于……”
　　表示原因、动机，例如：
Some of the men suffered from snow blindness.
I felt fatigued from having sat up all night.
Note:
1表示心理上的原因（动机）时，通常用out of 或from，有时也用for，但主要见于某些短语。
2.because of, on account of只能表示非心理性的原因，不能表示动机。on account of比because of 正规，主要用于正式文体。例如：
Because of the drought, the price of bread was high that year.
Richard and Ben, on account of their wide experience in climbing, were voted leaders of the expedition.
J“离开……”、“与……（隔离）”
　　表示脱离、离开，例如：
I am going away from home for about three weeks.
The American houses have no fences separating them from the pavement or from each other.
Note:
1.from表示“分离”，与表示“依附”的to相对应。
例如：
We must not depart from our original plan.
We must adhere to (stick to) the original plan.
2表示“分离”、“离开”时，from之前常加用副词away以加强语气。
例如：
Take the knife (away) from the baby.
3下列句中的apart是副词，from表示“分离”，但在许多情况下，apart from的作用相当于一个复合介词，意思是“除……之外”。例如：
It's hard for a child to be kept apart from its mother.
4.from有时由“离开”引申表示“避开”、“隐蔽”、“不在”、“缺席”等意思。例如：
She can keep nothing from her friends.
He called to see me during my absence from here.
K表示免除、阻止等
例如：
The officer was dismissed from the service for neglect of duty.
Nothing can hinder me from doing my duty.
L表示区别、对照
例如：
I don't trouble much about clothes and I couldn't tell nylon from cotton, or a well-cut suit from a badly cut one.
You must be able to distinguish right from wrong.
　
granted
　　动词grant含有“（姑且）承认”的意思。它的过去分词（granted）在句中所起的作用很象介词，实际上已经被当作介词来使用，在争辩的时候，用于对“未经证明的事实”等表示“姑且的让步”，可以译为“姑且认为”、“假定”、“就算”、“即使”。例如：
Granted that he has enough money to buy the house, it doesn't mean he's going to do so.
Granted that he was drunk, that is no excuse for his conduct.
Note:
　　Grant的现在分词（granting）也可以表达同样的意思。例如：
Granting that your statement is true, how does it answer my question?
　
in
　　in可表示“在……里面”、“朝……（方向）”、“通过……向里”、“进入到……中”、“处在……（状态）中”。“由于……”、“为了……”、“在……（范围）内”、“在……方面”、“在……（内容）里”、“在……（素质）里”、“在……任职（服务）”、“从事于……”、“经营……”、“穿着（戴着）……”、“以……方式”、“达到……（程度、数量）”、“每……”、“用……（质料）”、“以……（颜色）”、“用……作为述意的媒介”、“在……期间”、“在……以后”等。
A“在……里”
　　表示地点、场所、部位，例如：
He lived in a pretty cottage in England.
I have a pain in my chest, sometimes, when I breathe.
Note:
1.in表示在某一平面或立体空间范围内，on(upon)表示在某一平面之上（有接触）。试比较：
There was a nail in the wall.
There were smears of blood on the wall.
2与地名连用时，一般来说at之后接小的地方（如村、镇等），in之后接大的地方（如市、县、省、国家、州等）或者说话的人认为重要的地方。
3在下列句中，in含有“抓着”、“含着”等意思。
I took my last bit of courage in both hands.
He had a cigarette in his mouth.
4在海里或海域里用in，在海上用on(upon)。试比较：
They went swimming in the sea.
A liner was sailing on the sea.
5在表示“在街上”时，英国用in the street，美国则用on the street。试比较：
He is trying to buy a cafe in / on(upon) the High street.
Houses stood on the street（这里的on表示与“线”的接触）
6.in bed指“卧床”、“躺在床上”，on the bed泛指“在床上”，身上不盖东西，试比较：
He was lying in bed.
He was sitting on the bed.
7坐在扶手椅上用in the armchair，坐在没有扶手的椅上则用on(upon) the chair, in the desk属于类似的用法。
We sat down in armchairs in front of the fire.
I sat on a chair without arms.
8.in front of 的意思是“在……前面”，in the front of则是“在……前部”。试比较：
There is a smooth lawn in front of the house with beds of roses in it.
Those are sleeping-cars in the front of the train.
B“朝……（方向）”
　　表示方向，例如：
The aircraft was flying in a northerly direction.
They were running in every direction.
C“通过……向里”
　　口语用法，例如：
He came in the door.
He is going in the front door of the building.
D“进入到……中”
例如：
She put the money in her purse.
He threw her letter in the fire.
Note:
　　表示由外向里的动作时，in和into常可以互相替用，注意下列句子里into和in的区别。
Run into the house.
Don't run (when you are) in the house.
E“（处）在……中”
　　表示环境、状态，例如：
I can see the frosty light of stars in the dark blue sky.
You could smell the spring in the air.
You should consider yourself in luck to get such an opportunity.
The house is in good state of repair.
Note:
1.in之后可以加定冠词，再加某些形容词，表示状态，这时候这个形容词实际已经名词化了。例如：
In these pictures we could see life in the raw among those wild tribes.
They swam in the nude (=in the raw).
2.in之后加冠词，再加名词化的动词，也表示状态。例如：
He is in the know.
F“由于……”、“为了……”
　　表示原因，例如：
The child screamed in terror.
I'm in a slightly awkward position, in that he's not arriving until the 10th.
G“在……之内”，“在……方面”
　　表示领域，范围，例如：
They are like each other in some respects.
It is not in (within) my power to do what you ask.
H注意下列由in引导的短语的意思。
There wasn't a house of any sort in (=within) sight.
周围什麽房子也看不到。
Always do what is right in your own sight.
只要你认为是对的，你就要去做。
The sun was shining in our faces.
太阳正照耀在我们脸上。
They will laugh at you in your face.
他们会当面笑你。
Have you any man in mind?
你有意中人吗？
In my mind (=in my opinion) he is a rare scholar.
我认为他是个难得的学者。
I still have some money in hand.
我手头还有些钱。
I have left the matter in my lawyer's hands.
我已把这件事交由我的律师处理。
She carried a child in her arms.
她手里抱着个孩子。
The people are up in arms.
人民揭竿而起。
I“在 …里”
　　表示文字或口头谈话的内容，例如：
"What's on at the pictures?" "It should be in the local paper."
　　表示人的性格和品质，例如：
He has in him the makings of a great man.
She has very much of the diplomatists in her.
Note:
1.in有时也表示“某物的品质”。
There is no flavour in these apples.
2有时在in所引起的短语之后再加不定式短语（句子的真正主语）。
It is in God to command success.
It is not in gold to be oxidized.
3.in itself表示事物的固有性质，相当于in its own nature.
The dictionary is a library in itself.
A thing good in itself may become bad by its use.
J“在……任职（服务）”
　　表示成员，例如：
His eldest son is in the army.
He holds quite an important post in the civil service.
Note:
　　表示某个组织机构的成员时，有时用on，例如：in the parliament / cabinet, on the Town Council / a committee / a board of director。
K“从事于……”、“经营……”
　　表示活动、职业，例如：
She had one uncle in the medical profession and another in the church.
He is no longer in business / in politics / in the law.
Note:
1.in之后接具体的物品时，表示经营该物品。例如：
He's in plastics / furniture.
2.in与某些单位名称（如hospital, school等）连用时，表示接受该机构的服务。比较下面两组句子:
Goerge is in hospital with a broken leg.
Goerge works at the hospital.
The boy is still in school.
We shall keep him at school for another year.
L“穿着……”、“戴着……”
　　表示穿着，例如：
While we were talking, a scholarly-looking man in cap walked past and smiled at John.
M“以……”、“按照……”
　　表示行为的方式，例如：
He looked at me in a puzzled way.
The house is built in modern style.
Note:
1我们可以说cut in two，也可以说cut into two。这里的in表示行为的方式，into表示行为的结果。但in之后只能接单数名词，into之后必须接复数名词。例如：
He cut the cake in two (=into two).
Cut it into two exact halves.
2用in引导的方式状语、常相当于一个表示方式的副词。例如：
He doesn't like to speak in public.
3.in之后可以接某些形容词，表示行为方式、形式。例如：
In brief, he was so furious that he killed his friend.
Write it out in full.
N“以……”，“在……上”
　　表示程度、数量，例如：
He has been generous in the extreme.
They were slain in great number.
O“每……”
　　表示比例关系，例如：
There are 8 pints in a gallon.
P“以……”、“用……”
　　表示原料、颜色，例如：
We have furniture of this design in oak.
I like the pattern, but I should prefer it in green instead of red.
Q“用……”
　　表示所用的表达方法、媒介、手段、物质等，例如：
Can you write in German?
Do this exercise in ink.
Note:
in与with的详细区别见with条。
R“在……期间”
　　表示时间，例如：
I have never been to a doctor in my life.
Note:
1表示一段时间用in，但若表示在某一天的一段时间则要用on。（详见at条）试比较：
I will come to your help in the morning.
We arranged to meet on the morning of June 20th.
2注意下列“in the night on …”以及“on the night of …”的用法。
I returned at 11 o'clock in the night on the 8th (=on the night of the 8th).
3.in之后接动名词短语时，常含有when的意思，有时相当于一个时间状语从句，有时相当于一个定语从句。例如：
In saying this (=When I say this), I do not imply any criticism of your conduct of the affair.
My purpose in writing to you (=for which I am writing to you) is to ask your advice.
4.in之后接动名词时，可以表示完成某一行为所需要的时间。例如：
Good things are a long time in coming.
The temple was forty-six years in building.
S“在……以后”
　　表示时间，例如：
She will meet us in two hours.
He said he would come again in a few days.
Note:
1.in表示从“现在”开始，“经过若干时间以后”，因此动词必须用将来时态。如果动词是过去时态，则用after短语修饰。试比较：
He will be here in a few days.
He died after a few days.
2表示“从现在开始，经过若干时间后”时，常在时间长度之后加“time”，以加强语气。例如：
I cannot see you now; come back in an hour's time.
3.in可以表示“在……期间”，也可以表示“在……以后”，试比较：
He will come several times in a year.
He will come again in a year.
4.in表示“在……期间”时，可以指“在整个……期间”，也可以指“在…期间以内”。当in指“在……期间以内”时，可以与within替用，但within比in正规。例如：
Do you think you shall see him within / in the next three days.
　
in addition to
　　in addition to是复合介词，意思是“除……之外（还）”，相当于as well as。
We saw a Mickey Mouse cartoon in addition to the cowboy movie.
Note:
　　在下面句中的in addition to并不是复合介词，它的意思是“对……的补充”。
This is in addition to what I wrote yesterday.
　
including
　　including原是动词include的现在分词，在句中常起着介词的作用，因此已逐渐被确认为介词，意思是“其中包括”、“连……也算在内”，“包括……在内”。例如：
A new car of this kind costs ￡700, including purchase tax.
Note:
1.including所构成的介词短语常置于句末，并有逗号与句子其他部分隔开。但有时也可以置于句首或句中，同样也用逗号与句子的其他部分断开。例如：
Including the children, there were twelve people in the house.
Six people, including three women, were invited.
2.including之前加not，表示“假设性的否定”，相当于excluding，其含义是“如果不把……算在内的话”。例如：
There were ten present, not including myself.
　
inside
　　inside含有“在……里面”、“不超过……”等意思, 与outside相对，用于指空间位置。例如：
He has never been inside the church.
Note:
1表示“不超过”这个概念时，within比inside更常用。
Within / Inside a year, all was changed.
2.inside of是复合介词，意思等于inside，但一般用于非正式场合。在美国英语里inside of则被用来代替inside。例如：
He answered inside (of ) an hour.
　
instead of
　　instead of是复合介词，其中instead是副词。instead of有时可以写成in stead of， 但instead of更常用。instead of的意思是“代替”、“而不（是）”。
A“代替”
例如：
Will you go to the party instead of me, so that our host won't be too offended?
You may use milk instead of cream in this soup.
B“而不（是）”
例如：
Instead of improving the patient is getting worse.
Note:
1当instead of的意思是“代替”时，可以与in place of替用，但如果instead of用作“而不是”时，一般不能与in place of 替用。试比较：
The Vice-president talked at the meeting in place of (=instead of) the president, because the president was sick.
He sent his helper instead of coming himself.
2当instead of的意思是“代替”时，它后面所接的是名词或代词。如果instead of的意思为“而不（是）”时，它不但可以接名词、代词，还可以接介词短语、动名词等。instead of 这时带有明显的否定意义，因此译成汉语时常用“（是）……而不（是）……”或“不（是）……而（是）……”的结构。
He came by plane instead of by train（介词短语）
If he had gone on instead of going back the whole course of English history might have been changed（动名词）
　
into
A“到……中去”、“到……里面”
　　into表示to和in两者意义上的结合，即既表示动作的方向又表示后来进入某一地点，可译为“到……中去”、“到……里面”等。例如：
He walked into the room in which we were sitting.
I rang the bell and was shown into the waiting room.
Note:
1.in与表示“运动”或“动作”的动词连用时，常强调动作结束后的状态，而into则强调整个动作的过程。试比较：
He put the money in his pocket.
He put the money into his pocket.
2.into虽然表示to和in两者意思上的结合，但in和to作为两个词就不一定如此。试比较下面例句中into和in to的不同含义。
He came into the room with his wife and they went in to dinner together.
B“朝……内”、“向……内”
　　into还表示towards和in两者意义上的结合，译为“朝……内”、“向……内”。 例如：
He saw a figure hanging over the parapet and gazing into the river.
C“进入……状态”
　　into可以由“进入……里面”引申为或其他种种比喻用法。例如：
He is always getting into trouble.
She burst into tears.
D“进到……”
　　into用于表示时间的意义时，含有“进到……”的意思，并常与far, late之类含有强调意义的词连用，表示时间的持久。例如：
They sat talking far into the night.
It lasted well into this century.
Note:
　　在into之前有时可以加具体的时间，表示延续的长度。例如：
We're 8 minutes into the second half.
E表示转化的结果
例如：
He changed his Swiss francs into English pounds.
She developed into a beautiful woman.
F.into之后接数词
　　into之后可以接数词，表示被除数，除数则置于into之前。例如：
4 into 16 is 4.
Note:
　　into偶尔还有“乘”的意思。例如：
7 into 9 is 63.
G“对……感兴趣”
　　into在俚语里含有“对……感兴趣”，相当于keen on。例如：
He's given up photography now and he's into religion and modern music.
　
less
　　less一般用作形容词、副词或名词，意思是“更（较）少”、“更（较）小”。但当less用作介词时，则含有“但其中要扣除（减去）……”的意思。例如：
She gave me 100 pounds, less 5 pounds for her own costs (=she gave me 95 pounds).
Note:
1介词less有时可以译为“还差……”。
The time cycle is a year less three days.
2与介词less相对应的反义词是plus而不是more。请比较：
Eight hundred pounds, less commission at 2.5 percent.
Fifteen pounds a week plus commission.
　
like
　　like原来不是介词，而是形容词或副词，后接to或as，意思是“象……一样”、“如……一样”，如：
The structure of the language (Turkish) is very like to the Latin.
但现在to和as都省略了，因此like实际上已经起着介词的作用。具体用法如下：
A“象……”
　　表示“方式”、“方法”的相同、相似（与行为动词连用）。例如：
I can't speak Spanish like you (= as you do).
He roared like a bull.
B“如……”
　　表示“程度”的相同、相似（与形容词或副词连用）。例如：
He is brave like anything.
Note:
1在上述两种用法里，常可以看到like anything, like hell之类的固定词组，用于加强语气，含有very violently, very much so等意思，即“猛烈地”、“使劲地”等，主要用于口语。例如：
He ran like hell(=very fast).
He was working like the devil (=very hard).
2因为like含有“象”的意义，所以常用来比喻某种行为，形成了一些固定的成语。例如：
He drinks like a fish.
He smoked like a chimney.
3介词like之后一般是接名词或代词，但在口语中有时可以接形容词，如like crazy（发狂似地）。例如：
They dashed down the road like mad (= as though they were mad).
When the car's painted it will look like new.
C“象……一样”
　　表示“性质、性格、形状、外貌”的相同、相似（与系动词连用）。例如：
The child is like its mother in looks.
There is no place like home.
Note:
1有些不定代词与like连用，含有特定的意思。如nobody /nothing like（没有可以比得上……的人<或物>）；something like（大约、将近；好极、妙极）。例如：
There is nothing like trying (=as good as trying).
It'll cost something like (=about) ￡ 100.
Three months' holiday a year? That's something like (=excellent)!
2. Like用于表示比喻，as则表示“以……身分（资格）”。请比较：
Let me speak to you as a father (=I am your father and I am speaking in that character).
Let me speak to you like a father (=I am not your father but I am speaking in the way your father might).
3.like之后的接一代词时，常表示“某人的特征”，相当于typical of。例如：
It was just like him to say that.
4有时like被用作for example的意思，但这种用法不算正规。例如：
There are several people interested, like Mr Johns and Dr Simpson.
5.look like含义视后接宾语的性质而有所差异：
(a) （后接名词）看起来象：
It looks like gold.
(b)（后接动名词或可作动词用的名词）看来好象要，看来好象会：
It looks like rain (=as if it will rain).
The weather does not look like clearing up (=as if it will clear up).
　
minus
A“减去”
　　minus主要用于数学，意思是“减去”。例如：
Seven minus four equals (or is, gives) three.
15 minus 10 leaves 5。
Note:
　　在数学上表示“加”、“减”分别用plus和minus。但如果plus的意思是“增加”、“外加”时，它的反义词是less而不是minus。
B“失去”
　　在口语中，有时用minus 来表示“没有”“失去”等意思。这种用法常含有“幽默”或“玩笑”的意味。例如：
He won the fight, but when it ended he was minus 2 front teeth.
I found the purse minus its contents.
　
near
　　near的意思是“在……附近”、“靠近……”。
A表示“空间”的“接近”、“靠近”
例如：
The only disadvantage is that there is no railway or road near it.
The small child was afraid to go near the dog.
Note:
　　在否定句中near与地点名词连用，常含有“没有去”，“没有走近”的意思。
She has not been near her family for over a year (=She has not visited her family for over a year).
He never goes near a public house (=He never enters one).
B表示时间的“接近”、“靠近”
例如：
It must be getting near dinner-time.
C表示其它种种状态的“接近”、“靠近”
例如：
You are getting near the end of your year's work now.
Hope comes near fulfillment.
D表示“性质”的“接近”、“靠近”
　　在这种用法里，near通常与不定副词连用，near之后所接的是形容词（或过去分词）（这里的near也可以理解为副词）。例如：
Your answer is somewhere near right.
The work is nowhere near finished yet.
E表示“靠近”某种行为
　　在这种用法里，常用come（或go）near的结构，near之后接动名词（短语），含有“几乎”、“差一点儿”的意思。例如：
This act came near spoiling his chance.
I came near being (=was nearly) drowned in attempting to cross the river.
Note:
　　near原是形容词或副词，要表示“接近”、“靠近”某事物时，须与介词to连用，后来to省略，near也就承担了介词的作用。但介词near毕竟还保留有形容词或副词的特征，它可以有比较级nearer和最高级nearest（如Call me again nearer <to> the time of the meeting），甚至还可以为另一个副词（如very, quite等）所修饰。在现代英语里，near之后接to与否，并无定则。一般倾向于：
1当near表示空间位置时，不接to。如：
Mr Thompson lives near me.
2当near表示时间位置时，接to。如：
It's getting near to Easter.
3当near表示趋近过程时，接to。如：
As we got near to London the traffic grew denser.
　
nearby
　　nearby这个词本来只用作形容词或副词，但在现代英语里，已经开始被用作介词，意思相当于near by。例如：
They built a pumping station nearby the bridge.
　
　
next to
　　next to可以表示“紧接在……的旁边”、“紧接着……”、“仅次于……”的意思。
A“紧接在……的旁边”
　　next to是复合介词，意思是“紧接在……的旁边”，用于指“空间”位置。例如：
Henry is sitting near the window, next to John.
He is in the shop next to the corner.
B“紧接着”、“仅次于”
　　next to可以引申表示“顺序”、“程度”等的连接。例如：
The Lord Mayer came first, and next to him the Town Clerk.
Which is the town next to London in size?
C“讨好某人”
　　短语 next to somebody含有“接近某人”，“讨好某人”的意思，但这种用法不算正规。例如：
I could never get next to him to find out what he was really like.
She concentrates on getting next to the rich people.
　
notwithstanding
　　notwithstanding的意思是“不管”、“不顾”、“尽管”、“虽然”，用于表示让步。例如：
We started notwithstanding the rain (=We started though it was raining).
Notwithstanding the long delay, I shall still go.
Note:
1介词notwithstanding有时也可以放在它自己的宾语之后。如下面两句同义：
He insisted on going to the football match, notwithstanding his doctor's advice to the contrary.
He insisted on going to the football match, his doctor's advice to the contrary notwithstanding.
2.notwithstanding也可以用作副词和连词。例如：
He persisted, threats notwithstanding he had lived there a long time.
3.in spite of, despite, notwithstanding这三个介词都是表示“让步”，其中in spite of语气最强，despite次之，notwithstanding最弱。
　
of
　　英语冠词和介词是英语词类中使用频率最高的两大词类，而介词当中又以of的使用率为最高。介词of的基本意思是“……的”，主要用于说明词与词之间所存在的种种逻辑关系，如形容关系、同位关系、动宾关系、主表关系、主动关系、动状关系等。
A形容关系
A)表示“所有”、“所属”，例如：
He has no house of his own.
I know the tune of the song, but I don't know the words.
Note:
1我们可以用of表示“所有”、“所属”关系，也可以用名词的所有格（即在名词的词尾+'s）表示这种关系。如果这个名词是有生命的（人或动物），那么两种方法都可以使用，只是用of时，突出了“所有者”，用名词所有格时，则突出“所有物”。试比较：
He was familiar with the works of Shakespeare.
Shakespeare's works include plays and poems.
2如果这个名词太长，特别是本身也带有定语时，为了平衡的关系，一般用of结构，而不用名词所有格，如：
The person you mentioned will be the father of the person with the same name, is that right?
3如果“所有者”是无生命的东西，它的所有关系一般用of表示。但有些东西虽然没有生命，我们可以采用拟人的手法，如the ship's company（全体船员），the country's good（国家的利益），the ocean's roar（海啸），the sun's rays （太阳光线）等。此外，在报刊的标题上，为了节省篇幅，有时没有生命的东西也用名词所有格，并没有拟人的意味，如“London's Traffic（伦敦的交通）。
4时间、距离、价值、重量等都是无生命的概念，在实际应用上，常用所有格表示它们的数量关系（这时已经不是所有关系）。如two weeks' leave of absence（两周的假），a hair's breath（一发之差），a dollar's worth （一美元之值），two pounds' weight（两磅的重量）等。
5在某些固定的词组中，无生命的名词可以用所有格。如at his wit's end（不知所措），for form's sake (为了礼貌的缘故）。
B)表示“属性”、“特征”
of所组成的介词短语作定语用：
This is a matter of great importance.
of所组成的介词短语作表语用：
Those articles are of no value to me.
Note:
1“of+抽象名词”常相当于一个形容词，用于表示人或事物的属性、特征，如a man of wisdom = a wise man, a man of property =a wealthy man, a lady of virtue = a virtuous lady, a girl of twenty years = a twenty-year-old girl, seven years of age = seven years old有时不易找到与之相对应的形容词，如a man of his word（言行一致的人），a man of character（品格高尚的人）。
2在“of+抽象名词”结构中的of虽然也如同with那样常含有“having”的意思，但with之后接的是具体名词，其结构是“with+ 具体名词”，它是用于说明人或事物的形状，如：
a coat with three pockets
a book with green cover
a man with white hair
a soldier with a sword in his hand
3“of+a+名词”常含有“共同”、“相同”的意思，用于表示不同事物的共同“性质”、“特征”。
Birds of a feather flock together.
These are two of a kind.
4“of +a +形容词+名词”的结构常用来表示人的某种性格、性情、脾气、容貌等，有时也表示事物的某种特征。
He is of an inquiring turn of mind.
C)表示“内容”、“关连”
It was a story of adventure and romance.
Have you heard anything of Smith lately?
Note:
1“a picture of John”中的of表示“内容”，它说明图中“画的是John本人”，而“John's picture”，则可能含有两个意思：“John的画像”（相当于a picture of John），或“John画的像”（相当于a picture by John）。如果要说“这是John所有的许多画像中的一张”，则说“This is a picture of John's ”这时of所表示的是“部分关系”。
2某些动词如“所”、“讲”、“知道”等之后常接of，表示这些行为的关连，这里的of译为“到”、“及”，如hear of（听到），speak of（讲到），tell of（谈及）。know of （知道点）。这些动词如果接about，所关连的程度则有明显的差别，think of用think about则有语意的差别。试比较：
I know him我认识他。
I know of him我听说过他。
I know about him关于他的情况，我是了解的。
3.hear, tell, inform, dream等动词之后如果接有宾语从句，of则省略掉。试比较：
Has he been informed of his father's death yet?
I informed him that he must start at twelve o'clock.
4有些动词跟of结合形成了固定的词组，如approve of, take care of等，of之后的成分可以看成是这些动词词组的宾语。
His mother approve of his match.
The words admits of no other meaning.
We make use of electricity every day.
The early navigators disliked losing sight of land.
5在数量、单位、群体、种类等名词之后接of的短语时，of表示组成这些单位、群体或种类的具体内容。
Get me a piece of wood and a pot of paint.
What kind of paper do you want?
D)表示“数量”、“程度”（说明“部分关系”）
Would you like a little more of this soup?
He is not so much of a help as I thought he would be.
Note:
1在这种用法里，of说明了“部分”与“整体”之间的数量关系，表示“部分”的数量可大可小，小至零，大至全部。例如：
There are faults from which none of us is free.
All of us agreed on the question.
2.of前的数词如果带有定冠词，这个数词表示的就是“全部”数量，如果不带冠词，则表示“其中的一部分”。试比较：
Three of them were punished for disobeying orders.
他们当中有三个人因为不服从命令受到惩罚。
The three of them were punished for disobeying orders
他们三个人因为不服从命令受到惩罚。
3在all of之后一般接复数代词，接复数名词时，of可有可无。试比较：
All of them swam across the river.
All (of) the students swam across the river.
4我们可以说“one of her friends”也可以说“a friend of hers”却不能说“a her friend”，因为不定冠词是不能与代词（或名词）的所有格连用的。例如：
That seems to be one of his books.
That seems to be a book of his (=a book of his books).
5.of与独立所有格的连用，不但丰富了英语的表现形式，有时还有区别语义的作用。试比较：
A portrait of the president.
A portrait of the president's.
6我们可以用最高级词+of的结构来表示“其中之最”。例如：
This is the most difficult of the three.
He walked the slowest (=the most slowly) of the three.
7表示“其中之最”时，偶尔有这样的结构：“the bravest of the brave"（勇士中之勇士），“the fairest of the fair”（美人中之美人），有时名词也仿照这种结构，带上了最高级的意味，使意思表现得更有丰彩。例如：
Her daughter is the bone of her bone, flesh of her flesh.
她的女儿是她最亲的骨肉。
Shakespeare is called the poet of poets.
莎士比亚有“诗圣”之称。
8“of all”这种短语结构与它前面名词的关系常常比较松懈，因此被用来作为句子的状语，含有“在……中”的意思。例如：
She disappeared on her birthday of all days.
Why ask me to help, of all people?
9在口语中有时用“all of a …”的结构表示“一时的状态”。例如：
The train stopped all of a sudden.
He was all of a tremble (all of a sweat).
10两个或两部分事物间的比较可以用than的结构，也可以用表示部分关系的of结构。例如：
John is a better man than Henry.
John is the better man of the two.
11表示“程度”的of是由表示“数量”的of引申来的，它的结构常常是“不定代词+of+不可数名词”或“不定代词+of +a +可数名词”。例如：
I have had much of your impudence, I don't want any more of it.
She was nothing of a musician.
B同位关系
例如：
He went no further than the city of Los Angeles.
He became blind at the age of eleven.
Note:
1表示同位关系的of与表示内容的of（如a portrait of my mother）有些相近，但又有区别。表示同位关系的of常常用来表示国家、城市、山河、湖泊、岛屿、称号、姓名、年月、年龄等等的名称。
2有时这些名称也可以不用of。试比较：
We had a swim in the River Nile.
River of blood flowed during the war.
（这里的of表示“内容”，而不是表示“同位”）
The mount of Olives橄榄山
Mount Everest艾沃斯特山（即：珠峰）
the lake of Constance康斯坦茨湖
the West Lake西湖
Cape Hern合恩角
the Cape (of Good Hope)好望角
C动宾关系
A)动名词+of+逻辑宾语
例如：
The writing of that book took him ten years.
I couldn't help thinking of my hard life before liberation when I heard the words.
Note:
这里的逻辑宾语系指动名词的逻辑宾语。
B)动作名词+of+逻辑宾语
例如：
There has been some delay in the execution of my father's will.
Note:
这里的逻辑宾语系指动作名词的逻辑宾语。
C)含有动词意味的名词+of +逻辑宾语
例如：
He went about in fear of his enemy.
Note:
这里的逻辑宾语系指of前的名词的逻辑宾语。
D)行为者名词+ of +逻辑宾语
例如：
Who was the driver of the car when the accident happened?
Note:
　　象driver这样由及物动词形成的行为者名词与of之后的名词在逻辑上常常存在着动宾关系。
E)make…+名词+of+逻辑宾语
例如：
The other boys made fun of him.
Let us make an end of this foolish quarrel.
Note:
　　动词make常与一些带有动词意味的名词连用（这个名词有时带有不定冠词），再通过of引导出某种动作的逻辑宾语。
F)情态形容词+of+逻辑宾语
例如：
You may always be sure of a welcome at my house.
I am tired of your stupid conversation.
Note:
　　所谓的“情态形容词”是指形容人的“感情”或事物的“状态”的形容词。这些形容词之后有时可接宾语从句，而将of省略。常见的情态形容词有fond, proud, ashamed, aware, suspicious, hopeful, desirous, jealous, fearless, apprehensive, sick, patient, innocent, guilty, careful, worthy, lavish, economical, full, short, bare等。
D主表关系
A)形容词+of+逻辑主语
例如：
It is very kind of you to invite me.
It Is very good of you to take so much trouble.
Note:
1当我们对某人的某种行为的性质进行评述的时候，常采用“It is+性质特征形容词+of+逻辑主语+不定式”这样的结构。这里的it是形式主语，真正主语是不定式。不定式所表示的行为常常可以从上下文理解出来，因此常可以省略不提。例如：
It is very clever of you (to do so, to say so).
2形式主语it有时为that所代替，在感叹句中则同时省略形式主语及连系动词。例如：
That was so careless of you.
How stupid of me.
3与上述结构（It is+性质特征形容词+of+逻辑主语）相类似的是“真正主语+系动词+性质特征形容词+of+逻辑主语”，前者的逻辑主语指人，后者的逻辑主语指事物。例如：
He is hard of hearing.
The girl was lame of/in the left leg.
B).make+a +名词+of+逻辑主语
例如：
He said he was going to make a singer of me, but I thought he was trying to make a fool of me as I knew I could never make a success of music.
I occasionally have a glass of wine with my lunch, but I don't make a habit of it.
Note:
1在“make+a+名词+of+逻辑主语 ”结构中的逻辑主语是指make之后的名词（或代词）的逻辑主语。代替“I will make you a good teacher”这种说法，我们习惯上说“I will make a good teacher of you” 例如：
2与上述结构相类似的是“make+a+名词+of +a+逻辑主语”，这里make之后的“a +名词+of”含有 "象……“的意味。例如：
It is dangerous to make a friend of an enemy.
C)a+名词+of+a+逻辑主语
例如：
They lived in a paradise of a house (=a paradise-like house).
That is a fool of a person (=a foolish person).
Note:
1在“a+名词+of+a+逻辑主语”这种结构里，“a+名词+of”相当于一个形容词，用来表述逻辑主语的状态、特征，这里的of含有“象”的意思，因此这类短语常译为“象……”“如同……一般”，比如“a gem of a house”译为“象宝石一般的房子”。
2如果要具体指明某一个人时，可用代词（或指示代词)代替名词前的不定冠词，如her angel of a daughter（她那天使般的女儿）。
That brute of a husband beats her every day.
3在俚语里有不少此类表达法，如a devil of a fellow, a hell of a day, a dickens of a time, 这里a devil, a hell, a dickens都有加强语气的作用，它们的具体意思须视上下文而定。例如：
He has the devil of a time.
他处于极端（困难、快乐、兴奋等）境地。
That's a hell of a good car.
那是一部极好的汽车。
4这种结构是由表示“种类”转化而来的，试比较：
What sort of a man is he?
He is a saint of a man.
E主动关系
A)动作名词+of+逻辑主语
例如：
The resignation of the Prime Minister was hourly expected (The Prime Minister would resign).
The library is demanding the return of the book. (The library demands the book be returned.)
Note:
1这里的逻辑主语指of前动作名词的逻辑主语，这个动作名词在意思上是不及物的，如果这个动作名词在意思上是及物的，那么of之后常接逻辑宾语。试比较：
Do you need the services of a lawyer? （这里of表示“主动关系”）
Pride was her destruction, for it caused the loss of all her friends.(这里of表示动宾关系）
the shooting of the hunters
2可以用名词（或代词）的所有格表示动作名词的逻辑主语，of短语表示动作名词的逻辑宾语。
parents' love of their children
the hunters' shooting of the animals
F动状关系
A)表示“脱离”、“解脱”、“剥脱”、“解除”等。例如：
He cannot break himself of the bad habit.
If you won't follow my advice, I shall wash my hands of the business.
This medicine should cure you of your cold.
He was relieved of his post.
They knocked him down and robbed him of his watch.
Note:
1.of表示“脱离”，表示脱离的of与动词连用时，只限于几个特殊的动词。有时候同一个动词之后可以接from，也可以接of，但他们的用法有差别。of之后接被“解脱”的事物，from之后则接“脱离”的“处所”。试比较：
She can't free herself of the idea that someone's watching her all the time.
She freed the bird from its cage.
2有些形容词也含有“脱离”的意思，它们通过of表示脱离的对象。例如：
We are well quit of him.
If you have a car you are independent of trains, trams, and buses.
B)表示“距离”
例如：
The ship was wrecked within a mile of the coast.
Note:
1.of还可以表示时间的距离。
He died within a few days of his ninetieth birthday.
It lacks ten minutes of twelve.
2英国英语说five minutes to four，美国英语则说five minutes of four（四点差五分），这里的of也是表示“离开”。
It is a quarter of nine.
C)表示“来源”
例如：
The invention is of German origin.
Note:
1表示“来源”的of常用来表示“出生”。
2.of与表示“请求”、“探询”等动词连用时，表示“请求”、“探询”的对象。例如：
I inquired of him what he wanted.
He demands too high a price of me.
3在表示“期待”、“获得”之类动词之后，of与from常可以替用。例如：
What can you expect of (=from) me?
Don't borrow money of (=from) a person at so much interest.
4在come of 词组里，of也有表示“来源”的，译为“由……引起”、“是由于……的结果”，相当于develop from, arise from, result from等。例如：
I don't know if any good will come of your action.
The accident came of your carelessness.
5有些表示“来源”的所有格形式不能用of的短语来替代，举例如下：
the girl's story (=a story that the girl told)
John's telegram (=a telegram from John, a telegram that John sent)
6有些表示“描述”的所有格也不能用of短语替代。例如：
a woman's college (=a college for women).
a doctor's degree (=a doctoral degree)
D)表示“原因”
例如：
I told him that only a man who was dying of hunger could eat such a breakfast.
He died of acute pneumonia.
Note:
1“of one's own accord”, “of one's own choice”等短语含有“自愿”的意思，“of oneself”含有“自动”的意思。这些短语都是表示某种行为的“动机”、“动因”。例如：
We signed up for the protest march of our own accord.
She married him of her own choice.
He did it of his own free will.
It didn't happen of itself.
I did not wake him; he woke up of himself.
2与这种用法类似的是“of necessity”（不可避免地，必然）。例如：
That discussion must of necessity be postponed for a while.
E)表示“材料”
例如：
Concrete is made of cement, sand, stones and water.
Furniture is made mainly of wood.
Note:
1除of之外，from, with等也可以表示“材料”。
2在“make a man of him”这种结构中的of也可以理解为表示“材料”、“人材”。
F)表示“成分”
例如：
Parliament consists of the House of Commons and the House of Lords.
The chemistry teacher asked the pupils what water was composed of.
Note:
　　“A family of five”，“a committee of seven”之类短语中的of也是表示“成分”，这与表示“内容”的of很接近。
G)表示“时间”
例如：
We usually go shopping of a Sunday (=on Sundays).
What do you do of an evening (=in the evenings)?
Note:
1“of a Sunday”这种结构里虽然用不定冠词，表示的都是复数的意味，即表示“经常性的行为”，这里的“of”含有“during”（在……时候）的意思。
2用of表示的时间短语还有of late（近来），of recent（近来），of late years （最近几年），of recent years （近年来），of old（从前，凭经验）等。例如：
The days have been getting warmer of late.
Knights of old had to wear armor in battle.
H)表示“被动”
例如：
He is beloved of all.
He was warned of the headmaster.
Note:
这里的“of”含有“by”的意思，但这是古英语的用法。
　
off
　　off与on相对应，on表示“接触”，off则表示“离开”、“脱离”；on表示“处于”某种状态，off 则表示“脱离”某种状态。具体用法如下：
A表示“离开”
例如：
Here's a train that has just come in, with crowds of people getting off it.
It's so interesting that I can't take my eyes off it.
B表示“脱落”
例如：
You might get hurt badly if you fell off that ladder.
The cat knocked the flower-pot off the stand.
C表示“除去”
例如：
It's very difficult to clean paint marks off the floor.
Ten minutes later I was lying in a hot bath washing off the dusty reminders of the Continent.
D表示（小路）“从大路上岔开”
例如：
Turn off the main road here and you will come to the lake.
Elder Row runs off Newsome Street.
E表示“沿岸”（离岸不远）
例如：
There was a ship in distress off the coast of Kent.
The Doutelle escaped in the mist and made her way among the rocky islands off the west of Scotland.
F表示“离题”
例如：
Half of what he said was off the subject.
The student received low marks for his essay, as much of it was off the point.
G表示“减价”
例如：
2.5 per cent off (=taken off the price) for ready cash.
H表示“少于……”、“低于……”
例如：
It's a few minutes off five o'clock.
We are a few miles off Workshop.
She is two years off twenty.
Note:
　　表示地点或时间时，off的意思相当于before，而不是beyond。
I表示“来源”、“依靠”
例如：
He lived off his sister.
He made his living off the tourists.
J表示“不吃”、“不喝”、“不抽”、“戒……”等
例如：
Bill's off drugs now.
He has been off wine/ smoking /tea/ meat.
K表示“讨厌”、“不喜欢”
例如：
I'm right off love stories for some reason.
He's off playing tennis now.
L注意下列词组、成语的意义。
1）off duty（下班、不当班）
We go on duty at 7:00 a.m. and come off duty at 5:30 p.m.
2）off work（停工、中断工作）
Let's knock off work early today.
3）off colour（身体不舒服、气色不佳，精神不好）
I am feeling off colour today.
4）off guard （不提防、不警惕）。
Timmy's question caught Jean off guard, and she told him the secret before she knew it.
5）off hand（马上、立刻）
I can't answer your question off hand (=immediately) without thinking more about it.
6）off the map（远离人烟的，不重要的，不复存在的）
That's a place off the map.
7）off the record（秘密地、不许记录、不许发表的、不得引用的）
The President gave his opinion to the newsmen off the record.
8） off the beam （脱离航向的；错的、不对的）
A radio signal tells the pilot of an airplane that his plane is off the beam.
9）off the beaten track（偏僻的、不寻常的）
We are looking for a vacation spot that is off the beaten track.
10）off one's head （发狂的、神智失常的）
He is off his head for pride.
11) off one's feed (因不舒服而不想吃东西，心情忧郁）
Jerry seemed to be off his feed; he did not joke and laugh with others.
12）quick off the mark （聪明的、精灵的）
She didn't understand what to do, she's not very quick off the mark .
13）come off it（别说得天花乱坠、别吹；别假装；停止）
Come off it, Ben, we know you married her for money.
Now, you boys, come off it; haven't I told you not to tease the dog?
14）go off the deep end（因感情冲动而贸然行事）
Mike warned his roommate not to go off the deep end and get married.
15）get sth. off one's chest（把闷在心里的东西说出来）
After David told the principal that he had cheated on the test, he was glad because it was off his chest.
　
on (upon)
　　在多数情况下upon可以用来代替on，但upon主要用于正式文体。on与off相对应，off表示“离开”、“脱离”，on则表示“接触”、“附着”，并由此引申出其它的含义。
A“在……上面”、“在……上”
　　表示“接触”、“附着”。某一个事物与另一个事物的“表面”相接触，即一个事物“附着”在另一个事物的“表面”时，我们用on表示，所谓的“表面”可以是“面”（surface），也可以是“线”（line）。
B与“面”的接触
例如：
One or two good water-colours hung on the walls.
He wrote down all the names on a piece of paper.
Note:
1“附着”在身体 的on常含有“穿着”的意思。in 也是表示穿着，但in是从“包裹”的角度来表达“穿着”的，in之后接用以“包裹”的东西，on之后则接所“附着”的地方。
试比较：
They put a robe of purple on him with white fur at the edges.
The magistrate was dressed in black.
2“on the wall”一般是指“在墙壁的纵面上”，但也可能指“在横面上”。
试比较：
A mirror hung on the wall.
Humpty-Dumpty sat on the wall.
3.in表示“在……面上”，强调“表面”。下列各组例句中的in与on一般不能互相替用。试比较：
There is a picture of a ship on page 44.
Who do you think is the greatest man on (the) earth?
It is one of the greatest countries in the world.
Breakfast is generally a bigger meal in England than you have on the Continent.
They are touring in Europe.
The players were practising on the field.
He lost life on the field of battle.
Cows were grazing in the field.
They are working on the farm.
4“on you”一类的短语含有“在……身上”的意思，相当于with，但这里的on不能用upon来替换。例如：
He doesn't usually carry much money on him.
Have you got a match on you?
5以下是一些由“在……上面”引申的比喻用法。
Can you describe the expression on the man's face?
They made a very deep impression on his mind.
I know another idiom, it's on the tip of my tongue but I can't quite say it.
Why on earth are you sitting there?
C与“线”的接触
例如：
Hang the clothes on the line.
There was a bird sitting on a branch.
Note:
1河流，街道等从远处着眼就是一条“线”，因此表示“与线接触”的on在“on the river ”，“on the street”等短语里含有“在河岸上”、“在街旁”的意思，即“在……边缘”，而不是“在河上”、“在街上”。
London is on the Thames.
There were a number of hotels on the main road.
2.on既可以表示“在……边缘”，也就引申出“在……附近”、“在……近旁”、“与……比邻”等意思。
3在 “apples on the trees”这样的结构中，on表示“苹果”与“树枝”的接触，含有“悬挂”（hanging from）的意思。
Big apples hung red and ripe on the trees.
There was a slight wind gently moved the leaves on the trees.
4“on one's way to…”里的on含有along的意思。
A funny thing happened to me on my way to the theatre!
D“与……毗邻”
　　表示“邻近”、“附近”、“毗邻”、“接连（不断）”等。某一事物与一条“线”接触时用on；有时虽然没有接触，但非常接近（very near），也就沿用了on的这种用法，因此on便含有“在……旁边”、“靠近……”、“临……”、“与……毗邻”等意义。例如：
San Francisco is on the Pacific Ocean.
The United States borders on the two oceans.
试比较：
He lived on the outskirts of the forest.
They started a plant in the outskirts of the city.
They lived in the suburbs.
Note:
1“on the right (side) of the road”这样的短语既可能含有“在路以外的右边”的意思，也可能指“路本身的右边”，因此具体含义必须根据说话人心目中的“中心线”而定。如果“中心线”是这条路本身，那么所指的意思就是前者，如果“中心线”是路中心，所指的意思便是后者。试比较：
Cars drive on the left side of the road in England.
I am planting the apple trees on the left and the pear trees on the right of the path.
We live on the north side of the city, they live on the south side.
2有时用连字符把两个“接触”或“接近”的地名连接起来，构成复合名词，如Stratford-on-Avon(Avon河畔Straford)，Newcastle-upon-Tyne(Tyne河畔Newcastle)。
3由“地点”的接近，又引申表示“时间”、“年龄”、“重量”等的接近，在on前常加just, near 等词以加强语气，这时的just on, near on含有almost（差不多）的意思，有时也可以说hard on。同样地，on也可以表示“顺序”的连接，含有“紧接”的意思。例如：
It's near on twelve o'clock.
I am just / hard upon eighty years of age.
It's just on ￡20.
One misfortune followed on another.
Her illness followed on her mother's death.
4在“loss on loss”（重大损失），“heaps on heaps ”(一堆堆）等结构中的on表示“累加”、“接连不断”等意思。例如：
Defeat on defeat discouraged him.
Trouble on trouble followed his involvement with her.
E“支撑”
表示“支撑”、“支持”。on表示一个事物“附着”在另一个事物之上，反过来说，被附着的事物“支撑”、“支持”着“附着”在它上面的事物，因此on又可以引申为“支撑、支持”。例如：
When he could no longer walk he tried to crawl on hands and knees.
He was so confused but so happy that he hardly knew whether he was standing on his head or his heels.
Note:
　　表示“支撑”的on有时可以用来表示“交通手段”，如on foot, on bicycle, on horse (back), on motorbike等，它们一般不能与表示手段的“by”替用，其它情况下则一般用by，如by bus, by car, by train 等,有时也用on。例如：
It takes longer to travel on foot than by car.
I like to travel on horseback.
F“依靠”
　　表示“基础”、“根据”、“理由”、“依靠”。on的这些意思都是跟“支撑”、“支持”的意义密切相关的。例如：
A）“基础”
例如：
His argument was rested on a basis of conjecture.
A building must be laid on a firm foundation.
B）“根据”、“理由”
例如：
On what ground do you suspect him?
I engaged the men on his recommendation.
C）“依靠”
例如：
You can't do a hard day's work on a cup of coffee and a slice of toast.
For many years he ran his business on a staff of only five.
Note:
1短语live on之后接食物时，含有“以……为主食”的意思。例如：
They lived chiefly on fruit.
I don't know what he lives on.
2短语live on之后接“收入”、“人”、“特性”等时，意思是“靠……生活”。例如：
He is just a scrounger, who lives on other people.
He lives on wits.
G“由……支付”
　　在非正式的场合下（特别是喝酒的时候）常用on表示“由……支付”。例如：
Have a drink, on me.
"Well, boys, the drinks are on me! Give it a name!"
H表示发生某行为的“媒介物”
　　相当于by means of，例如：
I want to listen to the programme on the radio.
He was playing an old tune on his flute.
Note:
1.on表示具体的“媒介物”，因此在媒介物之前一般要带定冠词，by常直接与表示“媒介物”的名词连用，不带定冠词，表示抽象的“手段”。
2某些交通工具可以与on连用，表示交通的“媒介物”。
I表示“状态”、“过程”、“从事”。
A）“状态”
例如：
The house is on fire.
It is difficult to keep on good terms with such a person.
B）“过程”
例如：
The murderer is now on trial.
I'll get some chocolate on my way back.
Are you travelling on business or for pleasure?
He has just published one novel and is already on a second.
Note:
1有时候可以见到“on + the + 形容词”这样的结构，它们相当于一个状语，用来表示行为的“状态”。但这种结构所表达的意思并不完全等于与该形容词相对应的副词，而是略带贬义。这里的形容词实际上已经名词化了。例如：
They got married on the quiet.
他们偷偷地结婚了。
The boy smoked on the sly.
这孩子偷偷摸摸地抽烟。
She got some new trousers on the cheap down at the market
她在市场上买了条便宜的新裤子。.
2用on表示“过程”时常用到“on + the + 动作名词”这样的结构，例如“on the increase”，它的意思相当于现在分词increasing，表示行为正在进行过程之中，类似的还有：on the run, on the move, on the watch, on the look-out, on the march, on the prowl等等。
He is always on the make (=trying by all kinds of means to make money).
She has been on the go all day.
3.on和in都可以表示“状态”，但on常表示可以随时解除的状态，与off相对应；in则表示处于相对稳定的状态，与out of相对应。试比较：
Long hair is not very much in fashion now for men (= out of fashion).
He struck me while I was not on my guard (= off my guard).
He is in poverty.
He is on holiday.
4.on和at都可以表示“从事”。on的原意含有“接触”的意思，因此用on时强调处于从事某事的“过程”之中；at则是从“点”上看问题，因此强调了从事某事的总体状态。试比较：
He is coming home on leave of absence.
Will you be at the meeting tonight?
J表示“主题”、“内容”
A)表示“主题”
　　on含有“系统论述”、“专题讲演”的意思，因此“讲演”、“论著”等必须接on。about则是表示一般提及“讲演”、“论著”等的“内容”，或说明“谈话”、“思考”、“忧虑”等的“内容”。试比较：
Today your father received an invitation to come to Oxford University for a year to give a course of lectures on philosophy.
In some of my earlier letters I have told you all about the other students here.
Note:
1有时动词、名词或形容词不可能含有“系统论述”的意思，因此它们只能接about表示有关的“内容”，而不能接on。例如：hear about this, quarrel about money, be careful about what you say, a story about famous men等。
2讲演某一个专题时，习惯上用on，如lecture on a particular topic。但大学里讲授课程时则用in；讲授课程中的专题部分时，仍用on。如：
Dr. Watson is at Oxford University, where he lectures in English literature. This term he is lecturing on the nineteenth-century novelists.
3.give a lecture on意思是“作一次有关……的演讲”；而give sb. a lecture on则是“就……训斥某人”。如：
Prof. Li gave a lecture on philosophy.
His father gave him a lecture on smoking.
B)表示“内容”
　　有时on也被用作about的意思，并没有“论述”的意思。例如：
I think you will all like to get a little sidelight on English education.
Foreign governments consulted her on the health services for their countries.
　　on之后接“团体”、“机关”时，表示属于该机构的“成员”或“职员”。例如：
When you are on our staff, you must be more careful and not to leave valuable papers lying about!
He's on the committee that controls government spending.
C)表示“时间”（包括“日期”和“时间的连接”）
　　表示“日期”。特指某一天时用on表示。例如：
My birthday is on April 9th.
I usually go to the cinema on Tuesday.
Note:
1表示“点钟”用at；泛指比一天长或比一天短的时期用in；特指某一天，或某一天的某个时期都用on。例如：
They landed on an evening in October.
I am leaving early on Thursday, the 23rd, and I shall arrive in Basle on Friday morning.
2在美国英语里，表示星期的某一天前的on可以省略，但如果星期的某一天之前有冠词时, on不能省略。例如：
What stopped you from coming Wednesday?
She arrived on the Sunday and left on the Tuesday.
He arrived on a Thursday.
3某一天前如带有last, next, this, that, late, early等修饰语时on 一般都省略，但在Monday next 这种结构里可以用on，也可以不用on 。试比较：
He retires next Friday.
He retires (on) Friday next.
4在today, yesterday, tomorrow之前的on一律省略。例如：
Tomorrow I shall be quite free.
5在occasion之前习惯上用on，虽然它并不特指某一天。例如，on occasion, on one occasion, on several occasions等。例如：
Send you my best wishes on this happy occasion.
6成语on the hour是“在某一点钟准点地”的意思，相当于every hour on the hour，即一点整，二点整或三点整等。
7表示“在晚上”时，有时说at night，有时说in the night，但如果特指某个晚上时，则用on。例如：
It would be a crime to send the boy out on such a cold, wet night.
K表示“时间的连接”,“空间的接触”可以引申到“时间的紧接”，在on之后接动名词或动作名词时，常含有“一……就……”这样的意义，即“一件事（或一个行为）紧接着另一件事（或另一个行为）”。例如：
He changed his wet clothes on reaching home.
On hearing the crash, he rushed out of the house.
L表示“着落”“对象”“方向”
on 除了表示“面”或“线”的“接触”之外，还可以表示一个物体向另一个物体运动，并“着落”在它的“面”或“线”上，形成“接触”，这里的on相当于on to 。
A）“着落”
例如：
In this office most work seems to fall on me.
Finally he was brought to trial in London for having made war on his people and being an enemy of his country.
Note:
在“touch one on the …”这种结构中的“the”不能改用代词所有格，如不能说strike the man on his mouth, 应该说strike the man on the mouth。
B）“对象”
例如：
The child has set his heart on that toy: I wish I could buy it for him.
C）“方向”
例如：
Charles, as a matter of fact, was all for advancing on London.
Note:
1表示“方向”的on与表示“目标”、“对象”的at和to的区别，以及look on, gaze on, smile on等与look at, gaze at, smile at的区别，见at条。
2表示“方向”的on有时含有against 的意思，在这种情况下on与for相对应，试比较：
They shut the door on me.
他们把我拒于门外。
They shut the door for me.
他们为我关门。
　
Onto ( on to )
　　onto同时含有on和to的意思，它表示某一事物向某一“平面”、“线”或“点”运动，并“着落”、“接触”在其上面，通常译为“到……上”。在美国英语里一般用onto这样的形式，在英国英语里则以“on to”为佳，这里的“on to”是复合介词。此外，在非正式的场合，onto还有“了解”、“挑剔”、“认识”、“注意”等意思。
A“到……上”
例如：
He was never systematic about his paper; they piled up on his desk and work table and oveflowed on to the floor.
They tried to put the blame on to me.
Note:
1.on也可以被用作onto的意思，表示“由运动而接触”。如：He finally got on (=onto) the horse但用onto时常常强调“作这种运动的努力”。另一方面，如果要强调运动的方向，比如与from连用时，也必须用onto。例如：
Water splashed down from the roof onto my hat.
2在英国，into常写成on to，这里的on和to 构成复合介词，它们之后仅接一个介词宾语。如climb on to the box。但有时候on是副词，用来修饰前面的动词，在这种情况下on与to已不是复合介词，不能连写在一起，如：
Let us go on to the next item on the agenda.
3在副词on之后有时接不定式的to，这时on和to已不是复合介词，因此不能连写成onto，如：
He went on to tell us of his experiences on Tibet.
4偶尔onto也被用作on的意思，表示“静止的接触的位置”。主要用在“圆状的东西”与其它事物的接触。例如：
The coat has big buttons onto it.
B“be onto sb / sth”
　　在俚语里“be onto sb”含有“知道某人的意图”、“发现某人的错误（或非法行为）”、“挑剔某人”等意思。“be onto sth”含有“注意到某物”、“了解某物的重要性”、“能利用某物”等。例如：
He's always onto me.
他老找我的岔子。
I'm onto your little tricks.
我知道你的鬼把戏。
　
　
opposite
　　严格地说opposite是形容词或副词，表示“在……的对面”。opposite 须与介词to连用，如“opposite to the school”，由于to被省略的缘故，opposite 也就变成介词，在它的后面可以直接跟着介词的宾语，如opposite the school。opposite与opposite to的关系，就象like与like to, near与near to一样，都是因为省略to而形成介词的。
A“在……的对面”
例如：
The man opposite him at the table was a total stranger.
There is a bus stop right opposite the post office.
B“在……的另一边”
例如：
He made a check opposite the name.
C“与主角相对的异性角色”
例如：
He played opposite her.
Note:
　　在美国口语里across from相当于opposite。这里的across是副词，across from可以理解为复合介词。例如：
Directly across from the door was a little window.
They live right across from us.
　
out
　　out 表示“向外”的运动，相当于out of，这是美国英语的用法。在英国，一般只能说go out of the door，而不说go out the door，除非在口语中。
A“通过……而出”
例如：
He ran out the door.
He is peering out the door at the river.
Note:
1. out表示“向外”时一般是指“向门外”。“向窗外”。
2在美国英语里，表示“通过……而出”时，只能用out，不能用out of, out含有through的意思。如果要表示“离去”，则必须用out of。试比较：
He jumped out the window.
他从窗口跳出去。
He went out of the house.
他离开这所房子。
3有时介词out之前还带有另一个介词from，意思是“从……外面”。例如：
From out the open window came bursts of laughter.
4有时表示“在外”的静止位置。例如：
I like sitting out dances in the refreshment room.
我不喜欢跳舞，宁可坐在小吃部里。
B“沿着……而去”
例如：
He drove out the street.
We drove out the dark road to his house.
　
out of
　　out of是由副词out加介词of构成的复合介词，表示“由内向外”的运动，或“在外”的静止空间位置，并由此引申出“突出”、“离开”、“超出”、“失去”、“没有”、“脱节”、“结果”、“来源”、“出生”、“比例”、“材料”、“器具”、“动机”、“原因”等。一般情况下，out of与in或into相对应。
A“从……里（向外）”
例如：
I took a handkerchief out of the drawer.
Note:
1. out of 与into相对应，out of表示“由里向外”的运动，into则表示“由外向里”的运动。
One of the letters dropped out of the bag.
He dropped the letter into the mailbox.
2.out of有时可以用outside, 或outside of来替用，表示“由里面向外面”的运动。
They went out of the house to talk.
They went outside (of) the house to talk.
3.out of有时还表示“从某一表面或物体向外突出”
Don't stick your head out of the carriage window.
A nail was sticking out of the wall.
B“在……外”
例如：
He stayed out of the district.
This animal is not found out of Europe.
Note:
1表示“在……外”的out of有时含有“在离开……的地方”。例如：
There was a railway accident a few miles out of Nottingham.
One evening I was taken by a friend to a drivening cinema about eleven miles out of the city.
2.out of之前有时还可以用另一个介词from，如from out of the room.
The rubbish should be removed from out of the house.
C“超出……（范围）之外”
例如：
The car went out of control and crashed over the cliff.
We're out of reach of the railway here.
It is out of (all) reason to expect him to get here by half past six each morning.
D“脱离……（状态）”
例如：
He helped me out of my difficulties.(difficulty的复数形式一般指“经济上的困难”）
Out of debt, out of danger.
E“失去”、“没有”、“缺乏”、“与……脱节”
例如：
Long skirts for women are out of fashion now.
He's been out of work (=out of a job) for two years.
After climbing to the top of the tower, we were so out of breath that we had to sit down.
We are out of stock in this item.
Note:
1表示“失去”、“没有”、“脱节”等的out of与in相对应。试比较：
The new edition of that dictionary has long been out of print.
I am not sure whether the work you mention is still in print.
2.out of question的意思是“毫无疑问”，相当于beyond (all) question, without question, past question等，可以用作状语，也可用作定语。out of the question的意思，是“不可能的 ”、“办不到的”。
The boys had no money, so it was out of the question for them to go to the movies.
His honesty is out of question.
3.out of还可以由“失去”、“没有”引申表示某种行为造成消极、否定的“结果”。
You cannot laugh me out of my belief.
He talked me out of my resolution.
F“来自……”
例如：
It is sometimes more difficult to translate than to write out of one's own head.
I paid for it out of my own pocket.
Note:
1.from, of等也可以表示“来源”，但out of表示“来源”时，含有“从……当中”的意思。
2表示“来源”的out of有时还用来表示动物（特别是马）的“生育”，意思是“由……所生殖”，out of之后接雌性动物，by之后接雄性动物。例如：
Golden Trumpet, by Golden Rain out of Silver Trumpet.
G表示数量的“比例”关系
例如：
Out of all the people present, only two spoke in favour of the proposal.
This is only one instance out of many.
Note:
　　of表示纯粹的“比例”关系，即“部分与整体”的关系，而out of则更含有“来源”的意思，这里的out 有强调的作用。试比较：
You may choose one of them.
你可以挑选其一。
You may choose one out of them.
你可以从中挑选一个。
H表示“材料”、“器具”
例如：
Flour is made out of wheat.
He drank milk (tea, wine) out of a glass.
Note:
1.of也可以表示材料，是由out of省略out而来的，但out of中的out往往有加强语气的作用，with则表示制成品的成分之一。
2.out of或off都可以后接饮食器具。即“从……（器具饮食）”，它们都可以被of所代替，但不能用with来代替。
I表示“动机”、“原因”
例如：
I opened the box out of curiosity.
I came out of real interest, not just to have a good time.
Note:
out of表示心理上的原因即“动机”时可以与from替用。与for, because of等的区别，见from条。例如：
She obeyed from fear (=out of fear).
J注意下列词组的用法：
1）out of hand（无法控制，立即）
You should not allow a young child like that to get out of hand.
I cannot comply with your request out of hand, I must consider it first.
2）out of one's mind /head（发狂，精神错乱）
The poor fellow went out of his mind.
3）out of humour（不高兴的，情绪不好的）
The chief is feeling out of humour again.
4）out of temper（发脾气、生气）
He soon gets out of temper if things do not go as he would like them to.
5)out of the way（不挡道的）
Please get out of the way, and allow this gentleman to pass.
6）out of date（过时的，废弃的）
These words are out of date.
7）out of place（处于不恰当、不相称、不适当的地位）
I felt out of place among the foreigners.
　
outside
　　outside用于表示“在外面”或“由里面到外面”的空间位置，并由此引申表示“在……范围之外”、“除……以外”等意思。
A“在……外面”、“到……外面”
例如：
Wait outside the door until you are asked to come in.
We are not allowed to go outside the ground.
Note:
1表示“在外”或“向外”的outside与inside相对应。
2.outside有时含有“在……上面”的意思，这是英国口语的用法。例如：
He rode outside the coach.
3.out of 表示“由里向外”的运动，强调了运动的出发点，即强调了“里面”，outside表示“由里面向外面”运动的结果所处的空间位置，它强调的是“外面”。在没有强调这种意思时，out of与outside可以替用。例如：
While we were standing in the road we saw somebody come out of (=outside) the house.
B“在……范围之外”、“超过……范围”
例如：
I cannot make concessions which are outside the regulations.
That is outside his interests.
Note:
1表示“超过范围”的outside与within相对应。
2如果outside之后接的是某一具体数量，outside则含有“多于……”、“……以上”的意思，这是英国口语的用法。例如anything outside ￡ 100(起码100镑以上）。
C“除去……”、“除……以外”
例如：
He has few hobbies or interests outside golf and gardening.
Outside her cooking, what does she have?
Note:
用outside表示“除外”的意思一般只用于口语之中。
　
outside of
　　outside of是复合介词，在英国口语中，可以用来代替outside，表示“在……外面”或“除……以外”的意思，而在美国口语中，outside of一般只用来表示“除……以外”的意思。例如：
Women students work harder than men and one seldom sees them outside of the classroom.
Mrs Cox had no jewelry outside of her wedding ring.
Note:
1在英国口语中outside of有时含有“在……上面”的意思，如outside of a horse是“在马上”的意思。例如：
He looked better outside of a horse than on his own legs.
2.get outside of含有“吃”、“喝”的意思，这是英国俚语的用法，在美国get outside of 则含有“理解”、“掌握”意思。例如：
Get outside of that, and you'll feel a different man.
We got outside of a good dinner that evening.
　
over
　　表示相对空间位置的over含有“在……上方”、“在……上面”、“遍及……”、“越过……”、“从……到另一端”、“在……对面”等意思，并且由此引申出“从……边上溢出（落下）”、“撞到……”、“（数量）超过……”、“（职权，努力）高于……”、“在……旁监视……”、“从头至尾……一遍”、“在……期间”、“从事……”、“过了……（时间）”、“由于……”、“关于……”、“通过……（通讯途径）”等意思。
A“在……正上方”、“高于……”
例如：
We slept without any roof over our heads.
Note:
1.over与表示运动的动词连用时，含有“从……上方”的意思。例如：
The aeroplane flew over the city.
2.over与under相对应，over 表示“垂直在上”，under则表示“垂直在下”，它们反映了两个物体之间的相对位置，这两个物体通常是不接触在一起的。例如：
Give me a blue sky over my head and a green road under my feet and I am happy.
3.over表示“垂直在上”，above则不一定。因此above可以代替over, over却不能任意代替above，除非above所表示的是“垂直在上”的位置。例如：
The lamp hung over (above) the table.
His room is above mine.
4分数的分子在分母的上方，因此over也被引申表示分数，如 读作three over four (或three four)。
B“覆盖在……上面”、“在……上面”
例如：
She spread the blanket over her knees.
Do you see that flock of sheep scattered over the hillside?
Note:
1.over有时也可以指“覆盖在……前面”。
She wore a veil over her face.
2.over表示“覆盖在……上面”时，与under相对应。可能是全部覆盖，也可能是部分覆盖。覆盖物与被覆盖物可能有接触，也可能没有接触。在这种情况下，over不能被above替用。
C“遍及……”
例如：
English is being spoken all over the world.
The boy scattered paper over the floor.
Note:
　　表示“遍及”的over之前常加all，以加强语气。
D“横在……上面”
例如：
The bridge over the river is closed for repair.
E“越过……”、“从……到另一边”
例如：
The ball rolled over (=across) the grass.
The train ran over a boy.
Note:
1.over可以表示沿着水平方向，也可以表示象“翻山越岭”那样沿着曲线从一边到另一边。例如：
He jumped over the wall.
If we can't go over the mountain we must go round it.
2.across可以表示“沿着水平方向从一边到另一边”，但不能表示“翻山越岭”那样的曲线运动。可以说jump across the ditch，也可以说jump over the ditch;但只能说jump over the wall，而不能说jump across the wall。
3短语get over含有“越过……”、“克服……”、“从……之中恢复过来”等意思。例如：
It's not easy to get over the garden wall.
How shall we get over this difficulty.
He is just getting over his severe illness.
F“在……的那一边”、“在……对面”
例如：
He lived just over the river.
The sun disappeared over the horizon.
Note:
1短语be over sth含有“度过（困难等）”的意思。例如：
We're over (=past) the worst of troubles now.
2表示“在……的那一边”的over有时含有“运动结果所处状态”的意思。例如：
The horses are over the fence (having jumped the fence).
G“从……边上溢出”、“从……边缘落下”
例如：
The river flowed over its banks.
He rolled forward and fell over the edge.
H“撞到……”
例如：
Someone left a box in the garden and I fell over it in the dark.
He stumbled over the edge of the pavement.
I“（数量）超过 …”、“在……以上”
例如：
The repairs to my watch cost me over five pounds.
He couldn't enter for the examination because he was over age.
Note:
1在“for over two hours”这样表示时间长度的词组中，over前的for常可以省略。例如：
He has lived abroad (for) over five years.
2短语 over and above含有“在……之外（还）”的意思，相当于in addition to。例如：
You will get ￡ 50 a year over and above your usual salary.
J“（职位、权力、势力）高于……”、“在……之上”
例如：
A captain is over a lieutenant.
The mayor presided over the city council.
He has great command over himself.
Note:
　　表示职位、权力“高于……”的over与under相对应。over与under所表示的两者之间存在着“支配”与“受支配”的关系。如下例：
The King is ruler over the whole country.
The army is under the King's direct command.
K“在……旁边监视”
例如：
His mother had to sit over him and make him do his homework.
Will you watch over my clothes while I go for a swim?
Note:
　　表示“在……旁边监视”的over主要与sit, stand, look, watch等动词连用。
L“从头到尾……一遍”、“从……到另一端”
例如：
Did you go over the book carefully before it went to the printers?
Will you look over this exercise that I have done, and see if it is correct?
Note:
1表示“从一端到另一端”的over有时含有along的意思。例如：
We motored over a new route.
2表示“从头到尾……一遍”的over一般是指“粗略地看一遍”，否则必须加象carefully之类的修饰语。
M“在……期间”、“在（做）……的时候”
例如：
Over the years he's become more and more patient.
He fell asleep over a book.
N“从事……”
例如：
They are taking a very long time over a simple job.
She took a lot of time over the arrangement for the journey.
O“过了……（时间）”、“直到……过后”
例如：
Can you stay over Monday?
Are you staying over (the) night?
Note:
　　表示“经过……（时间）”的over相当于through，主要用于较短的时间，如night, weekend, Christmas等，through或throughout则用于较长的时间，如summer, years等。over强调时间的“终止”，for表示时间的“长度”，during则表示“在某一段时间内”。
P“由于……”、“关于……”、“在……方面”
例如：
They quarrelled over some political issue.
Can I see you a moment? I've an important matter to talk over with you.
Note:
　　表示“关于”的over常可以与about替用，如worry, gloat, quarrel, talk, think等动词都可以与about连用，但用over时，多少都含有“由于”的意思。
Q“通过……（通讯途径）”
例如：
The matter was arranged over the telephone.
It was broadcasted over the radio.
　
owing to
　　owing to是复合介词，意思是“由于……”
例如：
That's a mistake owing to carelessness.
Owing to the long drought the crops have failed.
Note:
现在, owing to 和due to常可以互相通用, 只是owing to很少用作定语，due to一般不用于句首, 除非为了特别强调，或用于正式的场合。
　
past
　　past可以表示时间、数量、范围、能力、空间、地点等的“超过”。
A时间、数量或数目的“超过”
例如：
We start work at half past seven every morning.
Stella is still good-looking, but she's past her prime.
Note:
　　用past表示超过某整点时，可以与o'clock连用，但是如果说“几点过几分”，则不能与o'clock连用，而应与minute连用，这是现代英语的用法。试比较：
It is past five o'clock.
The train arrived at exactly four minutes past eight.
B范围、限度、能力、可能性等的“超过”
例如：
The old horse is past work.
The strange idea is past (=beyond) belief.
Cancer is past (=beyond) cure.
Note:
1. past表示“能力的超过”时含有“失去往常的能力”的意思。例如：
She used to be a good tennis player, but at the age of fifty she is past it.
2短语“past praying for”的意思是“无可救药”。例如：
The wicked man is past praying for.
C空间、地点的“超过”
例如：
She walked past my door.
Who is the man who has just run past me?
　
pending
　　“直到……时候”、“在……之前”（一般用于正式场合）。例如：
This matter must wait pending his return from Europe.
Pending the trial, he was kept in prison.
　
per
　　per含有“每……”、“经由……”、“按照……”等意思。
A“每……”
例如：
The building rents at 15 pounds per month.
The rate of deaths fell from sixty per thousand to three per thousand.
Note:
1.per原是拉丁介词，在英语里，表示“每……”时，一般用不定冠词a，有时也用定冠词the，per主要用于商业上或正式场合。试比较：
These apples cost 20 pence a kilo.
These apples cost 20 pence per kilo.
2在正式的场合可以与once, twice, three times等连用，如once per day，一般情况下不用per，而用a, every, each等，试比较：
She visits me three times a (every, each) week.
She visits me three times per week.
3.per cent从per centum省略而来的，per cent所构成的短语可以用作名词，也可以用作副词用。试比较：
I am 100 per cent in agreement with you. (100 per cent作状语）
The interest is 5 percent yearly（“5 percent”作表语）
4.per cent所构成的名词性短语，可以看成是复数的，也可以看成是单数的，必须根据实际情况而定。例如：
Of the new subscribers, 75 percent have paid already.
Nine per cent is a large percentage.
5.per diem(出差津贴)，per annum（每年）都是拉丁词，用于正式场合。
6.per capita也是拉丁词，意思是“每人”、“按人口（计算）”，仅用于正式场合。per capita既可以用作形容词，也可以用作副词。例如：
What is the average per capita wage in this country?
＄ 40 for eight men is ＄5 per capita.
7拉丁词per se，意思是“本身”、“本质上”，用于正式文体。例如：
Alcohol per se is harmless.
B“经由……”、“靠……”、“通过……”
例如：
The goods will be delivered per rail.
I sent the message per Mr White.
Note:
　　这里的per相当于by，但per用于商业行话或正式场合，by则常用于一般场合。
C“按照……”、“根据……”
例如：
Employers paid their quota per the number of employees working for him.
Would you please pay us as per enclosed account?
Note:
1只有在正式场合才用per表示“按照……”、“根据……”的意思，而且更常用as per代替per。
2短语as per usual（照常）等于as usual，但as per usual仅用于口语，属于戏谑语。例如：
I shall accompany him, as per usual.
　
plus
A“加（上）”
例如：
Eight plus five is thirteen.
75 plus 15 equals 90.
Note:
　　在数学上，plus表示“+”，minus表示“-”，它们都是从拉丁词引用来的。
B“另外”、“外加”
例如：
The cost is a pound plus 10 pence for postage.
His salary is ￡450 a year, plus commissions.
Note:
1当plus的意思是“另加”、“外加”时，它与less相对应，而不是与minus相对应。
2.plus fours被用作名词，意思是“宽大的运动裤”，“灯笼裤”。
3在口语中，plus有时还可以引申表示“得”、“穿”等意思。例如：
He's plus a dollar today.
He was plus a coat.
C“以及”
例如：
Compiling a dictionary costs much time plus patience.
This kind of work needs experience plus care.
　
　
regarding
　　regarding作为介词，意思是“关于”。例如：
Let me have your comments regarding our new products.
I have no information regarding his whereabouts.
Note:
1表示“关于”时，通常用about或on；而regarding通常用于正式场合，特别是用于商业函件。试比较：
Regarding delivery, this normally takes two or three weeks.
What comments have you about my son's behaviour?
2.as regards可以看成是复合介词，其意思相当于regarding，但在正式函件中as regards常用在句首，以引起一个新的议题。试比较：
As regards the time of delivery, we haven't decided yet.
I cannot agree with you as regards that.
除regarding, as regards之外，表示“关于”的复合介词还有：in / with regard to, in / with relation to, in / with reference to, in / with respect to, as respects, as relates to, in connection with, on the subject of, in the matter of等等。
　
regardless of
　　复合介词regardless of含有“不顾”、“不管”、“不注意”、“尽管”等让步的意思。例如：
I shall go regardless of the heat of the sun.
She is regardless of consequences.
Note:
1复合介词regardless of是由副词regardless和介词of组成的。Regardless有时也可以写成regardlessly。
Some people act regardlessly of what will happen.
表示“让步”的介词除了regardless of以外，还有in spite of, despite, notwithstanding等，它们所构成的介词短语在意义上相当于由although /though所引起的让步状语从句。这些介词当中，in spite of最普通，despite / despite of，notwithstanding多用于比较正式的场合，regardless 则只用于正式场合。
　
respecting
　　respecting含有“关于”或“鉴于”的意思。
A“关于”
例如：
We are completely at a loss respecting the answer to the problem.
Note:
　　respecting表示“关于”时，比about, on正规。在正式的场合，特别是在商业信函中respecting常用在句首，以引起一个新的议题。
Respecting your recent complaint, I beg to infrom you that the matter is under investigation.
B“鉴于”
例如：
Respecting the facts, it seems useless to continue.
Respecting the weather, the match had to be postponed.
Note:
一般很少用respecting表示“鉴于”、“由于”，而用in view of, considering等。
　
　
round
　　round含有“绕着”、“围绕”、“在……周围（附近）”、“向……四周”、“到……各处”、“绕过”、“在拐弯过去的地方”、“包裹”、“拥护”、“大约”等意思。round与around的意思基本相同，因此它们在口语中常常可以替用。
A“绕着”
例如：
The earth goes round the sun.
The wheel goes round an axle.
Note:
1在英国，表示“绕着某一轴心”的运动时，通常用round，在美国则喜欢用around。
2表示“绕完一周”时，在round之前加all ,或right。例如：
We have gone all round the circle.
Drake sailed right round the world and came back to England.
3表示“连续往返转动”时，用round and round的结构，即在两个介词之间加上一个连词and。与这种用法类似的还有up and down, in and out等，它们都表示某种“反复”的运动。例如：
They danced round and round the Xmas tree.
His eyes moved up and down the rows of people, looking for his son's face.
4短语round the clock含有“持续24小时，不停顿、不休息”的意思。例如：
The doctors worked round the clock to stem the epidemic.
5短语round the day (year)的意思是“一整天（年）”，这主要是美国英语的用法。例如：
She has been writing letters round the day.
The mountain is so high that the snow does not melt on it all round the year.
B“在……四周”、“在……周围”、“在……附近”
例如：
If it is very cold I sometimes wear a scarf round my neck.
It's beautiful place with about four square miles of land round it.
Note:
　　在“all round”或“right round”等结构中，all或 right起加强语气的作用。例如：
All round the hall are portraits of great men.
There's a veranda right round the building.
C“向……四周”
例如：
In summer I have a walk round my garden every evening.
Note:
　　round与运动的动词连用时，常常含有“走……一圈”、“绕一圈”的意思，运动的路线不一定是圆曲线，但多少都与圆周有关，“(all) over”则含有“遍及”、“到处”的意思。试比较：
He travelled round the world.
He travelled over the world.
D“绕过”、“向拐弯那边”
例如：
Let's go round the building, not through it.
There's a fruit-shop just round the corner.
Note:
　　短语“get round sb”含有“说服、笼络、争取某人”的意思，“get round sth”含有“克服、越过、逃避某物”的意思。
1. get round sb.
Alice knows how to get round her father.
2.get round sth.
He finally got round the difficulty of language.
E“包裹”
例如：
Each of the chocolate had a piece of tin foil round it.
F“对……的支持”
　　与表示“聚集”的动词连用时，字面意义是“聚集在……四周”，比喻为“对……的支持（拥护）”。例如：
The reformer soon gathered a band of followers round him.
G“围绕……展开争论”
　　与表示“争议”的动词连用时，含有“围绕某个中心议题（展开争论）”的意思。例如：
Most disputes have arisen round this subject.
H“大约”
例如：
I'll see you round seven o'clock.
It cost me somewhere round ￡ 10.
　
round about
　　round about可以看成是复合介词，含有“在……周围（附近）”、“大约”等意思，可以与round或者about 替用。
A“在……周围（附近）”
例如：
He lived in the countryside round about Ovan.
They danced cheerfully round about the Xmas tree.
B“大约”
例如：
He died round about two o'clock.
Round about a hundred people are expected to attend the conference.
　
　
save / saving　　
save源于法语sauf，用作介词时意思是“除……以外”，相当于except，saving有时被用来代替save，表示同样的意义。例如：
The whole class save Peter arrived on time.
All is lost save honour.
Note:
1.save之后可以接名词也可以接代词，接代词时，和as, than 一样，代词可以是主格，也可以是宾格。
2.save之后还可以接that引导的名词从句，意思是“除……以外”，“只是……”。例如：
It is a very satisfactory hat, save that it doesn't fit me.
3.save 之后除接名词或名词从句外，还常接介词短语或状语从句。例如：
At this hour the great tunnel was quiet, save when a train roared above.
4跟except for一样，save for也可以用来表示对某一个陈述的“修正”，含有“如果不把……考虑在内的话”的意思，而except和save一样含有“不包括……”的意思。试比较：
We all failed save him.
The bar was empty save for the landlord.
5.except或save表示“从……扣除……”的意思，因此被扣除的那个名词一定是复数或含有复数意味的单数名词。例如：
I go to school every day save Sunday.
6.but也表示“除……以外”，但只跟no, all, nobody, anywhere, everything等词连用，或跟where, who, what之类的疑问词连用，通常后接名词或代词。
7短语saving your presence （或reverence）是古英语的用法，意思是“说句失敬的话”、“请恕我冒昧”。这里的saving不能用save或其他的介词代替。例如：
There's nothing I hate so much as music&#0;&#0;saving your presence.
8有时save通过连词and 与except连用(save and except), 起加强语气的作用。例如：
This is the most pleasing poem in our language,--save and except one or two of Dryden's fables.
　
since
　　since的意思是“自从……以来”，“自从……之后”。例如：
Since ancient times such things have been common.
Until then we had not seen each other since our school days.
Note:
1.since表示从过去某一点时间开始，延续到现在（讲话时刻）的行为或状态，或者表示从过去某一点时间开始，延续到过去某一特定时间的行为或者状态，因此谓语动词一般都要用现在完成式或过去完成式。
2如果句子主语是it，谓语动词be后接表示一段时间的词语时，be经常可以不用完成式，只用一般现在式。试比较：
It's exactly three weeks today since Christmas Day.
I've been here ever since my leaving England.
3.since和from都表示时间的起始点，但用since时，时间的起点是在过去，并着重说明某种行为或状态延续到某一特定时刻的最终情况，因此谓语动词一般必须用完成式，用何种时态，必须视具体情形而定。
4.since偶尔也表示从过去某一时间延续到将来某一时刻的情况。
Next year it will be ten years since my coming here.
5与from或after 连用的动词有时也可以用完成式，这时候强调了到某一时刻为止的情况，因此from或after都可以用since来代替。试比较：
After being invited, I have been told the party was cancelled.
From that first day I have never looked back.
Since then he has never looked back.
6.after表示在某一点时间之后，这个时间可能是过去，也可以是现在或将来，情况同since相类似。试比较：
After being in the army he was an engineer.
Since being in the army, he has been in much better health.
　
through
　　through可以表示“穿过”（空间）、“经过”（时间）、“通过”（媒介手段）、“由于（原因、理由）”等意思。
A“穿过”、“通过”、“经过”
例如：
What did you see when you peeped through the key hole?
Walking through the park, I saw some lovely flowers.
We went through France on our way to Switzerland.
Note:
through有时还表示穿过某一个点，但横过一条线时应该用across或over。试比较：
Draw the line from A, through B to C.
The two lines pass across each other at right angles.
He walked over / across the road to greet me.
　　across也可以表示横过一个平面，但强调穿过一个立体空间时就应该用through。例如：
He walked across the fields and through the woods.
B“遍及”
例如：
For months they have hunted through the Highlands.
The police searched through the house, but found no clues.
Note:
　　through只限于表示遍及某个“区域”或“立体空间”，不能代替around或along表示普及“整个周围”或“整条路线”。throughout可以与through替用，但throughout语气更强。试比较：
I rang the bell, and I could heard it ringing through the house.
Many people stood around the injured man.
There are many tall trees along the road to the village.
The news spread quickly throughout the Highlands.
C“在穿过……的那边”
例如：
He lives in the house through the trees.
　　由空间的“穿过”引申为“经历”、“过关”、“校阅”、“做完”、“耗光”、“看穿”等意思。
1）“经历”
He has come through a lot of difficulties.
2） “过关”
Five per cent of the candidates got through the examination.
3） “校阅”
Go through these papers carefully.
4）“做完”
Are you through (with) your work?
Note:
　　be through with sth里的with有时可以省略，这个短语含有“完成”、“完结”、“不再”的意思。例如：
He is through with smoking.
5）“耗光”
He soon got through his fortune.
6） “看穿”
I see through your little game.
D“经由……”、“以……”、“依靠……”
例如：
Inflammable articles must not be sent through post.
We learn to speak through speaking.
He bought the property through a house agent.
Note:
　　表示媒介手段的介词有时必须根据习惯用法搭配。试比较：
I heard of the news through the radio.
They sent the message by radio.
E“由于”、“因为”
例如：
Through his kindness I got my present job.
It was through Fred's carelessness that the money was lost.
Note:
1.through表示原因时，常常表示消极的因素，例如常与carelessness, negligence, fault等词连用。如果through表示的原因与某种手段有关联 ，through就可以与by替用。
2表示“原因”的介词还有许多，如from, out of,for,at,of,because of, on account of等。
3.through有时也可以用来代替from，表示“根源”、“动机”。for常表示“感情”上的原因；at则表示对感官的“刺激”因素。例如：
I fell asleep through tiredness.
She was crying for joy.
I was alarmed at his behaviour.
F“从头到尾”
例如：
Owing to urgent business, some of the staff had to work through the weekend.
Note:
1表示“从头到尾”的through相当于throughout，但throughout语气更强。例如：
He worked throughout his holidays.
2在英国，说from Monday to Friday时，是包括Friday在内的，相当于from Monday to Friday inclusive 为了清楚表明这个意思，可以用（from） Monday through Friday。试比较：
I stayed there from June 15 to June 25.
I stayed there from June 15 through June 25.
　
throughout
　　throughout可以指“遍及”整个地区，或“贯穿”整个时期。
A“遍及”
例如：
Terror spread throughout the city.
A description of the wanted man was circulated throughout the country.
Note:
　　如果表示遍及某个抽象范围时，只能用over,这时候的over还含有“关于”，“在……方面”的意思。此外over或all over也同样可以表示“遍及”某个“区域”或“立体空间”。试比较：
The fire soon spread throughout the whole part of the town.
His interests now spread over several subjects.
B“贯穿”
例如：
It poured with rain throughout the night.
Throughout his life, the doctor always tried to help others.
Note:
表示时间时，over通常指“过了某个特定时节”，如over the weekend, over the holiday。through (out)经常指“贯穿某个时期”，因此over之后所接的时间往往比through之后所接的时间短。
　
till (until)
　　till含有“直到……为止”、“在……之前”、或“直到……（才）”的意思。until在意思上完全等于till，它们常可以互相通用。
A“直到……为止”
例如：
I waited for you yesterday till six o'clock, but you didn't come.
I have a lot of work to do, and usually after dinner or supper I go to my study and read or write until twelve or one o'clock.
Until then, she had always lived in London.
Note:
1当till是“直到……为止”的意思时，它表示某种“行为”或“状态”延续的时间终点，因此谓语动词必须是持续性的动词或表示“状态”的动词。而by表示“到达某一时刻的行为或状态”，没有延续的意思，因此上述例句中均不能用by 代替till (until)。
2为了同时表示“延续”的起点和终点，可用“from…till (until) …”的结构，也可用“from …to…”的结构。例如：
The exhibition will be open from May lst until / to June 30th.
I stayed in bed till ten o'clock every morning.
3在口语中，有时用up till (until)代替till, until。例如：
He was here up till last week.
4.till (until)之后有时接地名，表示“直至到达某地的时候”，含有“as far as”，“up to”的意思。例如：
Stay on the train until (we get to) Birmingham.
B“在……之前（不）”，“直到……（才）”例如：
I cannot see you till 3 o'clock..
Well, no more lessons now until after Christmas.
Note
1.till (until)表示“在……之前（不）”、“直到……（才）”时，主要见于含有否定意义的句子中，如上述例句。但偶尔在肯定句中也表示这个意思，例如：
Until his accident, he had always been strong and healthy.
2在否定句中，till (until)表示否定状态的结束时刻， by则表示到达某时刻的否定状态。试比较：
The ship will not arrive in Southampton until ten o'clock .
The ship will not arrive in Southampton by ten o'clock.
3在叙述过去某一件事情的否定句中，为了加强语气，可以用“It was not until … that…”的结构。例如：
It was not until 1856 that she returned home -- an invalid for life.
直到1856年他才回国，带回了终身之疾。
上述例句还可以改写成另一种强调结构：
Not until 1856 did she returned home -- an invalid for life.
　
to
　　可以表示“方向、方位”，“目的地、终点”，“对象”，“关联”，“接触、附着”，“程度、极限”，“结果、效果”，“比较、比例、比率”，“按照、一致、搭配”，“目的”等意思。
A“向……”、“往……”
例如：
While on his way to the cinema, he met an old friend.
I want a ticket to Brighton, please, second class.
Note:
1.to还表示两个事物间的“相对位置”。这种用法与表示方位的to相类似。例如：
A funny family named Wiggins lives next door to me.
That man is the station master. There's his office next to the ticket office.
2短语“in the north of”是“在……北部地区”，“to（或on）the north of”是“在靠……北部的地区”。试比较：
Yorkshire is in the North of England.
Canada lies to / on the north of the United States.
B表示“目的地、终点”,“到”、“达”、“至”
例如：
I am going to London tomorrow.
When you come to the traffic lights, turn left.
I have come to the end of my patience.
C表示“对象”
例如：
He shouted to me and warned me of the danger.
At five o'clock the entrances to St. Giles are closed to traffic.
The exhibition will be open to public from 10 a.m. till 6 p.m.
Mr Jones has sent this book to you.
She gave an orange to each of the children.
She was always kind to children.
He had always been generous to good causes.
Let's drink to the health of our respected foreign guests.
Here's to the bride and bridegroom!
They built a temple to Mars.
We visited the Monument to the people's Heroes.
Note:
1有些动词要求两个宾语（直接宾语和间接宾语），间接宾语通常指人，直接宾语通常指物。间接宾语一般放在直接宾语之前。但有时为了强调间接宾语，就把间接宾语置于直接宾语之后，并在间接宾语之前加上“to”。试比较：
I told him the whole story.
I told the whole story to him.
2如果两个宾语都是代词时，间接宾语必须置于直接宾语之后，而且在间接宾语之前可以不用to。但若要强调间接宾语，就必须加to。试比较：
I gave it him.
I gave it to him.
3.to表示某种东西的实际接受者，for则表示意想中某种东西的接受者。例如：
I really brought these shoes for Mary, but I think I'll give them to James.
D表示“关联”
　　“就……来说”．例如：
I had never before lived in a big city, so London was a new world to me.
To a child, any unfamiliar noise may be frightening.
Note:
1在某些名词之后也用to表示“关联”，如an honour to, an enemy to, a victim to等等。例如：
Care is an enemy to life.
忧患乃人生之敌。
Our doctor's been a good friend to us.
我们的医生一向是我们的好朋友。
2在某些形容词之后也用to表示“关联”，如pleasant to the senses, attractive to the eye等等。例如：
Her voice is pleasant to the ear.
The scenery is attractive to the eye.
E“关于”
　　用to表示“关于”时主要见于复合介词as to, with regard to, in reference to等。例如：
I don't know anything as to the others.
关于其他人的事我一无所知。
As to the journey, we must decide about that later.
至于这次旅行，我们过些时候必须做出决定。
Note:
1在动词之后用as to表示“关于”的这种用法不算正规，但在英国却很常见，如：
The sun and the wind were quarrelling as to which was the stronger.
We want to knowfrom you as to whether you have ever experienced any cruelty.
2.as to还可以接在名词或形容词之后，表示“关于”的意思。例如：
He said nothing as to whether he would come back.
He is worried as to where to go.
F所属关系
例如：
I don't know the answer to the puzzle.
He is the private secretary to the prime minister.
G亲属关系
例如：
Mrs Priestly is daughter-in-law to William Priestly.
Arthur was nephew to the King.
Note:
of也表示“所属”关系，但to主要侧重于相互间的关系。试比较：
What relation is he to the King? He is nephew to the King.
Whose nephew is he? He is a nephew of the King.
H表示“接触”、“附着”
例如：
We asked him to write a preface to the book.
I attached the document to the letter.
Note:
1.to有时表示由于运动的结果而达到接触，例如：
In despair I crossed the street on to an island, where I found a policeman.
He raised the cup to his lips.
2.Shoulder to shoulder, cheek to cheek, back to back之类的短语通常表示“接触”，有时表示“接近”。例如：
Arrange the tables end to end.
They stand shoulder to shoulder.
3.to有时与with连用，表示“附有”某种性质、特征等。如：music with a swing to it, food with a taste to it.
4“to it”有时还表示“附有”某种抽象的意义，而不是实际的东西。例如：
They may say that the regulation has been made to prevent overcrowding, but there is more to it.
I表示“程度”、“极限”
例如：
He mimics people to a hair.
Drink to excess has a bad effect on health.
J表示“结果”、“效果”
例如：
The train came to a stop in another little station.
She sang her baby to sleep.
Note:
to表示“增”、“减”的结果, 这个用法与表示“程度”的to类似。例如：
I bought this shirt because it was reduced from ￡ 3 to ￡ 2.
It all adds up to this, she is entirely untrustworthy.
K表示“比较”、“比例”、“比率”
例如：
The ratios of 1 to 5 and 20 to 100 are the same.
I prefer the country to the town.
L表示“按照”、“与……一致”、“伴随”
例如：
"Is that tea quite to your taste or do you need more sugar?" "No more sugar, thank you; it is exactly to my liking."
The map is drawn to a scale of one inch to the mile.
They were dancing to the music of a barrel organ.
M表示“目的”
例如：
We sat down to dinner.
I am going to a dance tonight.
　
together with
　　together with是由副词together和介词with构成的复合介词，意思是“和……一起”、“连同”、“加之”。例如：
Plant disease, together with a prolonged drought, has ruined the crops.
He is selling the house, together with the furniture.
　
towards
　　towards可以表示“朝、向”，“对于、关于”，“接近，将近”，“为了、作为”等意思。
A“朝”、“向”
例如：
He crossed the road towards the House of Parliament.
We were on the road again towards home.
Note:
1.towards单纯表示“方向”，不带有主观意志，to则可以表示主观意志。试比较：
Their backs were towards me.
He turned his back to me.
2.towards和to都可以表示“朝”、“向”。强调“朝着某个方向”时，常用towards。 towards不含有“到”、“达”、“接触”等意思，to则可以表示“到达、接触”。
B“对于”、“关于”
例如：
I have always felt kindly towards him.
I felt a lot of gratitude towards my host.
C“接近”、“将近”
例如：
As we got towards Exter the traffic became heavier.
She must be getting towards fifteen.
D“为了”、“作为”、“有助于”
例如：
You should save as much money as possible towards your holiday.
I've saved a bit of money and that would help towards buying the furniture.
Note:
　　短语go far towards的意思是“大有助于……”。例如：
Your success in the examination will go far towards helping you to get a good and well-paid position.
　
under
　　under的本义是表示（位置）“在……下面”，并引申表示“从属关系”，“地位、能力的低下”，“数量的不足”，“类属”，“法规、条例的依据”，“所处的状态”，“在进行的过程中”等。
A表示位置“在……正下方”、“在……之下”、“在……下面”例如：
There were beds of spring flowers, red and yellow and blue, in the beautiful, smooth grass under the trees.
St. Frideswide is a Saxon saint who died in A.D. 750 and is buried under the floor of the Cathedral.
Note:
1.beneath也是指“在……正下方”的位置，因此under与beneath常可以互相替用。
2.below是泛指“在……下方”，under则强调上方的事物。试比较：
He hid under the table (=underneath the table).
He hid below the table (=lower than the top of the table.
3如果具体指明“在多少距离之下”时，一般用below。
The submarine travelled 100 feet below the surface of the water.
4.under常见于某些比喻的用法里，below一般不能这样用，如：
I sometimes think you don't see the simplest thing that is taking place under your very nose.
Under the rough outward appearance there was kindness.
B表示从属关系，“在……统治下”、“在……领导下”
例如：
England under George Ⅱ was prosperous.
The university staff will be happy to work under his leadership.
C表示数量少
例如：
His income is under ￡ 1,000 a year.
We went from our house to Cambridge in under an hour.
D表示类属
例如：
Rabbits come under the heading of animals.
You will find these two works listed under "Biography".
E表示依据……
例如：
I raise the objection under Article 5 of the Constitution.
The soldiers were searching under orders.
F表示身份、行为的假托
例如：
Samuel Clemens wrote under the name of Mark Twain.
The thief gained entry under the disguise of an inspector from the Electricity Board.
G表示所处的状态
例如：
His mother would not permit him to go under any condition.
Please keep your dog under control.
Note:
1.under之后接某一农作物的名词时，常指“某一块土地种植该种作物”。例如：
The farm has 200 acres under wheat.
Many parts of Asia are kept under rice.
2表示状态的under常含有subjected to的意思，如under the pressure of，under a spell这些都表示“遭受”外界的某种力量，但do something under protest却不是指“遭到别人的抗议”，而是指“行为者本人在提出抗议的情况下做某事”。试比较：
We listened to the young as though we were under a spell.
He signed the document under protest.
H表示在进行的过程中
I can't use my office at present; it is under repair.
The question is still under discussion.
I注意下列成语的意思：
1）under age（未成年）
People who are either under age or over age may not join.
2）under the counter （私下地）
During the war some shopkeepers made a lot money by selling scare goods under the counter at high prices.
3） under cover（在……掩护下）
The prisoner escaped under cover of darkness.
4）under separate cover（另函）
This is a receipt. The goods will be sent later under separate cover.
5）under fire（受到攻击）
The government's plans came under fire from the opposition.
6）under foot（在地面）
We found it very slippery under foot.
7）under one's hat（秘密的）
We don't want this information to be made public. So keep it under your hat.
8）under lock and key（妥善地锁藏着）
All the valuables were kept under lock and key.
9）under the sun（天下，世界上）
There's nothing new under the sun.
He gave every excuse under the sun.
10）under the weather（有点不舒服、不太愉快）
I am not really ill; I am just feeling a bit under the weather.
　
underneath
　　underneath也用来表示“在……下面”的空间位置，常可以与beneath, under, below等互相替用。例如：
A cat was sleeping underneath the table.
Note:
1.underneath主要表示相对的空间位置，有时也由表示“空间位置”引申为“在……形式下”、“在……支配下”等意思。例如：
Underneath his apparent politeness there was deceit and cunning.
They are living underneath a tyranny.
2当underneath表示空间位置时，常可以与underbeneath,below等替用。
beneath, below或under常可以引申表示其它非物质、非空间的意思，形成一些固定的词组，例如：beneath notice, beneath contempt, below the belt, under Queen Elezabeth I, under such conditions,这些词组中的beneath, under或below均不能与underneath替用。
　
unlike
　　介词unlike是介词like加前缀un-后形成的，意思是“不象”、“和……不同”。例如：
The two brothers are completely unlike each other.
He is quite unlike any of my other friends.
　
unto
　　unto的意思是“对”、“到”，属于古旧用语，相当于现在的to。在现代英语里，unto仅用于诗歌及少数固定的成语。例如：
Do unto others as you would have others do unto you.
Do unto them as they do unto us.
　
up
　　up常与“运动”的动词连用，表示“由下向 上”的运动。up与down相对应。例如：
The brave firemen quickly climbed up the ladder and rescued the old man.
As you get higher up the school you get more home-work.
Note:
　　up也可以表示静止的空间位置，即“在（高处）”、“在……上面”。例如：
His office is up the stairs.
　　当up与road, street, path, passage等连用时，含有along的意思，而并不一定是向上运动。例如：
We walked up the path to the front door.
He whistled as he went up the passage.
Note:
1离开说话者的运动通常用“go up…”，向着说话者的运动则用“come up…”。试比较：
Who is this coming up the road?
Who is that going up the road?
2“come down the road”和“go down the road”都可以表示离开说话者的运动，也都可以表示向着说话者的运动。
3.up跟along一样，表示静止的空间位置时，意思是“在……的那一端”。例如：
He lives just up the road.
C由下游向上游的运动也用up，因此有“由……上游”、“逆着……的方向”的意思，表示静止的空间位置时，就是“在……上游”。例如：
The ship sailed up the river.
The ship is up the river.
They ran up (the ) wind.
D由沿海到内地，由南方到北方，由次要地点到重要地点等均用up，反之用down。例如：
They marched up country with Indian guides.
We travelled up north.
　
　
upon
upon是副词up与介词on组合成的介词，意思等于on，但upon主要用于正式文体。在多数情况下upon可用来代替on，但upon习惯上不能表示“在……身上”、“由……支付”、“在……状态（过程）中”等意思，也不能表示某一特定的“时、日”，如不能说upon Friday, upon May 9th,应该说on Friday, on May 9th，但可以说upon his arrival, upon the morning of May 19th.
　
　
up to
　　up to是由副词up与介词to构成的复合介词，它的基本意思是“到”、“直到”，可以表示“时间的限度”、“程度”、“胜任、适应”等，在口语中还用于表示“忙于、搞”，“责任、义务”等。
A“直到”
表示时间的限度，例如：
Up to that time, all had gone well.
Up to now, I've never met her.
Note:
1.up to表示时间时，常指“到过去某个时刻为止”的状态，因此常用过去完成式（有时也用过去式）。up to now表示“到现在为止”的行为或状态，表示“到将来某时间”只能用till (until)， 不能用up to。试比较：
Up to that time I had been in the country.
I shall stay here till the end of this month.
2下列句中up to之后所指的时间不是过去、现在或将来，而是指“一般情况下的时间终点”，因此动词用一般现在式。例如：
On Friday the shops keep open up to 6 p.m..
3当up to之后接的不是某一点时间，而是一段时间时，up to之后的这段时间通常是不包括在内的。例如在“He worked up to Saturday”这个句子中，He只工作到星期五，星期六那天就没工作了。
B“到”、“达到”
表示程度，例如：
This article does not come up to standard.
He was up to any villainy.
Note:
1表示程度的up to有时引申为“包括”的意思。
Everybody works, from the boy who sweeps the floor up to the president.
2.up to有时还引申为“知道”的意思。
We were up to their tricks.
Sam was not up to many things about a horse.
C“胜任”、“适宜”
例如：
I don't feel up to the job.
Do you feel up to going out, or does your head hurt?
Note:
　　up to表示“胜任”、“适宜”时，通常用于否定句或疑问句，表示“不胜任”、“不适宜”。
D“忙于”、“搞”
例如：
Those boys are always getting up to mischief.
What are you up to with that knife?
Note:
　　be / get up to常含有“搞……名堂”这样指责的意味。
E表示责任、义务，“该由……”、“轮到……”
例如：
It's up to you (=It is your duty) to do your best.
It's up to you whether you decide to take the job.
Note:
在口语中常用“be up to someone”这样的词组表示责任或义务。
　
　
via
　　via是拉丁介词，主要表示“取道、经由”，“通过、利用”等意思。
A“取道”、“经由”
例如：
They flew from Boston to San Fransisco via Chicago.
He is on the way to London via Paris.
B“通过”、“利用”
例如：
I've read this French play via an English translation.
They arrived at a solution via scientific investigation.
Note:
　　在美国口语中，有时用via来表示搭乘交通工具，例如：
You folks going via car or railroad?
On April 26, 1860, 264 Chinese arrived in Victoria via a Norweigian vessel.
　
　
with
　　with主要可以表示“与……一起（一致，同时）”、“具有，带有”、“用（工具）”、“以（方式）”以及“原因”、“材料”、“对象”、“关联”等意思。
A“与……一起”、“跟……一起”
例如：
He discussed with you the work you have done.
The man who shared the room with him was a self-confident fellow called Adams.
Note:
1.have a talk to与have a talk with都很常用，但have a talk to是“从单方面看问题”，因此常用于“劝告”、“责备”等场合；have a talk with常含有“双方共同讨论”的意思，不过这两个词组常被互用，没有什么差别。
2在“He sends you this with his compliments”这样的句子中，with的基本意思也是“与……一起”，并由此引出某些套语。例如：
With the author's compliments.
With love and good wishes from John.
B“在……一边”（指赞成、支持等）
例如：
Are you with me or against me?
During the Second World War, the American fought with the English against the Germans.
C“跟……（对手）”、“对着……（敌手）”
例如：
In 1066 the English fought a great battle with the Normans.
It's no good trying to compete with those who have so much more experience than you.
D“与…… 一致”、“与……同方向”
例如：
Do you think this red tie goes with my blue suit?
There is no help for it, they must swim with the tide.
E“与……同时”、“随着……”
例如：
The old man gets up every morning with the sun.
Times change, and we must change with them.
F“具有……”、“带有……”
例如：
It was a big room with six tall windows.
I saw the man with the scar on his face.
Note:
　　当of与with表示“having”时，of后接抽象名词， with后接具体名词，如a man of courage, a man with money, a coat of many colours, a coat with two pockets, a girl with blue eyes。
G“尽管”
　　with作此用时，后接名词常冠以all或最高级形容词，例如：
With all his faults he was a kind-hearted fellow.
With all his abilities, he failed completely.
He failed with the best of intentions to win the sympathy of his pupils.
H说明“附带情况”
例如：
He died with his life's work still unfinished.
I know there isn't a better-hearted person than you anywhere&#0;&#0;and I say that with my hand on my heart.
I“以……的方式”
例如：
She spoke Italian, French and German with ease.
He opened the door and, with a smile and some words of welcome, shook hands with me.
The sick man climbed the steps with great difficulty.
Note:
1许多形容词都有相对应的抽象名词和方式副词，因此表示行为的方式时常有下列三种方法（前两种为比较正式的用法）；
She greeted us in a very courteous way.
She greeted us with great courtesy.
She greeted us very courteously.
2在“You may borrow the book with pleasure”一句中，with是表示“伴随”，而不是表示“方式”。这里是“出借者乐意出借”，而不是“借者乐意借”。这句话相当于：I shall be pleased to lend you the book对照下句：It is with great pleasure that I give you the prize.
J“由……处理（负责）”
例如：
The watchmaker said my watch wanted cleaning, so I left it with him.
It rests with you to decide.
K“由于”、“因为”
例如：
The old man was bent with age and dying with hunger.
She was jumping about with excitement and joy.
Note:
　　短语“what with…and what with…”的意思是“一方面由……一方面由于……”，这个短语也可以写成“what with…and …”，意思相同。它们特别用于表示某些不利的因素。例如：
What with war and what with famine, the nation was almost exterminated.
L“以……工具”
例如：
He sharpened his pencil with a small knife.
What will you buy with the money?
Note:
　　with作此用时，后接“工具”； by后接“行为者”，二者不可混用。试比较：
He was murdered by the angry mob (=The angry mob murdered him).
He was murdered with this old knife (=Someone used this old knife to murder him).
M“有……”、“由……”
指“材料”、“内容”
例如：
The floors were broken and the building was swarming with rats and mice.
There were three or four bookcases filled with books.
N“关于……”、“就……而言”、“对……”
表示关联，例如：
Did you have much trouble with your French when you were there?
There is nothing serious the matter with you, but you are rather run down.
Note:
1在口语中，with常与某些副词连用，构成祈使句。实际上这种结构可以理解为省略了某个动词，而with之后所接的就是这个动词所表示的行为的对象。例如：
Down with tyrants! (=Let tyrants be punished!)
Off with his head (=Cut off his head)!
Now then,out with it (=Tell us what we want to know)!
Off to bed with you!
2在短语to do with中，with表示“关联”，在这个短语之前常用anything, nothing, something等词，有时也有不用的。例如：
His job has something to do with schools.
That's nothing to do with me.
The letter has to do with your application for entry to the university.
3短语could / can do with含有“将就”、“能对付”、“需要”、“希望得到”等意思。例如：
I could do with a good holiday.
I could do with a cup of tea.
Can you do with cold meat for lunch?
　　否定式can't do with则暗示所指事物有“inconvenient”,“not desired”等意思。例如：
I can't do with visitors when the house is being redecorated.
I can't do with loud music.
　
within
　　within的意思是“在……之内”，可以表示“空间位置”，也可以表示“抽象范围”。
A“在……里面”、“在……内部”
例如：
By x-rays we can see what is within the body.
No sound came from within the big house.
Illness kept him within doors.
B“在……范围内”、“不超过……”
A）表示抽象范围，例如：
Shout if you want me; I shall be within hearing.
It is not within my power to do what you ask.
B）表示时间范围，例如：
You must pay me back within one month.
Within a very short time of being here I was told that the Cathedral in New York is the largest "Gothic" Cathedral in the world.
Note:
　　within表示“时间范围”时，意思是“不超过”，常暗示时间的“短暂”；in 则表示“经过若干时间”，有时也表示“在若干时间以内”。试比较：
I'll be there within an hour (=in less than an hour).
I'll be there in an hour.
C）表示距离范围，例如：
We are within a few miles of home when the accident occurred.
He lives within a stone's throw of us.
Note:
　　短语within an ace of的意思是“离……只差一点儿”，来源于赌博用语，本意是“差一点就赢了”。例如：
We are within an ace of achieving our purpose.
　
　
without
　　without 主要用于表示“不”、“无”、“没有”、“缺”等否定的意思，有时还有“在……外面”、“在……范围之外”等意思。
A“没有与……在一起”、“没有”
例如：
Mr Joyloy is going on holiday without his wife.
I don't like a house without a bathroom.
Note:
1. without之后有时接动名词或动名词短语，明确表示“没有做某事”。例如：
You can't have omelettes without breaking eggs.
2.without之后的及物动名词经常可以省略。只留下这个动名词的宾语。例如：
He has gone without (taking) his umbrella.
3.not without是一种“克制性的陈述”，这里带双重否定，其意思是肯定的，但语气比较委婉。例如：
He appointed General Grant to take command not without considerable opposition from the rest of the cabinet.
I rejected his proposal, and not without reason (=I had a very good reason).
4“never (no, not)…without”等句式也是双重否定，但这种结构却常起着加强语气的作用，如：
The two can never meet without quarreling.
He cannot speak English without making mistakes.
No one may enter or leave without permission.
5.do without, go without等短语的意思是“没有……照样……”
例如：
I haven't enough money to buy a car, so I'll just have to do without one.
There isn't time for breakfast; we'll have to go without it.
6短语do without之后所接的东西，有时从上下文可以看出，有时是泛指，因此被省略了。这时without成了副词。例如：
If we haven't any sugar, we shall have to do without.
They have got used to doing without.
7.without所组成的介词短语有时含有条件的意思，相当于一个条件从句。例如：
I can't do it without your help.
B注意下列短语的意思：
1) go without saying （不消说）
It goes without saying that he will come.
2) without fail（务必，必定）
I promise to be here at nine o'clock without fail.
3) without number（多得数不清）
I have told you times without number and you still forget.
4) without doubt（无疑，肯定）
Without doubt, he is the fastest runner today.
5) without exception（毫无例外地）
Everybody without exception volunteered to donate a pint of blood to the hospital.
C“在……外面”、“在……外部”、“在……范围以外”、“超过……”
例如：
He stood without the gate.
The forts were without the range of the guns.
